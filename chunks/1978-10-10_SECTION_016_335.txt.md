# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-335

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-335
- **Themes:** The Acts Lens, Platonist Alchemy, Natura Naturans.

## Key Claims
- **The Acts Lens as Decoding Grid:** The "Acts grid" is the mechanism by which the "vision in God" is achieved, revealing the eternal geometric forms underlying the flux.
- **A Priori Knowledge:** Knowledge of the eide is innate (like geometry) because God used them as the blueprints for creation. They are "discovered" rather than learned.
- **Abolishing Time/Space/Causation:** Because the eide are eternal (no time), ubiquitous (no space), and self-causing (no causation), perceiving them abolishes the lower categories.
- **The Alchemical Wedding:** The meta-abstraction is a "blitz" in the mind, a "Platonist alchemy" where conceptualization replaces passive perception.
- **Creation vs. Chaos:** Perception of absolute order is perception of creation (order imposed on chaos). Space-time are "fallen" categories that allow reality to slip back into flux.
- **Tears as the First Signal:** *Flow My Tears* reflects the "Acts grid" and the initial "hunch" as to the truth.

## Entities
- **St. Luke (Author of Acts):** Linked to the "Acts lens/grid" and the context of St. Paul.
- **Fibonacci Constant:** An example of a ubiquitous a priori category.
- **The 345 Doorway:** Symbolic entrance into the realm of the eide.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Natura Naturans:** The active creative force (the Will of God) as opposed to the created things (*natura naturata*).
- **Phylogon:** A temporal-spatial accretion added to the eternal form.
- **Quantum Leap:** The sudden shift from organizing by space-time to organizing by the Forms.

## Theological Motifs
- **Heaven vs. Eden:** Heaven is the realm of a priori eide; Eden is the lower realm of a posteriori creation.
- **Cosmological Mysticism:** The return to the "moment before cosmogenesis" where all is in the mind of God.
- **The Secret Presence:** The Kingdom is an "unscrambled" reality already present, revealed to those with the decoding grid.
