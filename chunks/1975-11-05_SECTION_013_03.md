# Correspondence / Critique of ICC
## concise_summary
Dick critiques the International Community of Christ (ICC) as a "Gnostic group" based on false premises. He identifies his 3-74 experience as a restoration of "lost faculties" (Original Sin reversal) and reflects on how the "Holy Spirit" extricated him from a double political trap involving the USSR and the US secret police.
## key_claims
- Failure of ICC: Claims the ICC's "Decoded New Testament" is based on the false premise that the gospels aren't authentic; he rejects their "closed, secret society" model.
- Original Sin as Sensory Deprivation: Humans have "lost faculties" (perception/mentation); 3-74 was a temporary restoration of these faculties.
- Political Extrication: Dick was in "dire danger" from the Soviets (who saw him as a "progressive" tool) and the US (who saw him as a subversive). The Holy Spirit saved him by giving him a "new personality."
- The "Bardo Thodol" experience: The 1974 event involved the "extinguishing" of his ego.
- The 2,000 Year Daimon: He identifies his visitor as a "Daimon" (like Socrates')—specifically a wise, educated Greek woman/saint from 2,000 years ago.
- St. Sophia as Creator: Admits his experience is "exactly Gnostic" because it enshrines Sophia as the "creator goddess."
