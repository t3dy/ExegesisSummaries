# Notes / Theoretical Synthesis
## concise_summary
Dick further develops his Supralunar/Sublunar cosmology, identifying the "fake" phenomenological world as the sublunar realm and the "real" world as the supralunar. He explores the gender-fluidity of the Logos (Sophia/Christ/Holy Spirit) and argues that his novel *Tears* carried a "synchronized" message for the historical removal of the "tyrant."
## key_claims
- Ascension vs. Descent: While humans seek to "ascend" to the supralunar, 3-74 was a "descent" or "penetration" by God into history.
- The Logos as Syzygy: The Savior has been Wisdom (female), Christ (male), and now the Holy Spirit (neutral).
- *Tears* as Message/Program: The book’s publication coincided with the theophany to provide an "apocalyptic analysis" to an undercover audience.
- Removal of the Tyrant: The primary goal of the 3-74 intervention was the political destruction of Nixon.
- Pre-incarnation Logos: "St. Sophia will be born again" implies a second incarnation.
- Lapidary Construction: Dick views his life and work as a "lapidary construction" prepared by the spirit to ensure the "determinism" of the 1974 historical shift.
