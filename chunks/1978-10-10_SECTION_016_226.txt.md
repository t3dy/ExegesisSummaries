# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-226

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-226
- **Themes:** The Ethics of Jesus vs. Torah Law, Kyrios Authority, Internalized Ethics.

## Key Claims
- **Jesus vs. Mosaic Law:** The ethics of Jesus represent a quantum leap over the Decalogue. Whereas Torah is external prohibition ("Thou shalt not"), Jesus' system is internalized ethics ("But I say to you...").
- **Authority of Kyrios:** Jesus could only amend or nullify the Torah (which God gave) if he himself were God (Kyrios). His claim to divine kingship was a logical necessity for his code to be accepted.
- **Internalization:** O.T. ethics are "law" (external); N.T. ethics involve the "inner person" (thoughts, feelings, desires). The individual becomes a "law unto himself."
- **Entropy vs. Negentropy:** The Torah ossified and spiraled downward entropically. Jesus' system introduced new energy (negentropy) into the realm of moral precepts.
- **Theophany Contrast:** God revealed the Torah with terror (Mt. Sinai); Jesus arrived on a donkey. His "authority" was based on the quality of the system and the (eventual) proof of resurrection.

## Entities
- **Jesus / Kyrios:** The authority that supercedes the Torah.
- **Paul:** Recognizes that the Law condemns but cannot save.
- **The Decalogue / Torah:** The "frozen" or "letter" of the law.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Quantum Leap:** The transition from external legalism to internalized morality.
- **Kyrios:** The title of "Lord" implying divine status.
- **Ossification:** The state of the Law before the "New Revelation."

## Theological Motifs
- **The Letter vs. The Spirit:** Paul's distinction between the "dead" law and the "living" grace.
- **Divine Authorship:** The intrinsic beauty of Jesus' system as proof of its divine origin.
- **Resurrection as Proof:** In antiquity, resurrection validated the man-god; today, the system validates itself.
