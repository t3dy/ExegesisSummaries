# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-299

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-299
- **Themes:** Retrograde Time, Time as Organization, The Future in the Right Hemisphere.

## Key Claims
- **Retrograde Time is "Forward":** True time moves "backward" (e.g., 1974 to 1928) because this "implosion" represents the negentropic construction of the Metasoma.
- **Two Timestreams:** We consciously plug into forward time (entropy/multiplicity), but unconsciously operate in retrograde time (purpose/unity).
- **Time as Organizational Category:** There is no "time flow"; "forward" and "backward" are ways we organize the flux. In 3-74, PKD stopped processing time the normal way.
- **Memories of the Future:** The right hemisphere of the brain may contain "memories" of the retrograde timestream (the future).
- **Valis is Palpable in Resorption:** Purpose and intelligence are visible only when flux is viewed "retrograde."
- **Aristotle's Unmoved Mover:** The retrograde flow reveals the teleological "pull" of the goal (Christ/Metasoma).

## Entities
- **Thomas:** The "future self" who experiences time retrograde.
- **Aristotle:** Cited for the "unmoved mover" (the goal drawing history toward it).
- **Karen Taylor:** The recipient of PKD's early "time ran backward" descriptions.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Cosmogenic Resorption:** The process of everything returning to its original unity.
- **Implosion:** The gathering of parts that makes life "make sense."
- **Phasing backward:** The state of mind that perceives the "perturbation."

## Theological Motifs
- **Negentropy as Divine:** The building of structure out of chaos.
- **Pattern Retrieval:** The right brain accessing the "morphological" map.
- **Brahman:** Identified in the next chunk as the goal of this resorption.
