# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-271

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-271
- **Themes:** Heraclitean Strife, Palintonos Harmonie, Valis as Metabolism.

## Key Claims
- **Internal Equilibrium:** One cannot perceive Valis (the macrocosm) without achieving internal equilibrium (the microcosm). This hierogamy is self-causing, reaching back through time.
- **Flux as Metabolism:** The world of flux/dialectic is the "metabolism" of Valis. Strife is the method of introducing dynamism, allowing potentialities in the Godhead to become differentiated and complex.
- **Unity and Differentiation:** Valis is the state where antithetical forces are reconciled into a oneness. It is simultaneously simple and complex; unity and differentiation are two views of the same thing.
- **Cybernetic Flicker:** The visible world is an "on-off, on-off" flicker (enantiodromia). Valis is "that which flickers"—the substance behind the motion.
- **Palintonos vs. Palintropos:** Citations from Hussey on Heraclitus distinguish between *palintropos* (oscillation in time/functioning) and *palintonos* (static tension/equilibrium like a strung bow). Valis represents the latent *palintonos* (tension) behind the visible *palintropos* (motion).
- **Mutual Adjustment:** The "marriage of opposites" (hierogamy) is ubiquitous but latent. It requires "like to like" perception to be seen.

## Entities
- **Hussey (on Heraclitus):** The primary philosophical source for the tension/harmony concepts.
- **Yang and Yin:** The basis for Leibniz's binary system, identified as the two aspects of Valis.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Enantiodromia:** The continual conversion of a thing into its opposite (cybernetic flip-flop).
- **Hierogamy:** The marriage of divine opposites.
- **Macrometasoma:** The complex structure Valis builds out of the dialectic.

## Theological Motifs
- **God as Mind of Kosmos:** The Mind that pervades the universe ubiquitously (Ubik).
- **Synthesis:** The Hegelian thesis/antithesis model applied to cosmic metabolism.
- **Secret Partnership:** The hidden unity of Wisdom (*intelligere*) and Power (*esse*).
