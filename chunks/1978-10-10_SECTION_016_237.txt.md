# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-237

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-237
- **Themes:** The Cold Pak Paradigm, Retracking the Life-Loop, Thomas as Former Living Self.

## Key Claims
- **Retracking for Freedom:** We relive our lives in a loop. When we add "new good-karma" through feedback from a "postmortem/retrograde" track, we inject freedom into a deterministic system.
- **Living vs. Dead Tracks:** PDK identifies Track A (forward) as "determinism/fake" and Track B (retrograde) as the source of "feedback/freedom."
- **Thomas is Alive:** PKD reverses his 1980 theory: **"Thomas is alive/awake; I am dead/asleep."** Thomas is the *former living self* whose real world intruded into PKD's *postmortem cold-pak simulation*.
- **The Reprise:** 3-74 was the second time around for the Xerox missive. PKD's "memory" (anamnesis) was of his "real and former actual life."
- **Ubik as Literal:** The world of *Ubik* (retrograde time, dead characters, information bits) is not a metaphor; it's the literal experience of 3-74.

## Entities
- **Thomas:** The "original prior self" (Acts persona).
- **Runciter (*Ubik*):** The one making "uncanny intrusions" from the living world to the dead.
- **Cold Pak:** The stasis/afterlife state.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Heimarmene:** The "Twin-Tape Synchronized Mapping."
- **Feedback Override:** Using retrograde knowledge to edit the "past."
- **Joint / Jory:** Reference to the struggle for reality in *Ubik*.

## Theological Motifs
- **Bardo Thodol:** The soul's movement through its former life.
- **Second Birth:** Awakening to the "True" (prior) life.
- **Heaven and Earth:** The two directions of time tracking.
