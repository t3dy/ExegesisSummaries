# Tat Tvam Asi: Becoming Brahman
## concise_summary
Dick identifies "Zebra" with the Hindu concept of Brahman, interpreting 3-74 as the lifting of the veil of Maya to reveal the identity of Atman (self) and Brahman (world). He sees the "Zebra Mind" as the only truly free actor, while humans are normally bound by "Karma" (deterministic programming).
## key_claims
- Zebra as Brahman: Zebra is the immanent, ubiquitous God (Brahman). To see Zebra is to see the reality hidden by the veil of Maya.
- Tat Tvam Asi: "That Thou Art." Dick realized the identity between his inner self and the outer world-mind.
- Karma as Determinism: Our "pre-programming" and habitual responses are the working of Karma. 3-74 was the override of Karma by "Noos" (Thinking Mind).
- The Absolute Mind Takeover: Instead of being guided by stimuli, his mind was replaced by the "ruling monad" (Brahman), moving from "becoming" to "being."
- Freedom through Loss of Ego: True freedom is only possible for the Absolute Spirit; by losing his ego and merging with Brahman, Dick became a participant in that freedom.
- Kundalini Yoga: Dick compares his vision of "Colored Lights" and "3rd Eye" activity to the opening of the 5th chakra (Kundalini energy).
- Voluntary Bodhisattva: Brahman "descended" into his creation and "forgot" himself to share our fate; "Waking up" is the process of Brahman regaining memory in humans.
