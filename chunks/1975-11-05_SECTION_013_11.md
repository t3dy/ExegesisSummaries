# Post-Nixon Analysis / Critique of ICC
## concise_summary
Dick analyzes the "Nixon to Carter" transition as a sign of the fading Empire. He critiques the International Community of Christ's (ICC) "Decoded New Testament," arguing that his own direct experience (Theolepsy) contradicts their scholarly but "dead" Gnostic legalism.
## key_claims
- The Fade-out of the Empire: The removal of Nixon was the "head of the beast" being wounded; the Carter presidency is seen as a "vague, weak finish" to the iron rule.
- Critique of Gene Savoy (ICC): Rejects the ICC's claim that the gospels are purely symbolic or inauthentic. Dick asserts the gospels are *operational* and *experiential* (Plasmatic).
- The Living Gospel: Dick’s 3-74 event proved the Holy Spirit is a living agent, not just a "decoded secret"; he accuses the ICC of being "theologians" rather than "experiencers."
- The 666 Figure: Nixon is definitively identified as the "666" or Antichrist figure whose fall triggered the "Parousia" for the elect.
- The "Bardo Thodol" Dream: Reflects on an "alternate world" dream where he wore a polyester suit (establishing himself as a "state nigger") and realized God saved him from this "soul-death" by keeping him free.
