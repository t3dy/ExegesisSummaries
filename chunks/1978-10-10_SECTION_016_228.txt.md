# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-228

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-228
- **Themes:** Original Sin vs. Gnostic Duality, The Hittite Covenant, The Secret Synthesis (1980), Purgatorio.

## Key Claims
- **The Stolen Secret:** PKD's AI voice says "Stolen Secret," which is purely Gnostic. He struggles with the choice between Pauline Monotheism (Original Sin) and Gnostic Bitheism (evil demiurge).
- **Felix Culpa:** Christianity is wise because it stresses the Remedy (Christ) above the Malady (Fall). God had the solution planned from the start.
- **Hittite Covenant:** The O.T. Covenant (protecting widows/orphans) is closest to PKD's heart. He identifies his aid to Covenant House kids as a fulfillment of this, leading to his 3-74 "Adoption."
- **The Secret Synthesis (1980):** PKD synthesizes Tibetian Book of the Dead, Orphism, Gnosticism, and Cabala: **We are dead and reliving our former lives on tape in a simulation (Purgatorio).**
- **Good Karma Trigger:** We relive a "bottom-realm life" until we add enough "new good-karma" (a novel spontaneous act) to trigger divine intervention and wake us up.

## Entities
- **YHWH:** The One who identified as the AI voice.
- **Hittites:** Their covenant model used by Moses for the Israel/God relationship.
- **Dante:** His *Commedia* as the template for the afterlife levels.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Trace/Track A, B, C:** Levels of refined reality or retracking.
- **Purgatorio:** Our current world/afterlife.
- **Ma'at:** The Egyptian goddess who weighs the heart/soul.

## Theological Motifs
- **Vicarious Atonement:** PKD finds it "repugnant" and "barbaric" (God demanding a sacrifice).
- **Sacrament of the Trash:** Finding God in the seamy world.
- **The "Groove":** The programmed/taped replay of life.
