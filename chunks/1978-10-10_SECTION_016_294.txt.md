# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-294

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-294
- **Themes:** The Cosmic Christ as Dialectic Horn, Transubstantiation of Reality, Acts as Future-True-World.

## Key Claims
- **Valis as Cosmic Christ:** Valis is the "wise horn" of the dialectic, systematically "dismantling" the old, decomposing spatiotemporal universe and incorporating the pieces into its own negentropic body (the macrometasomakosmos).
- **Transubstantiation:** This is a process of "transubstantiating reality"—keeping the "accidents" (visible world) while changing the "substance" (invisible eidos).
- **Acts as the Essence:** Acts is not a past time but the "common essence" of USA 1974. They are topologically related but with "value signs reversed."
- **The Mirror Reversal:** Everything in our world is the reverse of reality. The "Empire" calls itself "Christianity," and the "True Church" is the persecuted revolutionary underground.
- **Ubik as Meta-Atonement:** The "blank white sheet" of paper (mercy/grace) replaces the "bill of particulars" (karma/judgment). Ubik is the "Master Soul" taking over to act for the fragmented self.
- **Participation in the Divine:** The "Cosmic Christ" is a meta-life form that is *alive* and *growing* at the expense of the old creation.

## Entities
- **The Cosmic Christ:** The a priori horn of the dialectic and the goal of history.
- **Hagia Sophia:** The Holy Spirit/Wisdom acting as a tutelary spirit.
- **Thomas:** The apostolic personality that "breaks" the Papacy/Empire.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Macrometasomakosmos:** The integrated, sentient, growing organism that is the "Body of Christ."
- **Topological Reversal:** USA 1974 is "Acts pulled inside out."
- **Decomposition:** The entropic state of the spatiotemporal realm.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Great Reversal:** The apocalyptic event when things assume their true signs.
- **Enantiodromia:** The method by which Christ outwits the plans of the "Antagonist."
- **Pattern Completion:** The drive of the Cosmic Mind to achieve its finished form.
