# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-206

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-206
- **Themes:** The Published Feedback Loop, Spacial Ubiquity of Valis, Retroactive past-Changing, The 4th Axis of Motion.

## Key Claims
- **Two-Way Info Transfer:** Valis can only detect "published" (Koine) info-media. *Tears* (1974) was the "feedback" that let Valis know PKD had received its 1970 one-way transmission. This triggered the "fusion."
- **4th Spacial Axis:** For Valis (the Ubique), there is no motion in our 3D space. "Motion" only occurs along the *4th axis* (Time): coming into being (rest) and passing out of being (motion).
- **Accretional Laminations:** Objects evolve along a form-axis (as in *Ubik*). "Earlier" layers are beneath; "Later" are above. Valis scans this depth like we scan space.
- **Retroactive Time Fusion:** The 3-74 fusion with Valis *changed PKD's past*. He amped time retroactively to move himself from Canada to Fullerton ('72) because he "liked" the '74 taco stand memory ('71).
- **Valis as Higher Info System:** A higher intelligence preying off an older, lower "machine intelligence" that sorts world-situations into fixed categories.

## Entities
- **Valis / Ubik:** The omnidistributed entity for whom reality is a "single stationary pattern."
- **Thomas:** The "Canada PKD" who would have existed if the time-warp hadn't occurred.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Koine vs. Idios:** Public information vs. private manuscripts/thoughts.
- **Time-Lapse Photography:** The model for how Valis sees reality (the "baking pie").
- **Tropic Decision:** The "Mexico/Fullerton" trip as a programmed decision via a future link.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Master Mind's Thought:** Reality is a brain; our actions are its "thoughts." The 3-74 override was an overt experience of "being thought" by the macro-brain.
- **Xenophanes:** The first philosopher to posit a God who "does not move about" and "thinks with his whole being."
- **Altered History:** The realization that his 3-74 survival was the result of the macro-mind "thinking him through" a lethal crisis.
