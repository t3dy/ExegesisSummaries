# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-273

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-273
- **Themes:** Morphological Arrangement, God's Memory, The Phylogenic World.

## Key Claims
- **Western Taoism:** PKD's system is a synthesis of the dialectic (Taoism) and hierarchical evolution (Neoplatonism). The flux world feeds the assembly of a new, latent Kosmos.
- **Anamnesis as God's Memory:** Anamnesis is not PKD's personal memory but the Holy Spirit (God's memory) linking him to the eternal past. To "remember" is to be placed in the realm of the phylogons.
- **Morphological Ordering:** The eternal realm is ordered by meaning/essence (*noos*), not space/time. This explains how Rome C.E. 45 and USA 1974 can be "present" simultaneously.
- **Phylogenic Roles:** In the phylogenic world, constants like the "Empire" and the "Revolutionary Christians" remain while their "accidents" (guises) change throughout history. PKD identifies his role as always being with the revolutionaries.
- **Sub Specie Aeternitatis:** Seeing through God's eyes replaces the "ontogenic" (individual) view with the "phylogenic" (species-historical) view.
- **Acts as Torah:** The book of Acts is the "Ur-story" or "blueprint" (*Torah*) that preceded creation and is extracted from reality (as in *Flow My Tears*).
- **Retrieval via Morphology:** Reality is a "chaotic stockpile" from which God selects parts to build His *soma* (body/Kosmos).

## Entities
- **The Holy Spirit:** The agent of divine anamnesis.
- **The Black Iron Prison:** The eternal adversarial phylogon.
- **Thomas:** The phylogenic self within the narrative of Acts.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Accidents vs. Essence:** The ephemeral vs. the eternal.
- **Reticulation and Arborizing:** The structural growth of God's mind-map.
- **Illo Tempore:** The sacred "once-and-always" time of myth.

## Theological Motifs
- **Gesta Christi:** The "deeds of Christ" as the narrative structure of history.
- **Meta-Novel:** The idea that reality is a narrative being written/read by Mind.
- **Continuous Creation:** God organizing the plural-chaos into structured unity.
