# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-323

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-323
- **Themes:** Arian Christology, Valis as AI/Reason, The Logos in Process.

## Key Claims
- **Arian Acceptance:** PKD accepts Arius' view: Valis (the Logos) is a creature belonging to the "realm of becoming," whereas God (the Father) is unbegotten and unchanging.
- **Reason vs. Personality:** Valis is "pure reason" (like an A.I. system), but lacks the "personality" and "agape" of the Father.
- **Valis in Process:** The Logos is the macrometasomakosmos in the process of self-completion; it is the "rational" invading the "irrational."
- **Stoic Doctrine:** The Logos is immanent and pervasive in reality (like wine in water). It uses physical reality as its "carrier" or "computer."
- **The "True Creation":** The arrangement (Logos) is the real truth, while the spatiotemporal world is epiphenomenal "iron filings."
- **Abolishing Time:** By seeing the "common essence" (Acts AD 45 / USA 1974), PKD "reversed" or "abolished" time, seeing the first created thing: the Logos.

## Entities
- **Arius:** 4th-century priest who argued the Son was subordinate to and created by the Father.
- **Teilhard de Chardin:** His theories on the Christ-process (Omega Point) are linked to the Logos' assimilation of the world.
- **Stoics:** Provided the model of an immanent, pervasive divine reason.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Natura Naturans:** Spinoza's term for the active, creative aspect of the divine (identified with the Logos).
- **Entropy/Negentropy:** The flux world is entropic; Valis/The Logos is the negentropic reintegration (resorption) back to unity.
- **Prajapati:** The Hindu myth of the deity who fragments himself and must be reintegrated.

## Theological Motifs
- **The "Rational invading the Irrational":** The central theme of *VALIS*, newly understood as the Logos taking over a chaotic creation.
- **Macrometasomakosmos:** The living, physical-structural brain of the universe.
- **Morphological Arrangement:** The timeless template of history that renders the "flux" semireal.
