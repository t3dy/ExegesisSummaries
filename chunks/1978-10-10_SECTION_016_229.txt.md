# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-229

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-229
- **Themes:** Groove Override (GO), New Free Merit-Deed (NFMD), The Time-Travel Paradox, Ex Nihilo Acts.

## Key Claims
- **The Test:** Valis must remain concealed so our good deeds are "disinterested" (not done for reward). Once Valis shows itself, the "books are closed."
- **Reverse Causality:** PKD was saved by a *future* act of free will (giving to kids). The effect (Salvation/GO) preceded the cause (Deed/NFMD).
- **Thomas as the "Not-You":** "Thomas" acts without ratiocination (motor/speech centers only). He is the ideationless, ex nihilo self that breaks the tape/heimarmene.
- **Two Stages of Liberation:**
    1. **GO (Groove Override):** Breaking the replay determinism.
    2. **NFMD (New Free Merit-Deed):** Using total freedom to perform an act that gains release.
- **Bootstrap Paradox:** GO is the cause of NFMD, and NFMD is the cause of GO. It is a self-causing, ex nihilo system from outside time.

## Entities
- **Thomas:** The "not-you" ideationless self from the first tracking.
- **Ma'at:** The judge of the novel deed.
- **Valis:** The "master entity" or "reality generator."

## Recurring Concepts
- **Heimarmene:** Determinism/Damnation (the original tape).
- **GO / GO Situation:** The state of freedom from the groove.
- **Zen:** The quality of "Thomas'" spontaneous, intentionless action.

## Theological Motifs
- **Ex Nihilo:** Creation/Action from nothing.
- **Second Birth:** The birth of the "New Self" (Thomas) in the motor centers.
- **The One-Way Screen:** Valis observes us without disclosure to maintain the validity of the moral test.
