# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-320

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-320
- **Themes:** The Three Persons, God's "Will" vs. "Permission", Absolute Theodicy.

## Key Claims
- **Trinitarian Distinction:** 
    - **The Spirit:** Revealer, reminds and discloses (Noein).
    - **The Son (Valis/Logos):** Transubstantiates the universe, combats Satan, pure Reason.
    - **The Father:** Essence is Agape (Love), infinite rest, "imaged on us."
- **Will and Permission:** God's interaction with the world consists of (1) what He **wills** (decrees) and (2) what He **allows** (permits). The "allowing" creates the space for free will, error, and evil.
- **Absolute Theodicy:** Because God's essence is Agape, absolute theodicy (justification of all things) is a built-in fact. Rationality is secondary to theodicy.
- **Agape vs. Noein:** God’s nature is love (*agape*), not just thinking (*noein*). This ensures that we are "more than made up for" (joy/bliss) rather than just balanced (equipoise).
- **Joy as Proper Response:** If suffering is balance by joy, the response is equipoise. If joy *outweighs* suffering (infinite bliss), the response is absolute joy.
- **The "Little Ordeal":** Human life is an ordeal allowed by God, but it is "paltry" compared to the bliss He bestows.

## Entities
- **Goethe (Faust):** Cited for understanding the "wager in heaven" and the interplay of God's will and allowance.
- **Spinoza:** Rebuked for seeing only God's "will" as law, missing the "permission" aspect.
- **Leibniz:** Mentioned regarding "the best of all possible worlds."

## Recurring Concepts
- **Einai:** Being/Essence of God (Agape).
- **Equipoise:** A state of balance; PKD argues Christianity goes beyond this to Bliss.
- **Theodicy:** The core theological concern that God addressed by showing His nature.

## Theological Motifs
- **Voluntary Return:** Every creature will find its way back to God voluntarily due to His "permission."
- **Greco-Roman Reason:** PKD realizes he was trapped by the "Greek view" of a rational universe, missing the Christian "joy" of Agape.
- **The God of Job:** The God who pinpointed PKD's "underlying error" regarding theodicy.
