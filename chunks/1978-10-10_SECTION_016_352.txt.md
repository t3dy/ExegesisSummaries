# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-352

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-352
- **Themes:** Stopping the World, Laser Video Disc Reality, Beating Reality to Death.

## Key Claims
- **Stopping the World:** By freezing the flow of causality, one can discern the information. Perception of "flow" is at the expense of information.
- **The Binary Basis:** Reality works on a "rest-motion" (off-on) binary basis, linked to the linking-relinking of "meta-morphemes" (stored/finished info).
- **Laser Video Disc Model:** Reality is like 54,000 discrete frames on a disc side, replaced so fast they appear continuous. These frames are "sub-particulars."
- **Constants vs. Variations:** Certain constant structures (the *eide*) persist across all frames. Time (modulation) is just the method of finding out everything possible about these constants.
- **Continuous Creation:** God "destroys and recreates" the universe every nanosecond. "Causation" is our mind bridging the real gaps between these *ex nihilo* creations.
- **Retrieving the Zero Point:** The "Acts" perception may be a retrieval of the "initial frame" or "zero point" before variations were introduced.

## Entities
- **Castaneda:** Cited for the term "stopping the world."
- **Malebranche & Spinoza:** Their unitary view of God/Reality is modernized by quantum field theory.
- **Pat Warrick:** Credited with identifying that 3-74 required the modern physics worldview.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Exhaustion of Scrutiny:** We escape the loop of adjustments by "beating it to death" through relentless logic until the system commits an error.
- **Form-Axis:** Concept from *Ubik* where prior stages (Acts) are never lost but contained within the current frame.
- **Modulation:** The "wave form" that passes through the field to generate information.

## Theological Motifs
- **Cosmic Resorption:** Abstracting backward from particulars to the *eide*.
- **The True Motive Force:** God viewed under the aspect of world (unitary view).
- **Retrieval vs. Memory:** Acts as a recovered *frame* rather than a past event.
