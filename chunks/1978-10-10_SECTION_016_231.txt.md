# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-231

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-231
- **Themes:** Bicameral Time-Warping, Heuristic Scanning, Freedom as Time-Disruption.

## Key Claims
- **Ex Nihilo Information:** In 3-74, PKD knew things (like the contents of Acts) without reading them because his "Scanning Track" (Track A) had already done the work, then was erased.
- **Bicameral Certitude:** Solution B (the successful one) arrives without ratiocination because the "thinking" happened in the track that was abolished.
- **Time Displacement as Freedom:** Determinism (DNA robots) depends on linear time/causality. Breaking time (retrograde motion) breaks the prison.
- **The "Smart Typewriter" Erase Circuit:** Reality is a heuristic process where failure (A) generates the knowledge for the correction (B) which occurs simultaneously.
- **Self-Library:** The mind is "chambered." One part (ghostly future self) informs the other (conscious present self) to prevent the replay of failure.

## Entities
- **The Forest Bird / Kundry:** Wagnerian mythic guides.
- **Parsifal / Siegfried:** Archetypes of the "ignorant hero" who must be told his identity.
- **Zoroaster:** Cited for the idea of the "Anima/Spirit of Religion" acting as psychopomp.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Orthogonal Intervention:** Intervention coming from "90 degrees" relative to the time axis.
- **Feed-back Circuit:** The mind monitoring its own status across tracks.
- **Heimarmene:** The airtight tyranny of "groove tracking."

## Theological Motifs
- **The SIbyl:** The self acted as its own prophet.
- **Moksa / Liberation:** Achievement via knowledge derived from failure.
- **Creation at a Point:** Miracles appearing as "simultaneous" corrections of errors.
