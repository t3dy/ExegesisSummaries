# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-262

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-262
- **Themes:** Felix Culpa, The Workshop of Christ, Heraclitus the Steersman.

## Key Claims
- **The Workshop of Christ:** Suffering and war are allowed because they serve as a "workshop" that produces the parts for the Cosmic Christ. God has a "vested interest" in our combat.
- **Felix Culpa:** The "fortunate fall"—the goal of Valis is superior to a static, unfallen creation.
- **The Eternal Cycle:** History is self-perpetuating (revolutions become tyrannies). *Tears* and *Acts* represent the eternal Platonic reality that replicates itself forever.
- **Preservation of the Self:** Buddhism is "almost right" (weary wheel), but it misses the **resurrection**: everything we achieve is eternally preserved in the soma of Christ (phylogons).
- **Valis as Steersman:** Valis intervenes (as in 3-74) when one side (the Empire) becomes too powerful, using *enantiodromia* to restore balance and ensure the dialectic (and growth) continues.
- **God as a Child at Play:** Citing Heraclitus: "Time is a child at play, playing draughts." The divinity moves the pieces in combat according to rules to promote "evolution in strategies."

## Entities
- **Teilhard de Chardin:** Source of the "point omega" and "Christification" concepts.
- **Heraclitus:** The philosopher of flux, strife, and justice (measures).
- **The Imperial White House:** The historical imbalance that triggered the 3-74 correction.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Enantiodromia:** "Running the other way"—the flip to the opposite.
- **Measure (Justice):** The limit that the divine "Erinyes" (Furies) will not let the "Sun" overstep.
- **Stratagem Evolution:** History shows an increasing improvement in the ingenuity of both sides.

## Theological Motifs
- **Passion Play:** The sacred narrative is a tragic death followed by resurrection to immortality.
- **Cosmic Wheel:** The cycle of birth/death that actually builds a permanent edifice.
- **Divine Intervention:** A supernatural adjustment made to rectify a historical imbalance.
