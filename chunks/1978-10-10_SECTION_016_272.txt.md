# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-272

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-272
- **Themes:** Neoplatonist Hierarchy, Noos vs. Psyche, The Map Paradox.

## Key Claims
- **Ontological Superiority:** The view of reconciled opposites is a "higher" and "truer" view than the realm of flux. This justifies Parmenides' view that change is semireal compared to the Unchanging.
- **Plotinus and Ecstasy:** The One is God and sentient; ecstasy is rising to union with it.
- **Existence through Perception:** The One (Valis) exists uniquely when perceived, urging the dialectic toward greater consciousness. Perception of the One is "self-perception" by the One via the human microcosm.
- **The Map Paradox:** Seeing Valis causes a "frantic permutation" of thought where Valis catches sight of itself, initiating a "flight down a time axis."
- **Attributes of the Meta-View:** It is unitary, reconciled, accretional, permanent, sentient, and initiates movement.
- **Noos vs. Psyche:** PKD follows Anaxagoras/Hussey in replacing *psyche* (world-soul) with *noos* (mind). The universe is a set of ideas in God's mind (Berkeleyan idealism).
- **Taoist Alchemy (3-74):** The 1974 event was an "alchemical enantiodromia"—the hierogamy of the weak (Yin) reverting to the strong (Yang) and total estrangement turning into reconciliation.

## Entities
- **Anaxagoras:** Philosopher who introduced *Noos* as the ordering principle.
- **Milesians:** The early Greek philosophers concerned with the relationship between the Divine and the world-order.
- **Plotinus:** The primary Neoplatonist source.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Hierarchical Structure:** Reality as a series of levels (emanations) with increasing unity at higher stages.
- **Mapparadox:** The recursive feedback of the mind mapping the universe that contains the mind.
- **Krasis:** The total harmonious blend of forces.

## Theological Motifs
- **Self-Perception by the One:** God knowing Himself through human consciousness.
- **Latent vs. Obvious Form:** The master reality hidden beneath the chaos.
- **Ch'ang Tao:** The permanent, absolute Tao as the destination of 3-74.
