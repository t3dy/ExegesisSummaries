# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-306

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-306
- **Themes:** Sankara’s Two-Level Theory, The Dissociation of the Self, Valis as Brahman.

## Key Claims
- **Sankara's Solution:** Reconciles 3-74 and 11-17-80 through the distinction between: (1) *Nirgunam Brahman* (featureless Absolute seen in 3-74) and (2) *Sagunam Brahman* (personal Creator God seen in 11-17-80).
- **Accidental Disinhibition:** The "Thomas" (PKD2) personality was not supposed to link with PKD1 consciousness. Pentathol + the gold fish sign "dissolved the barrier."
- **Thomas as Revolutionary:** Thomas is an "intelligence officer" in a secret underground equipped with superior technology. PKD "blew the whistle" on them by publishing *VALIS*.
- **Scrambled Writing:** *VALIS* is a mixture of "arcane revolutionary knowledge" and "theological disinformation." 
- **The 3-74 Failure:** The "heroic act" (thwarting the Xerox letter) was PKD1 overriding the programming of PKD2 (Thomas). Thomas couldn't link with his contact (Joan?) and failed his mission.
- **Psychotronic Paranoia:** What PKD thought were "beams" or "telepathy" was actually his own other brain hemisphere (the "second self") breaking into consciousness.

## Entities
- **Sankara:** Indian philosopher whose two-level theory of Brahman explains both the absolute and personal deity.
- **Thomas (PKD2):** The "control" self, a 1st-century/revolutionary personality.
- **Joan (Sonoma):** Suspected contact for the underground revolutionary mission.
- **Pigspurt:** The "mind" or "programming" PKD felt he overrode.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Nirgunam vs. Sagunam:** The two aspects of the one Brahman.
- **Zwei Seelen:** "Two souls dwell, alas, in my breast!" (Goethe).
- **Acts Lens/Grid:** Thomas's way of viewing the contemporary USA as a "prison."

## Theological Motifs
- **The Self-Authenticating:** Both 3-74 and 11-17-80 are valid experiences of different levels of the divine.
- **Moksa:** The final discovery stage of liberation/knowledge.
- **The Word Made Flesh:** The Logos transubstantiating the physical world.
