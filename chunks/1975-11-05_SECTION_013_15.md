# November 5, 1975
# The Role of Elijah / Transformation of Character
## concise_summary
Dick concludes Section 13 by identifying the "fierce, earthy" spirit that came to him as Elijah (John the Baptist), preparing the world for the Parousia. He reflects on how "Christ stands in one’s stead" during judgment and emphasizes that his 1974 experience was a "saving intervention" rather than a mere memory.
## key_claims
- Elijah as the Preparer: The "fiery, zealous spirit" of 3-74 was Elijah (returned as John the Baptist); his task was to topple the modern "Ahab" (Nixon) to "make straight the weigh" for the King.
- Substitutionary Judgment: During judgment, "Christ stands in your stead," absorbing the "wrath" of the law; Dick experienced this as a "total rescue" from his own weaknesses.
- The "Crab" Analogy: Uses the analogy of an "honest crab" vs. a "cheating crab" (from an essay) to describe the "true exchange" required for salvation.
- The Withered State: After the "Head of the Beast" (Nixon) was removed, the state becomes a "withered, harmless thing," allowing the "Reign of Love" to begin in the hearts of the elect.
- Finality of the Vision: The 3-74 experience was the "finality" of his life; everything after is a "waiting" for the final Apokalupsis (visible end).
- Transformation of Character: The intervention changed his "very nature," replacing his "weak, depressive" ego with a "strong, ancient" fighter.
