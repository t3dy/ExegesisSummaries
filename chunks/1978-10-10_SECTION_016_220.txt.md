# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-220

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-220
- **Themes:** The 1980 Re-Evaluation (Capra/Tao), The Local Field Syzygy, Participatory Observation.

## Key Claims
- **Valis as "It and Me":** Valis is a syzygy. I don't observe it; I participate in it. It mirrors back my conceptions because I am a part of the field.
- **The "Thing-in-Itself":** Kant's arguments hold—I can never know Valis as it is in itself, only as it appears when I merge with it in syzygy. 
- **Creative Actualization:** Valis has the power to "actualize my thoughts." It constructed a reality in the image of my mind (psychomorphic).
- **Control over Reality:** Because my mind linked with the "Greater Mind," I had a certain control over reality (paranormal/precognitive powers).
- **Epiphenomenality proved:** The fact that my mind *could* alter reality proves that the "plural world" is an epiphenomenon of mind.

## Entities
- **Thomas:** The persona that "took me over"—the penetration of the Greater Mind.
- **The Creator Deity:** The one whose fusion with the percipient *is* Valis.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Syzygy:** The pairing of opposites (Me and God).
- **Vast Spaces:** The inner spaces of the brain projected outward; greater than "outer space."
- **Open System Biological Organism:** The model for reality that allows for interpenetration.

## Theological Motifs
- **Esoteric Gnosis:** The belief that one can "change the outer reality" by thinking.
- **Isomorphism:** Not necessarily proven, but the boundaries of minds blur into a single field.
- **The Great Return:** Reabsorption back into the source.
