# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-286

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-286
- **Themes:** Syntonic Superimposition, Morphological Ordering, Semireality of Accidents.

## Key Claims
- **Morphological Ordering vs. Spatiotemporal:** 3-74 was a "double exposure" where two space-time continua (Rome A.D. 45 and USA 1974) melded. This proved that reality is better ordered morphologically (by essence) than by space or time.
- **Semireality of Accidents:** "Accidents" (the spatiotemporal details) are only semireal. This confirms Plato's view that the flux world is an epiphenomenon hiding eternal constants.
- **Metamorphosis:** Superimposition is more like a metamorphosis; a building in California *is* a building in ancient Syria because they share a "common essence."
- **Parmenides and Change:** Change is "looking different" (accidents) but the essence remains unchanged. Time is a hypostatization based on the false assumption of change.
- **Anamnesis as Superimposition:** Unlike ordinary memory, true anamnesis brings the "past" into the "here and now," which is only possible if space and time are irreal.
- **Disinhibiting Stimulus:** Anamnesis must be triggered by an empirical perception (e.g., the golden fish sign) that connects two continua via a shared eidos.

## Entities
- **Plato:** The primary authority on the Form world and the semireality of flux.
- **Parmenides:** The philosopher who denied the reality of change.
- **Harvey (the cat):** Used later as an example of an "eternal cat" (eidos).

## Recurring Concepts
- **Syntonality:** The harmonious blending of two ostensibly different space-time realities.
- **Eidos (Form):** The eternal constant behind the shifting accidents.
- **Epiphenomenon:** The derived, secondary status of the physical world.

## Theological Motifs
- **Latent Form as Master:** The hidden structure controlling the obvious, visible form.
- **Maya/Dokos:** The veil of appearance hiding the "true einai" (being).
- **Proved in Writing:** PKD realizes he "proved" Platonism in *Ubik* (form-axes) long before he experienced it in 3-74.
