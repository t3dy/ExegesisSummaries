# GLOBAL NARRATIVE SUMMARY (1974-1981)

*This synthesis reflects the analysis of all 17 curated sections (883 chunks) covering the complete temporal span of the known Exegesis manuscript.*

## The Core Narrative Arc
The Exegesis follows Philip K. Dick's seven-year intellectual and spiritual journey to decode the "2-3-74" visionary experiences. The narrative begins as an urgent, epistolary effort (1974-1975) to share his "anamnesis" with friends and peers (Claudia, Ursula Le Guin, Robert Ornstein). By the mid-to-late 1970s, the narrative shifts from external correspondence to massive, internal journal-style notebooks (the Dorothy and Pat letters). 

The arc moves from a "detective story" (identifying the entity as Elijah, Dionysos, or Erasmus) to a mature "cosmological system" where the author identifies as a voluntary participant in a cosmic extrication process.

## Major Events & Figures
- **The March 1974 Vision**: The foundational "pink light" encounter that triggers the entire text.
- **The Fall of Nixon (1974)**: Interpreted as the literal overthrow of "The Beast" and the first visible sign of the Parousia.
- **The Death of Pinky (1975)**: A grief-stricken event that Dick interprets as a metempsychotic reincarnation of his mentor Tony Boucher.
- **The Writing of VALIS (1976-1978)**: The middle period is dominated by the recursive process of a novel (*VALISYSTEM A*) becoming the medium for his theology. 
- **The Binary-Fork Realization (1981)**: The final narrative resolution, where Dick reconciles his visions with the formal philosophy of Alfred North Whitehead.

## Developmental Arc
- **Early Phase (1974-75)**: Highly fragmented, reactive, and obsessed with immediate archetypal identification (Dionysos, Thomas, Elijah).
- **Middle Phase (1975-78)**: Systematic but paranoid; develops the "Empire Never Ended" and "Black Iron Prison" frameworks.
- **Late Phase (1978-81)**: Philosophical and abstract; shifts toward "Process Theology" and a view of God as an anti-entropic information system (VALIS).

## Final Resolution
The narrative concludes not with a single dogma, but with a refined method of "dialectic try-outs." In the final entries, Dick expresses a sense of being "scooped up" by a rational, loving information system that operates behind the veil of daily causality.
