# EMERGENT RESEARCH QUESTIONS (FINAL RECAP)

Based on the comprehensive 17-section analysis of the Exegesis corpus, the following research vectors are prioritized for the Humanities Workbench:

1. **The Evolution of the Divine Agency**: How does the entity Dick encounters shift from personified mythology (Dionysos/Elijah) to abstract systems-theory (VALIS/Binary Fork)? Is this a secularization of his mysticism or a more "scientific" theology?
2. **Computational Theology**: To what extent did the early SF trope of the "simulated reality" allow Dick to anticipate modern "Simulation Theory"? How does his "No/Yes selection" model in 1981 prefigure digital physics?
3. **The Scriptural Weight of the SF Canon**: A quantitative mapping: which novels are cited most often to explain theological puzzles? (Preliminary data suggests *UBIK* and *VALIS* are the primary hermeneutic lenses).
4. **Epistolary vs. Journal Modes**: How does the *format* of the Exegesis (letters to others vs. letters to self/God) change the rigor and tone of his arguments? (e.g., does he "perform" more for Ursula Le Guin than for Dorothy?)
5. **Gnosticism as Political Resistance**: Mapping the "Empire" motif. To what extent is the Exegesis a coded political protest against the Watergate-era surveillance state disguised as gnostic myth?
6. **The Whitehead Intersection**: A deep-dive into Section 17. How accurately did Dick "independently discover" Process Philosophy, and where do his "hallucinatory" data points conflict with Whitehead’s formal logic?
