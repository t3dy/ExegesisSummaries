# SUMMARY: SECTION_011
## September 17, 1975 — Letter to Doris Sauter

Section 11 is a pivotal theological pivot point, where Dick grounds his experiences in the biblical apocrypha—specifically **"The Wisdom of Solomon."** It formalizes the "Sophianic" identity of the 2-3-74 entity and introduces the concept of ontological transubstantiation.

### key_thematic_clusters

1. **The Biblical Haggia Sophia**:
   - Dick identifies the spirit of March 1974 with **Lady Wisdom** (Holy Wisdom/Sophia), the feminine personification of the Logos.
   - He argues that the Logos was originally feminine and that Western theology has "lost" this accepting, immanent presence.

2. **Cosmic Transubstantiation**:
   - Dick proposes a revolutionary ontological theory: the entire universe is undergoing a secret transformation into the "Mystic Body" of Christ/Sophia.
   - Using the Catholic model of the Mass, he explains that the "accidents" (the outward physical world) remain the same, but the "essence" (the underlying reality) is being transmuted from evil/pain to salvation.

3. **Justice and the Fall of the Tyrant**:
   - Wisdom of Solomon passages on "immortal justice" are used to explain the fall of Nixon and the end of the "great tyranny."
   - The "conspirators" were "seen" by the spirit, leading to their exposure and destruction—a literalization of the "judgment" theme.

4. **Teilhardian Evolution**:
   - Integration of Teilhard de Chardin's "Omega Point" and plasmatic principles.
   - Dick views himself as living "within Christ" during a "full year of Spring" (March 74-75).

### scholarly_value
This section is critical for identifying the specific literary and theological sources of Dick’s later *VALIS*-era Sophianism. It marks the transition from SF-inflected hypotheses to a more rigorous engagement with Catholic and apocryphal traditions, while maintaining his idiosyncratic "transubstantiation of reality" thesis.
