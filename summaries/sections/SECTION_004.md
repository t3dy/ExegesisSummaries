# SUMMARY: SECTION_004
## Undated — Letter to Claudia (second)

Dick writes again to Claudia, expanding on his theological eschatology. He elaborates on the "Kingship of God" Jesus spoke of—"among you / around you / within you"—arguing that a subtle seizure of power by the "Rightful King" has already taken place. The world *looks* the same, but the inner reality has changed: Truth and Justice now reign at the "heart" of a concentric-ring society, replacing the "rotten darkness" that previously stank at the core. He uses the extended metaphor of humane rescuers finding a factory-farm of enslaved animals, deposing the cruel owner, healing them, and showing them the green pasture beyond the prison wall. He also compares the situation to *Lord of the Flies*—the parents suddenly returning. Elijah came first to "refine" and "shape up" the elect before God's judgment. He emphasizes that judgment was not the purpose—*salvation* was. God acted like a teacher whose purpose is to teach, not to flunk students.

## key_claims
- The Kingship of God has arrived subtly; most men won't notice.
- The universe operates via "concentric rings," and the innermost ring has been replaced by light.
- Elijah came as a preparatory messenger with a "friends list" to refine people before Judgment.
- Divine Judgment is diagnostic, not punitive—it searches for who has learned, not who has "done bad."

## recurring_concepts
- Kingdom of God (already present)
- Elijah as herald
- Concentric-ring cosmology
- Prison / factory metaphor
- Spring replacing Winter

## scholarly_value
This section expands the political-theological framework of the Exegesis into a fully developed cosmology. The "concentric ring" model and the extended allegory of the animal factory anticipate Dick's later, more systematized treatments in *VALIS*. The emphasis on *salvation* over judgment is a key departure from Calvinist theology and aligns Dick with universalist currents.
