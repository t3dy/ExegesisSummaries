# SUMMARY: SECTION_007
## February 29, 1975 — Letter to Henry

Dick writes to Henry, recounting his discovery that the entity he believed was the ancient Phrygian god Dionysos was, in fact, Erasmus playing a joke. Erasmus had led Dick through layers of false profundity—puns and double-puns taken for solemn truths. But Dick then wonders: maybe Dionysos is pretending to be Erasmus pretending to be Dionysos? "Maybe they are the same." The universe is an onion whose innermost layer is also the outer.

The letter transitions into a systematic attempt to reconcile thermodynamics with his orthogonal-time cosmology. He argues that movement toward *form completion* (entelechy) must be orthogonal or opposite to entropy. He references Kozyrev's theory of left-spin energy, Heraclitus fragment 51 (the retractile pulling of bow and lyre), and the Christian fish sign as concealing two intersecting geometric arcs—representing the First and Second Coming of Christ as inevitable by the laws of geometry. He concludes with an alchemical vision: lead = poison, gold = spiritual transmutation.

## key_claims
- Dionysos/Erasmus/Zagreus may be a shapeshifting trickster-god layered in irony.
- "Jesus" is a substitution cipher encoding "Zeus" + "Zagreus."
- Orthogonal time is anti-entropic; entelechy moves against disorder.
- The Christian fish sign conceals two geometric arcs showing the Second Coming is geometrically inevitable.
- Alchemy's lead-to-gold transformation is psychological, not metallurgical.

## scholarly_value
This section introduces the important motif of *epistemological trickery*—Dick being unsure whether his revelations are from God, Erasmus, or Dionysos, and whether the layers can ever be fully peeled. The thermodynamic/cosmological argument (entelechy vs. entropy as orthogonal forces) is significant for mapping Dick's systematic philosophical ambitions. The fish-sign-as-geometry argument will recur throughout the later Exegesis.
