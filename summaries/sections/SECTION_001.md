# SUMMARY: SECTION_001

This section comprises the first two chunks of the Exegesis corpus, both from a long letter addressed to Claudia on February 27, 1975.

## What is being argued?
Philip K. Dick argues that the phenomenon of "form reversion" or "decay" depicted in his novel *UBIK* is not actually decay, but rather movement along an "orthogonal" axis of time. This orthogonal axis runs retrograde to linear time. He claims this orthogonal progression represents true spiritual growth and completion (entelechy). He argues that God "intruded" into linear time in March 1974—precipitating both the fall of Richard Nixon (whom he identifies as the "beast" of Revelation) and Dick's own spiritual awakening/Parousia. Furthermore, he argues that the endpoint of this orthogonal completion is fundamentally a return to the open, uncorrupted perceptual state of a child.

## What is being narrated?
Dick narrates personal autobiographical events that he believes have macro-cosmic significance. He recounts an intense visionary experience in March 1974, which he equates to Judgment Day, triggered in part by the death of his cat Pinky. He traces the spiritual roots of this event back to a deeply traumatic moment in the 1950s when he had to kill a rat to protect his daughters. This act of killing the rat taught him about the cosmic necessity of death (Karma and strife). He also narrates recent mundane experiences—a phone call with his ex-wife Anne accusing him of "hubris," feeling lonely and missing his deceased father, and lusting after Victoria Principal—which exist in stark, comical contrast to his visions of the Divine.

## Which concepts dominate?
- **Orthogonal Time vs. Linear Time**: The idea that true spiritual reality unfolds in a direction perpendicular (and retrograde) to our daily experience of time.
- **Entelechy / Completion**: The actualization of one's ultimate spiritual form.
- **The Parousia / Judgment Day**: Dick believes the "Final Things" have already occurred historically and personally.
- **Strife balancing Love**: Drawing on Empedocles to justify the necessity of death in the universe.
- **Anamnesis via Childhood**: The profound spiritual clarity available only to the archetype of the "child" before the World instills its limitations.

## Which entities and texts matter most?
- **People**: Claudia (recipient), Richard Nixon (identified as the Beast/666), Erasmus (a spiritual guide replacing his father), Pinky the cat, Tessa (wife).
- **Texts**: *UBIK* (whose plot is retroactively used to explain his own theology), *FLOW MY TEARS, THE POLICEMAN SAID* (which he views as his contribution to the war against the Beast), the Book of Revelation.

## How does the section relate to fiction and literary production?
Dick is actively engaging in self-exegesis, using his own science fiction to decode his religious experiences. He views *UBIK* not merely as fiction, but as a prophetic or intuitive modeling of the "orthogonal time axis." He also treats *FLOW MY TEARS, THE POLICEMAN SAID* as a strategic, almost magical text that helped topple the Nixon presidency. Fiction, for Dick, is a conduit for theological truth that bypasses the rational mind.

## What is uncertain or unstable?
Dick exhibits deep conceptual instability regarding his own "perfection." He claims his entelechy was completed in 1974 and that he was filled with the flawless Holy Spirit, but he immediately undercuts this by pointing out his ongoing "wicked thoughts" (lusting after actresses) and basic human flaws. He resolves this tension somewhat arbitrarily by concluding that God's grace intruded to save him *despite* his lack of perfection. His numerological breakdown of Nixon = 666 is similarly playful, associative, and unstable, shifting from 666 to 9-599 as his wife corrects his Latin.

## scholarly_value
This section is foundational for any researcher mapping the origins of Dick's post-1974 theological system. It establishes the critical vocabulary he will use throughout the Exegesis ("orthogonal time," "Parousia," "entelechy") and vividly demonstrates his primary hermeneutic method: reading his own fictional canon (specifically *UBIK* and *FLOW MY TEARS*) as divine revelation. It also highlights the idiosyncratic, often humorous fusion of high theology (Empedocles, St. Paul) with mundane pop culture (canned fish billboards, Victoria Principal), which is the hallmark of his late style.
