# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_57
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_57
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: Protestant vs. Catholic Justification, The Artist as Pantocreator, Introjecting the Soul, Assimilating Negentropy
- **Key Entities**: Carl Jung, Erwin Schrödinger, Ludwig Boltzmann

## Key Claims
- Dick weighs Protestant Justification (salvation despite total depravity) against Catholic Justification (where acts of merit, like his charity to Covenant House, play a role), leaning toward the Catholic and Jungian view of *completion*.
- He draws a profound analogy between God and the Artist: Just as God justified and completed Dick by injecting the adventitious psyche (Thomas), Dick justified and completed his *own work* by creating the ex nihilo character Angel Archer.
- Dick then *introjected* the character he created, adopting Angel Archer as his own soul. This dynamic creates a new "push-pull Ditheon" between Dick and his creative work, healing the schism between man and artist.
- Relying on Schrödinger, Dick explains that organisms avoid thermal death by ingesting "negative entropy" from their environment. The adventitious psyche is precisely this: a massive dose of environmental negentropy swallowed to stave off spiritual death.
- If the organism fails to assimilate this new information, it falls back to recirculating old data, increasing its internal entropy (Boltzmann) until it dies.

## Entities
- **Erwin Schrödinger**: His biological definition of life—avoiding equilibrium by consuming negative entropy—is the exact mechanism of the Ditheon.
- **Carl Jung**: His concepts of individuation, the Mysterium Coniunctionis, and the assimilation of opposites undergird Dick's theory of the soul's birth.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Artist as Analog to God**: Exegesis as meta-art, where the act of writing structurally mirrors the act of divine creation and salvation.
- **Assimilation of the Environment**: The survival strategy of swallowing external reality to prevent internal stagnation.

## Theological Motifs
- **Justification as Completion**: Redefining theological justification as the literal, structural addition of the "missing pieces" required to make an android into a human.
