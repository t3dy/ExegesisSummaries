## Key Claims
- Dick questions whether occasionalism (pretextual causation) is the normal rule for everyone, or if his experience with the Xerox missive and the "Strawberry Fields" information about his son was a customized anomaly (a temporary simulation of normal causality).
- He cements his highest conception of God: God does not simply make the universe real *in itself*; rather, God exerts his power directly on human minds to make the universe *absolutely real to us*.
- The 1974 event was empirical proof of Malebranche, Plato, Paul, Augustine, and Plotinus. Dick saw reality as it actually is: a set of interlinked permanent ideas in a mind, with causation abolished.
- Above all, Dick witnessed the "perturbation in the reality field." Because the reality field (world) cannot perturb itself ex nihilo, this perturbation serves as the ultimate, indubitable inferential proof of a transcendent God acting upon creation from the outside.

## Entities
- **Nicolas Malebranche**: His occasionalism (God as the only true cause) is declared absolutely verified by the 3-74 experience.
- **Plato / Plotinus**: Verified regarding the reality of the *eide* (eternal ideas/forms) rather than material substance.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Strawberry Fields / Chrissy**: Dick refers to the specific, actionable medical information he received about his son's birth defect as undeniable proof of pretextual cause/divine intervention.
- **Compelling Assent**: The Cartesian/Malebranchian concept that human beings require external divine power to actually believe the sensory representation of the world is real.

## Theological Motifs
- **Creation Ex Nihilo**: Redefined not just as the historical beginning of the universe, but as the perpetual, ongoing introduction of newness (teleology) replacing mindless mechanical causation.
