# The Ham Sandwich Model and the Ethics of the Gutter
## concise_summary
Dick identifies as an "acosmic panentheist," asserting that only the Urgrund is real. He presents the "Ham Sandwich" model of reality: man and God are the isomorphic slices of bread, separated only by the "ham" of the temporary, illusory world. He emphasizes that the final judgment is based on "ethics of the gutter"—simple acts of kindness toward the lowly and rejected, which are actually acts toward the hidden, disguised deity.
## key_claims
- Acosmic Panentheism: Nothing is real but the Urgrund; the world is a temporary and antagonistic illusion.
- Identifying with the Minimum: The deity (Christ) has identified himself with the "least of us" (the poor, hungry, imprisoned).
- Stealth Penetration: The Urgrund penetrates the world through its lowest strata (gutter trash) to assess and aid humanity.
- Second Advent Vengeance: The First Advent (meekness) will be balanced by the Second Advent (vengeance/destruction of the cosmos).
- Ham Sandwich Metaphor: Man and the Urgrund are one entity once the "world" (the ham) is removed.
