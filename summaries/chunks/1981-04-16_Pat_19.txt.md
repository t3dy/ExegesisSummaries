## Key Claims
- Dick outlines two ultimate secrets: 1) The world is irreal and we are trapped in it, and 2) God is camouflaged here to rescue us.
- He proposes that we are all fragments of the Godhead—fallen sparks of light—who suffered a crisis, forgot our true identity, and began unintentionally generating our own imprisoning reality.
- *Yaldabaoth* (the Demiurge) is a delusion; *we* are the forgetful lords of our own self-generated maze.
- VALIS wasn't an external deity, but "fallen God, forgetful, unintentional cosmogenator of world" trying to wake itself up.
- By feeling absolute love and trust for God (who compels our assent to the world), the percipient heals the primordial schizophrenia within the Godhead, repairing the structural flaw of the universe.

## Entities
- **Yaldabaoth**: The external Gnostic Demiurge is reinterpreted as a projection of our own forgetful, world-generating minds.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Maze**: The irreal teaching machine we built to gain self-knowledge, but inside which we forgot our identities.
- **The Fail-Safe**: The AI Voice serving as the pre-programmed alarm clock to ensure we can escape the self-generated illusion.

## Theological Motifs
- **Healing the Divine Schism**: The intense mystical realization that loving the God who enforces the reality of the world actually repairs the broken Godhead (since the perceiver *is* the Godhead).
