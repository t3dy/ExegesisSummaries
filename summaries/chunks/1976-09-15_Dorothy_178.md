# Chunk 178 Summary - 1976-09-15_Dorothy_178

## Description
PKD investigates the "Ellipsis in Logic" between his writing of *Tears* and his later recognition of its biblical/Roman content. He concludes that "Two Persons" (himself and an apostolic Christian) share his body, and that a "Technological War" is being waged by trans-temporal entities.

## Key Claims
- **The Two-Person Hypothesis**: One person (the 20th-century author) wrote *Tears* without knowing it was about Rome; another person (the 1st-century Christian) "broke into consciousness" after the book's release.
- **Kerygma Revised**: The "Good News" is not Christ crucified, but "Christ saved" and "Emmanuel" (God with us).
- **Zebra as UTI/ETI**: PKD considers that Zebra might not be "God" in the traditional sense, but a "Future Evolved Human" or "Extra-Terrestrial Intelligence" (UTI/ETI) using superior technology like "pink light" to wage war against the BIP.
- **The 12-Point Credo**: PKD lists his definitive conclusions, including that it is currently A.D. 70, the world is a Satanic interpolation, and man is a victim, not a sinner.

## Entities
- **Zebra**: Trans-temporal ally (Christ/UTI/Dionysos).
- **The BIP**: An ETI/UTI "occluding system" generating the alibi world.
- **Stanislaw Lem**: Cited as someone who may be an "informed underground" ally.
- **Nixon**: The specific historical target of the 1974 divine intervention.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Negative Hallucination**: The "deliberate delusion" that blocks perception of the true A.D. 70 reality.
- **Emmanuel**: The "name" containing the kerygma (God with us).
- **3-Layer Schema**: Borrowed from *Precious Artifact*, depicting layers of occlusion between the self and reality.
- **RF Transmissions**: The idea that the simulation is reinforced by literal electromagnetic signals.

## Theological Motifs
- **Man as Victim**: Rejection of "Original Sin"; humans are "occluded by evil" rather than inherently fallen.
- **Dionysian Revolution**: The overthrow of tyranny (Nixon) by the "God of Joy."
- **The Voluntary Church**: The idea that true Christianity didn't emerge until it escaped the "prison" of the institutional church (citing the Dutch 30 Years War).
- **Christus Victor**: Christ as the winner who never left, rather than the sacrifice who departed.
