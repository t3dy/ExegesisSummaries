# Chunk 168 Summary - 1976-09-15_Dorothy_168

## Description
This chunk emphasizes the restoration of "Pentecost to the present." PKD argues that if the 2,000 intervening years are "irreal," then the Holy Spirit is currently descending onto "the 12" (including PKD/Thomas), and the Roman Empire is currently falling to the "Light."

## Key Claims
- **Pentecost in the Present**: The spirit-filled world of *Acts* is our actual location; the 2,000 years between Advents are an illusion designed to banish the Light.
- **Overthrow of the Government**: The victory of the Light is manifest in the (1974) overthrow of the American government (perceived as Rome).
- **Trash Routes of Grace**: The Light re-enters history through "despised and rejected" forms to escape detection by " solemn" evil powers.
- **Agape and Revolution**: The decoding of *Tears* calls for a "revolution by the prisoners led by God" followed by "love and reconciliation."

## Entities
- **The 12 (Apostles)**: Restored to life as those "seized by the spirit" today.
- **Zebra**: The power recontouring causal trains.
- **Pinky**: Reference to a miraculous situation where Zebra/Christ's presence was ontologically experienced.
- **Asimou / Van Vogt**: Authors cited for their themes of fake memories and Roman Empire parallels.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Alibi**: Linear time (2,000 years) as a diabolical "negative hallucination" or alibi to conceal the true present.
- **Disinhibition of Blocked Circuits**: PKD's own writing acts to wake up the "inhibited circuits" of the reader.
- **Sacred Time vs. Mundane Time**: The permanence of archetypes reduces linear time to a "zero factor."
- **Visions of Masks**: References to a dream in Canada where everyone removes their masks to reveal the truth.

## Theological Motifs
- **Christ the Champion of the Broken**: "God of the very desperate"; Christ is found where the world "pulverizes" the individual.
- **Transubstantiation of Identity**: The "I" in the person is replaced by the "I" of the Holy Spirit/Christ.
- **The Word Virus**: Mention of Burroughs' "Ticket That Exploded" as a model for the "criminal virus" of words/delusion.
