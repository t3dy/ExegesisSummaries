# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_164
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_164

- **Themes**: Negentropy, Eschelatological Time, Justification as Cosmic Adjustment

## Key Claims
- Dick defines the Logos (Hyper-info) as negentropy itself, confirming the conceptual trajectory he began years earlier in his novel *Ubik*.
- He interprets the Tagore/Kabir visions through the pan-Indian "bride/bridegroom" mystical relationship. The collapse of 2000 years into a perceived "short interval" was the creation of eschatological time, born of the joyous, eager anticipation of the Bridegroom's (Christ's) imminent return.
- "Justification" is not merely a legal or moral designation; it is a literal, physical suspension of the deterministic cosmic retributive machinery (Heimarmene). It is an actual change in the person's status within the physical world-order.
- This kind of justification/leap cannot occur within a closed mechanistic system. Therefore, the very reality of justification proves the intervention of a transmundane entity entering the system from the outside.

## Entities
- **Kabir / Tagore**: Referenced for the bride/bridegroom metaphor, capturing the essential mystical feeling of eager anticipation for God's presence.
- **Shiva / Vishnu (Krishna)**: Recognized as parallels to Christ; the "sole male in the universe" to whom all souls relate as female devotees.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Eschatological Time**: Time experienced not as a linear progression of centuries, but as an emotionally charged, collapsed "now" defined entirely by the impending arrival of the Savior.

## Theological Motifs
- **Justification as Physics**: The Pauline concept of justification is reinterpreted entirely. It is not about avoiding hell after death; it is a real-time, physical adjustment of causality in the present universe to exempt a person from deterministic fate.
