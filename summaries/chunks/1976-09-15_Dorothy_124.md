# Anamnesis as an Organ of Perception
## concise_summary
Dick introduces a seminal breakthrough: viewing "anamnesis" (memory) as a literal organ of perception that allows one to see the divine organism (Brahman) extending through time, just as the eyes see it in space. He describes "Sacred Time" as a collapsed, mythic state where millenia are experienced as a single drama—the death and imminent return of the savior. He views the "Zebra" entity as a "master magician" or "prankster" whose temporal invasion abates our fear and prepares us for the "King's" arrival.
## key_claims
- Anamnesis as Sensory Organ: Memory is the "organ" required to perceive the extension of reality in time. In 3-74, PKD was "no longer blind" because he could see temporal extension.
- Temporal Organism (Brahman): Reality is a living mind/body whose "respiration" is the unceasing breaking down of forms to make way for new ones.
- Sacred/Mythic Time: A different kind of time where 2,000 years shrink into one immediate sequence. In 3-74, PKD stood at the point of "looking ahead to the savior's return."
- Zebra the Prankster: The divine entity uses riddles, pranks, and "will-o'-the-wisp" sightings (UFOs) to beguile us and reduce our fear of the unknown.
- Participation in the Totality: True joy comes from relinquishing the individual self (the "subsection") for participation in the destiny of the total One.
