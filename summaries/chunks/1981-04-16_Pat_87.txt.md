# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_87
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_87

- **Themes**: Recombinant Scripture, Transubstantiation of the World, The Acceleration of Time, Direct Inward Compulsion
- **Key Entities**: Nicolas Malebranche

## Key Claims
- In the Third Age (The Spirit), scripture does not take the form of a fixed canon (like the Torah or Gospels). Instead, it is "recombinant meta-morphenes" that instantly permutate like stock market ticker tape to provide highly individualized instructions for specific situations.
- Time is accelerating exponentially, inversely proportional to causation, heading toward a limit of pure space ("simultaneous grid-cause") and acausality.
- The distinction between God and the physical world vanishes in the Third Age. The world itself becomes the interface and the Holy place. This is "transubstantiation on a macro scale"—the mundane is entirely hallowed.
- The guidance of the Spirit is internalized and absolutely compelling. Unlike the Law (which forces external compliance) or Grace (which offers fallible choice), the Spirit guarantees correct action through an infallible inner voice.

## Entities
- **Nicolas Malebranche**: His philosophical doctrine of Occasionalism (where God is the only true causal agent) is cited to describe how Valis replaces or controls mundane reality from behind the scenes.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Living Information**: Scripture that thinks, behaves biologically, and recombines to meet the specific requirements of the observer.
- **The Parousia Dream**: Dick references a dream where God "takes the field" and directly blocks people from making errors using subliminal message traffic.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Evolution of the Holy Spirit**: The Spirit is conceptualized not just as a comforter, but as a cybernetic control system feeding dynamic, situation-specific routing data to the believer.
