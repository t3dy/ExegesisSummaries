## Key Claims
- Dick realizes that *VALIS* retells the story of the Acts of the Apostles: Horselover Fat grieving for Gloria corresponds to the eleven grieving for Jesus.
- The experiences of 2-3-74 represent Pentecost/Acts—the time of the Holy Spirit interacting with the disciples.
- The 3-18-81 revelation confirms that the Parousia has *now* arrived; historical time has progressed since 1974.
- Sophia's new *Kerygma* in the novel calls for revolutionary millenarianism (deposing the "tyrant" Nixon), showing that the Second Advent overlays the First Advent.
- Viewing Advents one and two as a "single event composed of two passes" explains why Dick saw both Acts and 1974 USA superimposed in the same spatiotemporal happening.

## Entities
- **Holy Spirit**: The Comforter that returned to transform Fat's grief into faith.
- **Horselover Fat**: Corresponds to the grieving early church/the eleven disciples.
- **Gloria**: Corresponds to the crucified Jesus.
- **Sophia**: Provides the new revolutionary Kerygma for the Second Advent.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Enthusiasmos**: The divine entering the individual, prompting the "alert waiting" for the Parousia.
- **Superimposition**: Acts and 1974 USA existing together based on essential (rather than chronological) identity.

## Theological Motifs
- **Two Advents as One**: The idea that the First and Second Comings form a single structural event completed in modern times.
- **Eschatological Judgment**: History acts as a "winnowing fan" allowing light and dark, initially commingled, to reveal themselves through acts.
- **Revolutionary Millenarianism**: Taking power from the tyrant under God's authority.
