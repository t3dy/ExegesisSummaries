## Key Claims
- The core of the Christian-Gnostic revolt is the absolute rejection of the "retributive machinery" (Karma, the Mosaic Law, Heimarmene) that keeps humanity enslaved in endless tragedy.
- True, cosmic victory is achieved through apparent defeat. The Black Iron Prison poses as "good" while it is actually evil; meanwhile, true Christians exist invisibly at the heart of the Empire, mimicking their surroundings while subverting it.
- This dynamic perfectly aligns with the *Tao Te Ching*—the yielding, meek, passive, and invisible power (Yin) ultimately conquers the rigid, violent, and proud power (Yang).
- Because the ruling power is perceived as good, the existence of a "secret good" hidden within it makes no sense unless you have the Gnosis to see that the ruling power is actually evil.

## Entities
- **Christ**: The master strategist who uses the gentle mechanics of the Tao to infiltrate and defeat the Roman Empire/BIP from the inside out.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Latent Structure is the Master of Obvious Structure**: Coaxial World B (the Kingdom) secretly controls and subverts Coaxial World A (The Empire).

## Theological Motifs
- **Christus Victor vs The Eschatological Judge**: Instead of returning as a stern King with armies, the victorious Christ (Tagore) arrives as a wounded martyr whose very suffering is the judgment that dooms the unrepentant world.
