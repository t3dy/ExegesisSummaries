# Liberation from the Program
## concise_summary
Dick explores the idea that humans are "programmed" by the artifact to maintain the simulation. Salvation is the liberation from this programming, allowing the "transtemporal self" or the "Omega Man" to emerge and see reality as it truly is.
## key_claims
- Programmed Humanity: Human behavior is largely a set of automated responses designed by the artifact.
- Transtemporal Self: Each person has a "higher" version of themselves that exists outside of linear time.
- Omega Man: The final, fully-realized state of humanity where the program is discarded.
- Liberation: Breaking the programmatic loop through divine intervention (Zebra).
