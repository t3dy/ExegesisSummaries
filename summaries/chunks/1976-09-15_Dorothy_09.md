# Maya, Ubik, and the Brahman Synthesis
## concise_summary
Dick fully embraces a Hindu framework, identifying the world as "Maya" (wizardry/illusion) and his 3-74 experience as the death of the "empirical ego" leading to "enlightenment" (Liberation). He equates the entity **Ubik** with **Brahman**—the divine stuff found even in trash and mundane objects.
## key_claims
- Maya (Illusion): The "veil of Maya" prevents man from remembering his true origin. 3-74 was the tearing of this veil.
- Death of Ego: The 1974 "annihilation of ego" was the prerequisite for seeing Brahman.
- Sum Atman Dei: Reinterprets a past acid trip phrase ("I am the spirit of God") as "I am Atman/Brahman."
- Liberation: This knowledge ensures he will not be reborn on the wheel but reabsorbed into the "adorable void" (Brahman).
- Ubik = Brahman: The "Ubiquitous" entity in his novel *Ubik* is actually a description of Brahman—present in "trash, nearby & small, temporary things."
- We are Gloves: Humans/objects are "forms which Brahman assumes" or "gloves which God puts on."
- I Ching as Brahman's Voice: Analyzes several hexagrams (14, 27, 37, 49, 58) as Brahman's instructions for the "superior man."
- Isomorphism: We don't see Brahman because we don't resemble Him; to see Brahman, one must "be Him."
