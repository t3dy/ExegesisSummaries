# Mater Dei: Humanity as the Womb of God
## concise_summary
Dick synthesizes several cultural and literary motifs—from *The Bacchae* to Hollywood comedies—to illustrate a "King of Tears" (the artifact) who brutally rules over a kingdom without realizing that the true Maker (the Urgrund) is present in disguise. He posits that humanity is a "Mater Dei" (Mother of God), an evolutionary host from which God is born through the "impregnating" intervention of the Holy Spirit. Pointless suffering is the "birth pang" that eventually forces the Maker to awaken (anamnesis) and abolish the artifact.
## key_claims
- King of Tears Motif: The artifact is a "regent" or "King of Tears" (like in *The Bacchae*) who opresses the world and will be overthrown by the returned "Stranger" (God).
- Humanity as Mater Dei: Biological evolution has produced humanity not to become God, but to serve as a womb (host) for the birth of God.
- Suffering as Catalyst for Anamnesis: The artifact's "pointless, unmerited pain" will eventually goad its own hidden maker into recollecting his identity.
- Homing Instinct: Humans possess an innate drive to return to the "Pleroma" (the supralunar region of divine fullness).
- Repudiation of Pain: Any attempt to find redemptive value in suffering is a trap; the only valid response is a total "repudiation" of the irreal system of the artifact.
