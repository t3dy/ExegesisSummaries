## Key Claims
- Applying Nicolas Malebranche's philosophy, Dick concludes that in 3-74 objects were transformed into intelligible Ideas. Because finite things cannot be experienced directly, God presents us with surrogate representations (icons) of the unitary Archetypal Ideas held in His mind.
- The core of the 2-74 meta-abstraction is recognizing: "Not two mothers, but one mother twice." There is only one eternal Idea of an object; it is reused infinitely in different times and places. Plurality is an illusion.
- Schizophrenia is the misapplication of this meta-abstraction: confounding two genuinely different objects/people as being one single entity. For example, Dick realizing that "Sandra" is actually two distinct personalities confounded into one concept.
- *A Scanner Darkly* was an early, unconscious exploration of this exact cognitive dysfunction—the failure to properly distinguish between identities.

## Entities
- **Nicolas Malebranche**: His concept that we "see all things in God" provides the structural explanation for how the physical world was transformed into a conceptual matrix (the MMSK) during Dick's epiphany.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The One Rose**: Using medieval philosophy, Dick illustrates that there is only one actual "Rose" (the archetype in God's mind). What we experience are pluralized icons/reflections of that single Idea.

## Theological Motifs
- **Omnipresent Ideas**: By abolishing the concept of spatial and temporal plurality, the sacrament allows the participant to experience the pre-existent, unitary Idea of Christ directly, rather than localized copies.
