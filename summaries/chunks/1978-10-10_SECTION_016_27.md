# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-27

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-27
- **Folder Reference:** Folder 21 - 122 to Page 9 (Valis Manuscript)
- **Themes:** Valis as Non-Fiction, 3-Eyed People, Rational vs. Irrational.

## Key Claims
- **Novels as Truth:** PKD concludes that his writing (*Stigmata*, *Ubik*, *Valis*) should be regarded as literal non-fiction describing our exact situation.
- **3-Eyed People:** Characters from the "Valis" manuscript (builders/crab-claws) are the actual controllers who can readjust our hologram at will.
- **God as Antidote:** The true God mimicks trash (mimesis) to ambush reality and "injure" us as a sober antidote to the madness of the world.
- **Rational Invasion:** The universe and man are mutually toxic/irrational. God (Zebra) is the "rational" that breaks into the irrational to provide a comparison.
- **Moksa Experience:** PKD identifies his 3-74 experience as the "Eckehart-Sankara" moksa: the abolition of the division between microcosm and macrocosm.

## Entities
- **The 3-Eyed People:** Deaf, mute builders with telepathic powers outside the hologram.
- **Zebra / Valis / Ubik:** The "information-rich beam" or "plasmate" that invades from the future.
- **Sherri / Gloria:** Characters from the "Valis" narrative context representing the "beleaguered mind."

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Empire Never Ended:** The persistent reality of the Roman prison masked by history.
- **Acosmic Christianity:** A melding of Sankarism and Christianity.
- **Pyrrhic Victory:** The Exegesis itself is seen as a "victory of a beleaguered mind trying to make sense of the inscrutable."

## Theological Motifs
- **Moksa:** Liberation/Enlightenment as described by Sankara and Eckehart.
- **Divine Ambush:** God hiding as "trash" to surprise the deluded world.
- **The World as Maze:** A test/study situation built by a higher species.
