## Key Claims
- Dick recalls a recent terrifying experience where God intervened to stop him from committing a "mortal sin" regarding his affair with Sandra and the potential abandonment of his family (Tessa and Chris).
- This was not just an intellectual realization, but an invasion of absolute moral knowing by YHWH. God compelled assent to the moral law, operating with the inexorable force of physical causation ("His will is law").
- While the 1974 experience was characterized by the loving mercy of Hagia Sophia, this recent intervention was the angry, just intervention of YHWH demanding moral rectitude.
- In Judaism, morality and reality are identical. Sin leads to death, and God stepped in to prevent Dick from suffering literal and spiritual doom.

## Entities
- **YHWH**: Experienced here not as the loving Father but as the imposing Lord of the Torah, enforcing the moral law as the foundational law of the cosmos.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Meta-Abstraction**: Reinterpreted here not just as epistemological insight, but as the act of *Knowing* exactly as God knows (a priori and absolute).

## Theological Motifs
- **Damnation**: The state of falling into mortal sin by willfully choosing chaos and disrupting the moral order of YHWH's cosmos.
