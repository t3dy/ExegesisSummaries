# The Restoration of Thomas: Retroactive Identity Erasure
## concise_summary
Dick explores the "profound mystery" of his identity shift in 3-74, concluding that his "PKD" personality was a retroactive replacement for his authentic "Thomas" personality. He posits that the "Urgrund" (God) uses the BIP world as a teaching machine, testing humans on whether they will obey or "balk" against cruelty. Once a human rebels, God retroactively changes their history, "erasing" the enslaved identity and replacing it with a saved, ancestral, or divine one.
## key_claims
- Identity Replacement: In 3-74, "Thomas" (the authentic A.D. 70 soul) did not replace PKD; rather, PKD was a "false" identity that had been retroactively imposed on the original Thomas.
- Retroactive Time Manipulation: God "tampered" with the past to erase the lost soul (PKD) and restore the saved soul (Thomas).
- The Teaching Machine: The world (as a hologram) is a series of "clues" and "puzzles" used by God to judge human behavior (moral and intellectual).
- Moral Balking First: Enlightenment usually follows a "moral aversion" or act of refusal, rather than preceding it.
- Tracks A and B: Track A was the original "Thomas" world (A.D. 70/Rome); Track B is the edited "PKD" world. The Xerox letter was a hold-over from Track A intended for Thomas.
