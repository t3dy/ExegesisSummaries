# Letter to Dorothy (Part 2) / The Cosmic Game
## concise_summary
Dick concludes his letter by describing the "two worlds" hidden beneath the veil (*Dokos*): the Iron Empire and the Palm Tree Garden. He introduces the "Cosmic Game" between the Wise Mind and the Dark Counterplayer, and details the symbolic colors of the Trinity.
## key_claims
- Dokos (The Veil): Reality is obscured by a "veil" of illusion.
- Two Hidden Worlds:
  - The Iron Empire (Rome/Prison): As seen in Daniel and Revelation.
  - The Holy Land (Palm Garden): A pastoral kingdom of "unchange."
- Orthogonal Time: The past and future are the same in "hypertime"; things never cease to be, they only "pass out of sight."
- The Dualist Game: A perpetual game between the "Wise Mind" (God) and the "Dark Counterplayer" (Blind Force).
- Salvation as Trick: The Wise Mind "wins every trick" (every soul) by loading the Dark Counterplayer into assisting the evolution of the universe.
- Trinity Colors:
  - Logos: Red and Gold.
  - Savior (Christ): Blue.
- *Deus Irae* References: Confirms that mystical scenes in *Deus Irae* (Pete Sands, Dr. Abernathy) are autobiography-based.
