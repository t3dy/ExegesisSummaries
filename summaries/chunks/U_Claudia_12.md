# Claudia
## concise_summary
Dick proposes a "Brain Colony" or "Grain Brain" model of the universe, where individual humans are interconnected cells in a vast compound eye of the Immanent Mind. He explores the "male womb" of Zeus/Dionysos as a metaphor for the motherless second birth and identifies with the "fiery power" of the subject of destiny (Brahmanism) rather than the lunar cycle of rebirth.
## key_claims
- The universe is an "Immanent Mind" or "Brain Colony" (many discrete cells forming one field).
- Humanity is a "compound eye" where each person reflects reality slightly differently.
- The "second birth" is a motherless birth (Dionysos in the thigh of Zeus), where the deity takes on both paternal and maternal roles.
- Dick identifies with "Form One" (the fiery God/Subject of destiny) rather than the "lunar" (object of destiny/Christos) cycle.
- The Greek concepts of *Moira*, *Ananke*, and *Harmonie* are superior to the Hebrew "whim" of JHWH.
- Reality is "Psyche to Soma": the universe is the body, and the Immanent Mind is the soul.
