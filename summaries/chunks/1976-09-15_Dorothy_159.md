# 1976-09-15_Dorothy_159
## Chunk Description
Analyzes the "Satanic Time Balloon," the role of the "Old Man," and the "Ape of God's" counterfeit world.

## Key Claims
- **Satanic Time Balloon:** Satan (The Artifact) generated a massive "spurious time balloon" to trick us into thinking 2000 years have passed; in reality, it is still C. A.D. 70.
- **St. Sophia as Repairman:** Sophia (the universe psyche) restores eternal life to the "cells" (humans) of the dying/insane part of the circuit board (our world).
- **The Two-Way Thrust:** "God became man so that man could become God"—this is an identity exchange where the "I" of the man becomes another "I".
- **The "Old Man" dies:** In salvation, what dies is the "old man" (personal history/accretions), replaced by the victorious Christ.
- **Invisibility of Rescue:** The penetration of the savior must remain invisible to the artifact, which is "pressured" by God to curb its own chaos.

## Entities
- **St. Sophia:** The Mediatoress between man and the absolute Creator.
- **The Artifact (Satan):** The creator of the spurious/counterfeit world.
- **Augustine/Patristic Fathers:** Fooled by the "spurious time balloon."
- **Pinky:** The "bait" who was secretly transfigured.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Spurious Time:** The "time run" spun out to make us stop watching for the 2nd coming.
- **Metastasis of Irreality:** The "cancer" of the isolated/autonomous part of the universe.
- **Syzygy:** The pairing of man and the divine (Jesus/Christ model).
- **Varying Decision Levels:** Lower level cause-and-effect vs. higher level sentient overrides.

## Theological Motifs
- **Victory as Hallmark:** Salvation results in "absolute joy" and victory over the ordeal.
- **Replacement of Victims:** God substitutes the real victim for his own voluntary sacrifice, over which Satan has no power.
- **Admixture Model:** True creation encroached upon by the "Ape of God's" malicious forgery.
