# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_05
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_05
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: The Two-Slit Experiment, Quantum Indeterminacy, Synthesis of Physics and Theology
- **Key Entities**: A.N. Whitehead, Wolfgang Pauli

## Key Claims
- Dick proposes his final, triumphant synthesis: the "binary forking" that he experienced as the basis of all reality is the macro-level equivalent of the "two-slit" phenomenon in quantum physics.
- The fundamental nature of reality is indeterminate. At every branching (the two slits), the sentient Mind of the universe steers or governs which slit is selected.
- This links subatomic physics (indeterminacy, Pauli's synchronicity) directly to A.N. Whitehead's theological definition of God as the "principle of selection of the good."
- Humans cannot normally see this selection process because the universe switches into a void/non-being state during the micro-instant the decision is made.
- Dick views this synthesis as finally resolving his seven years of vacillation between Christian theology, Gnosticism, Taoism, and physics.

## Entities
- **Wolfgang Pauli**: His principle of Synchronicity is invoked as a macro-level manifestation of this acausal selection process.
- **A.N. Whitehead**: Re-iterated as providing the exact definition that bridges quantum selection and divine action.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Two-Slit Phenomenon**: Used metaphorically and literally by Dick to explain how reality chooses its path at every juncture, proving determinism false.
- **The Synthesis**: Dick's grand unified theory bringing together Heideggerian *Sein*, Pauline mysticism, Taoist *Yin-Yang*, and Post-Newtonian quantum mechanics.

## Theological Motifs
- **God as Quantum Selector**: God is located in the space between causal events, determining outcomes by collapsing the wave-function/binary fork toward the optimal good.
