# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-135

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-135
- **Themes:** Mask vs. Real Psyche, The Subliminal Underground, The Phylogenic Facade, Eternal Rebirth.

## Key Claims
- **Mask Psyche (Facade):** Each member of the underground has an "Outer Mask Persona" (like "Phil Dick") that doesn't even know it's in the underground. The "Inner Real Psyche" (Thomas) is the actual member.
- **Thomas as the Member:** Thomas has been in the underground for years, communicating through the novels' "steganographic traffic." He is a "phylogenic self" dating back to Pentecost who is "eternally reborn."
- **A priori Underground Logic:** If one person (Thomas) can put code in a book without the "host" knowing, then other "Inner Psyches" can extract it without their "hosts" knowing.
- **Pluriforms of the Spirit:** The "Secret Church" members may be "Christs" or pluriforms of the Holy Spirit who escape each body at death and move to a new one (perpetuating the mind).
- **The Bait of Humanity:** Christ "took the death blow" (metaphysical death/G2 threat) in 3-74 to save the "host." He offered his humanity as bait to "hook" the powers of darkness.

## Entities
- **Thomas:** The "Phylogenic Self" / Real Psyche / Underground Member.
- **Phil Dick:** The "Ontogenic Facade" / Mask / Conventional Cover.
- **The Powers of Darkness:** The archons/G2 who were "hooked" by Christ's intervention.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Phylogenic vs. Ontogenic:** The eternal "Species-Self" vs. the fleeting "Individual-Self."
- **Steganographic Traffic:** Communication hidden from the "Mask Psyche" but visible to the "Real Psyche."
- **The Second Advent Preparation:** The secret church's activity over 2000 years to prepare for the "Assize."

## Theological Motifs
- **Adoption as Son:** The "host" (Phil) being adopted by the "Real Self" (Thomas/Christ) to be saved from the Fall.
- **Astral Determinism:** The "Karmic Debt" defeated by Christ's substitutionary death.
- **Restoration of Status:** Reverting from "Blindness" to the "Master Original State."
