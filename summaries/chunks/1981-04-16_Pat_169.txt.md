# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_169
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_169
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: Space that Remembers, Holographic Memory, The Apocalyptic Christ, The Ajna Eye
- **Key Entities**: Parmenides, Shiva, Brent Mini

## Key Claims
- In the "true cosmos," space as we know it does not exist; adjacency is based on conceptual/mathematical relation. Forms exist in a binary state of rest/motion. Reality is "space that remembers," analogous to a neural hologram where the whole is contained in every part.
- Re-evaluating his novel *VALIS*, Dick realizes his vision was not of the gentle Jesus, but of the fierce, apocalyptic Christ wielding the sword and power of YHWH as eschatological judge.
- This apocalyptic Christ is actively destroying the enemy (the Black Iron Prison / Satan) with the "sizzling St. Elmo's fire that kills." 
- The beam of pink light and the "ajna eye of Shiva" are synonymous: they represent divine Wisdom that simultaneously imparts absolute truth and incinerates wickedness with desiccating heat.

## Entities
- **Parmenides**: His assertion that whatever exists has always existed eliminates the concept of temporal change, leading to Dick's concept of a holographic universe ("space that remembers").
- **Shiva**: Equated with the apocalyptic Christ—the destroyer deity who annihilates the corrupted universe to cleanse it.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Third Eye / Ajna Eye**: Represents the dual function of the eschatological judge: total discernment (Wisdom) and absolute destruction (Slaying).

## Theological Motifs
- **The Warrior Christ**: A stark departure from the suffering servant metaphor, returning to the terrifying, commanding presence of YHWH encountered by Moses on Sinai and the Ancient of Days in Daniel.
