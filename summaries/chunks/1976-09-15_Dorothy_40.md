# The Hierarchy of Religion and the Final Stage
## concise_summary
Dick synthesizes Hegel's "three divisions of religion," placing himself and his 3-74 experience in the highest stage: "Absolute Religion" or Christianity, where the opposition between individuality and nature is overcome in the realization of the Absolute Spirit.
## key_claims
- 3 Divisions of Religion (Hegel):
    1. Religions of Nature (Sensual/Animal).
    2. Religions of Spiritual Individuality (Judaism/Greek/Roman).
    3. Absolute Religion (Christianity/Spirit).
- Realization of Spirit: Dick's experience of seeing Zebra "outside" and experiencing it "inside" is the Hegelian overcomes of the "I/Not-I" opposition.
- Evolutionary History: History is the "unfolding of Spirit." We have moved from biological evolution to the evolution of man within his social/historical context.
- Freedom as the Goal: The "progressive steps" of Spirit are taking us toward greater and greater human freedom.
- Whitehead vs. Traditional View: dick reaffirms that Whitehead's "Spirit of selection" is a better account of God than the traditional "Creator" model, as it accounts for the "novelty" and "pluralism" of the world.
- Conscious Selection: The fact that Zebra *talked* proves that the "selection" is not a blind force but a conscious, sentient mind (Macrocosm speaking to Microcosm).
- Incorporation into the Kingdom: The quest of early Christians was to be "incorporated into the Kingdom... while still alive," which 3-74 achieved for Dick.
