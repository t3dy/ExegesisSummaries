# Orthogonal Intervention and the Decoden
## concise_summary
Dick refines his theory of "orthogonal intervention," arguing that true change enters linear time "at right angles" from another dimension. He views his own "anamnesis" (memory restoration) as the key to breaking a "deliberate, purposeful delusion" that stretches 2000 years of "real" time into a fake history.
## key_claims
- Orthogonal Change: "True newness" cannot arise within a closed deterministic system. It must enter "orthogonally" from outside (Grace/Zebra).
- Expansion of the Present: 3-74 was an "expanded present" where Rome 70 AD was superimposed on Fullerton 1974. Rome is the "enduring form" within all frames.
- Wakefulness vs. Sleep: Humans are "sleepers" who fell at their post. The Savior (Runciter/Christ) wakes us up into our true state.
- The Decoden / Evil Plot: A "decoden" or "evil web spinner" created the delusion that a great deal of time has passed, ensuring we lose vigilance.
- Identification through Memories: You cannot "recognize" 70 AD unless you were there. Anamnesis proves that the "divine spark" (the soul) has been here before.
- The Altered Response: The "orthogonal intervention" manifested as an altered response to the Xerox letter—a "misfire" in the deterministic strip.
- Thomas as the Advocate: Dick concludes "Thomas" was not just a personality but the "Advocate" (Paraclete)—a non-deterministic agent sent to handle the crisis.
- Retrieval/Ubik Model: (1) Past layers lie within phenomena, (2) Info drifts up from substrata, (3) Info is meant to awaken us.
