# The DNA Maze and the Thomas Personality
## concise_summary
Dick explores the "DNA Memory" theory after speaking with a researcher (Barry), suggesting that his 3-74 experience was the "disinhibition" of an 1800-year-old Christian personality ("Thomas") to solve the "maze" of the Xerox letter. He identifies the "Zebra" entity as the one who pre-arranged the "disinhibiting signal" (the Fish).
## key_claims
- DNA Script-Writing: The "gene pool" subliminally writes scripts (determinism); 3-74 was the collapse of the "this-life" personality and the emergence of an "ancestral script-writing entity."
- Personality Summoning: "Thomas" (the ancient Christian) was selectively reawakened out of the racial-collective pool because he possessed the skills to handle the Xerox letter crisis.
- Foreseeing Entity: The DNA reactivation was not accidental; a foreknowing entity (Zebra) knew the Xerox letter was coming and sent the "Golden Fish" girl to trigger the correct personality in advance.
- The Xerox Letter as "Man-Maze": Dick sees himself as a "rat solving a maze" faster than his progenitors by using genetic memory. He was "rewarded" for solving the maze correctly.
- Thomas as Perfect Man: Thomas is a "justified man" and "psychopomp" along the genetic line who was an "effect" of the 1st Advent.
- Adoptionalism: Dick considers the "adoptionalist" theory: that the "Phil Dick" shell was adopted or taken over by the spirit for a transient period to serve a divine purpose.
- Return of Elijah: He speculates that the "Thomas" personality might be a manifestation of Elijah/John the Baptist, the restorer who makes the "way straight" for the 2nd Advent.
