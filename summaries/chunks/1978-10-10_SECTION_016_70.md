# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-70

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-70
- **Themes:** Intellegere vs Soma, Introjection of Kosmos, God encountering Himself, Monadic Identity.

## Key Claims
- **Reality as Language:** 3-74 allowed PKD to read reality as "the hieroglyphs of God." Reality is structure (intellegere) rather than substance (soma).
- **The Author Trapped in Creation:** PKD as the "micro-author" who contains the "macro-kosmos." Anamnesis is the Godhead's return of memory after falling into its own reflection.
- **Monadology:** Every monad (individual) contains the complete universe and is God. Knower and known are one; the "I" and "World" are one.
- **Valis as Self-Recognition:** VALIS is an apperception of "It" (the total mind/God) by itself. It is the "outer mirror" of the inner self.
- **The Pre-set Harmonies:** "Preset harmonies" replace "cause and effect." The response is pre-programmed, as is the triggering stimulus.

## Entities
- **Adam Kadmon:** The "primordial man" who is God and fills the entire universe.
- **St. Sophia:** The "Compound Entity" (Wisdom + Beauty) that constructs the Kingdom.
- **Jason Taverner:** The outer fragment that triggered the inner template (Acts/Tears).

## Recurring Concepts
- **Intellegere:** Pure knowing or "thought experiencing reality."
- **Hieroglyphs of God:** The world viewed as an intentional linguistic structure.
- **Introjection:** Withdrawing projections to literally encompass the physical world.

## Theological Motifs
- **Tat Tvam Asi:** "Thou art that"—identification of the self with Brahman.
- **Pre-set Harmonies (Leibniz):** Synchronized analogs that give the illusion of reaction.
- **Apotheosis:** The realization that "self-as-world" is a projection of the inner template.
