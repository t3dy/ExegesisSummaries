## Key Claims
- Dick observes that people who hallucinate are *compelled* to believe the hallucination is real, whereas people on psychedelics often know their visions are irreal. This proves that perception and cognitive assent can be separated.
- Because we cannot force ourselves to believe our own mental representations, God must be actively present within our cognitive systems to compel our absolute assent to the reality of the world. 
- He interprets his entire literary career up to *VALIS* as an exploration of what it is like to lack this divine guarantee (a universe without God compelling assent, leading to schizophrenic isolation).
- In 1974, for the first time in his life, Dick stopped fleeing reality out of fear. By turning to face the void (*das Nichts/Angst*), he achieved Heideggerian *Sein* (Being), allowing God to break through the lifelong cognitive occlusion.

## Entities
- **Descartes / Malebranche**: Their epistemological frameworks provide the only vocabulary capable of explaining Dick's specific philosophical dilemmas.
- **Martin Heidegger**: His concept of achieving authentic Being (*Sein*) by confronting anxiety (*Angst*) and death maps to Dick's psychological breakthrough in 1974.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The 10-Volume Meta-Novel**: Dick's collective term for his body of work exploring solipsism, fake worlds, and the terrifying breakdown of reality.
- **The Cartesian Doubt**: Dick pushes Descartes' extreme skepticism further, applying it to the mechanism of belief itself.

## Theological Motifs
- **God as the Guarantor of Sanity**: Without God constantly reinforcing the reality of the world-representation, the human mind would collapse into an inexplicable, horrifying dream-state.
