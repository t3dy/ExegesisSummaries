# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_141
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_141
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: God's Self-Awareness, The Universe as Information, The Cosmic Christ as Savior
- **Key Entities**: YHWH, Spinoza, Hans Jonas

## Key Claims
- Relying on the *Sepher Yezirah*, Dick observes that to God, "Idea, word + work are one + the same." Therefore, the universe is literally composed of the thoughts of the creator in verbal/informational form.
- The creator fell victim to his own creation, becoming imprisoned in his own thought-formations and forgetting his identity. The Cosmic Christ is a strategy employed by God to rescue Himself, awakening Him to His true identity (anamnesis) and causing Him to declare *Anokhi* ("I am").
- Karma and Heimarmene are the "retribution machinery" through which the creator's prior thought-formations return to enslave him (the Bardo Thodol experience).
- The "Maze" is a Chinese finger trap fed by the frantic thoughts of the creator trying to escape it.

## Entities
- **Christ (The Messenger)**: The strategy initiated by God before the fall to ultimately break into the closed loop and rescue the creator from his own creation.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Bardo Thodol**: The Tibetan concept of the afterlife where one's own prior thoughts return as independent realities; Dick equates this to the experience of being trapped in the Maze.

## Theological Motifs
- **Salvator Salvandus**: The Gnostic concept of the "saved savior"—the idea that God Himself is the one who ultimately needs rescuing from the material universe.
