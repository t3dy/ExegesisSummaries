# The New Type of Man and the Inner Light
## concise_summary
Dick identifies his experience with Hegel's third stage of religion (Absolute Spirit), arguing that evolution has moved from the biological to the historical. He predicts the emergence of a "new type of man" who is inner-directed and no longer needs external mediators (church or text).
## key_claims
- Evolution of History: Evolution is now historical rather than biological. 3-74 was a "mutant" leap forward in human consciousness.
- The New Man: A new type of human is emerging (Inner-Directed) who senses truth intuitively ("Inward Light") and bypasses all intermediaries, including the Bible.
- Overcoming the I/Not-I: Consistent with Hegel, Dick saw Zebra outside and felt it inside, overcoming the traditional split between observer and world.
- The Teleological Entity: Zebra is the entity that directed biological evolution and now directs history toward human freedom.
- Active Role vs. Passive Victim: Dick realized he was not a victim but an agent whose role completed a necessary historical event.
- Divine cause, Mundane mechanism: The underpinning of 3-74 was divine, but the events themselves (like his writing or decisions) involved mundane processes.
