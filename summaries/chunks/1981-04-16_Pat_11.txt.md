## Key Claims
- Dick recounts undergoing a spontaneous, involuntary reperformance of Christ's Passion during Holy Week of 1981.
- He maps his psychological and spiritual states to the Holy Week narrative: extreme mental exhaustion (Monday/Passion), receiving an extortion letter that brought the threat of death (Wednesday), and a subsequent breakthrough completing his "binary model" of reality (Resurrection).
- He views his entry into the "pure spatial realm" and the completion of his intellectual model as equivalent to spiritual resurrection—a new, joy-filled *Dasein* representing a net gain of energy in the system.
- This cycle corresponds to ancient springtime rites of cosmogenesis, wherein the old cycle wears out and a miraculous restoration of energy (a return to the Garden) occurs.

## Entities
- **Mircea Eliade**: Referenced regarding the "miraculous, supernatural" reperformance of mythic time and sacred rites.
- **Heidegger**: Mentioned again regarding "being-to-death" and the Christian attitude toward annihilation/nonbeing (*das Nichts*).

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Extortion Letter**: In 1981, Dick received a threatening/extortion letter that terrified him, which he maps to the "lethal" Xerox missive of 1974.
- **Phylogenic Renewal**: The return to the Garden is not just a personal (ontogenic) rebirth, but a restoration of the entire species' primordial faculties.

## Theological Motifs
- **Exhaustion as Crucifixion**: Dick experiences the burnout of his own ideas and thoughts as a literal spiritual/mental death preceding revelation.
