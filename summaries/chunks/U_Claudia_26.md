# Claudia
## concise_summary
Dick identifies the "Holy Spirit" as a collective immortal Light Body that re-entered him to stimulate his "Third Soul" (Aristotelian/immortal). He draws a parallel between his 1974 experience and the Protestant Reformation, arguing that both represent the birth of a "new modern type" of human who cannot be lied to by authoritarian systems.
## key_claims
- The "Holy Spirit" didn't leave in 100 AD; men simply fell away/blinded themselves.
- Blasphemy against the Holy Spirit is the denial of its secret presence and constant work of progression toward perfection.
- The "Third Soul" (Aristotle's intellectual soul) is stimulated by the "Light Being" through the 3rd eye to confer immortality.
- 3-74 was a "functional success": Dick's mechanism was designed for one job (defeating an evil force) and he did it.
- Reformation Parallel: Dick's experience of "Inner Truth" mirrors the first Protestants' break with Rome; he is a "new type" (Vancouver speech) who is "snow-proof."
- Spontaneous Generation of Values: The individual can now create their own value system (Digital/Logoic) instead of receiving it from the state.
