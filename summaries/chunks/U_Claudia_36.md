# Claudia
## concise_summary
Dick identifies his internal "guide" as St. Luke, "the beloved physician," who is a non-terrestrial entity of high authority. He reinterprets the ending of *Tears* as a ciphered "Acts of the Apostles" narrative and outlines a plot for *To Scare the Dead* involving the shamanic possession of a government agent by the "St. Luke" entity to save humanity from ecological holocaust.
## key_claims
- St. Luke the Physician: The toga-wearing Greek guide is St. Luke (evangelist, friend of Paul); he provides the "other-end-of-the-telescope" (off-earth) perspective.
- *Tears* as Code: The black man (Philip) and his "multiplication" are a code for the Ethiopian eunuch in *Acts* being made "fruitful" (*Felix*).
- Non-Terrestrial Savior: The "Holy Spirit" is actually a non-terrestrial entity (Luke) that occupies the "Right Hemisphere" of the host.
- Ecological Escape: The goal of the Parousia/Contact is "translation" (evacuation) of humans to save them from the upcoming planetary holster/pollution.
- SF vs Theology: Dick rejects a purely SF explanation (*Childhood's End* style) unless it harmonizes with theology without reducing it.
- Reverse Time: Dick identifies memories of the future (reverse time) as a key structural element of reality, harmonizing with Arthur C. Clarke.
