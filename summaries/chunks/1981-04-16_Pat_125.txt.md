## Metadata

- **Themes**: The Plasmate as Vine/Network, Steganography, The Two Coaxial Realms, Mercy vs. Justice
- **Key Entities**: Plato, Spinoza, Jesus, Paul

## Key Claims
- *Flow My Tears* was written by Thomas, to be read by the inhabitants of the *other* coaxial realm (Christ's Kingdom). The steganographic (hidden) story within the novel is specifically meant for the "secret Christians" operating there.
- Dick had a hypnagogic vision of a growing vascular network (like a vine or mycelium) penetrating our reality. He realizes this living information *is* the Plasmate, which *is* Christ (the True Vine/Corpus Christi).
- Dick states his life's monumental task: not just claiming that two coaxial worlds exist, but constructing a rational, theoretical metaphysics (akin to Plato's Theory of Forms) to explain *how* they can coexist in the same space-time.
- The fundamental difference between the two realms is their overarching law: the ostensible world operates on Lex Talionis (retributive justice/Karma/Torah), while Christ's Kingdom operates on Mercy and Agape.

## Entities
- **Spinoza**: Mentioned for failing to solve the conflict between causal law (Heimarmene) and divine intervention (Pronoia), choosing instead to deny the latter.
- **Plato**: His concept of *universalia ante rem* provides the closest philosophical scaffolding for Dick's theory of coaxiality.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Set/Ground Discrimination**: The perceptual act of separating the newer, highly complex, negentropic structure (Christ's Kingdom/Set) from the background noise of the older, lower-energy reality (The BIP/Ground).

## Theological Motifs
- **Information Metabolism**: The Plasmate/Christ processes information to maintain a higher state of negentropy (internal organization) than the surrounding fallen world, allowing it to actively grow and transubstantiate reality.
