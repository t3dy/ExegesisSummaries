# Claudia
## concise_summary
Dick identifies Christ’s purpose not as suffering, but as an “Ontological Alteration” of reality that demonstration immortality. He views the "Second Comforter" (St. Sophia) as the released, ubiquitous form of Christ that infuses the world to draw man back to union with God (the All).
## key_claims
- Christ’s suffering was just the price of being human; his real purpose was an "Ontological Alteration" of the world.
- Resurrection was a "Technical Demonstration" of immortality under adverse conditions (a guinea pig experiment).
- St. Sophia / Holy Spirit: Prior to the incarnation, she was limited to special saints; now she is ubiquitous and ranges within every soul.
- Immortality = returning to God (the All), not personal survival in "white robes."
- Transubstantiation of the World: God can instantly transform the "dead/dead" world into his living organic body for any man.
- Victory in Defeat: By being "defeated" (Crucified), Christ proved his divinity and the truth of the scriptures (Sacerdotal win).
