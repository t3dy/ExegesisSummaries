# We are Spools of God's Memory
## concise_summary
Dick uses Whitehead's and Leibniz's philosophy to argue that humans are "memory spools" or "perceiving cells" within the vast organism of God. Our primary purpose is "witnessing," allowing the total organism to "restore" and "remember" its various stages of growth.
## key_claims
- God as Arranger (Whitehead): Zebra is the Whiteheadian God who arranges becoming for good ends, even if those ends are invisible to us.
- The Universe as Organism: We are "subcolonies of monads" (cells) within a vast single organism that communicates via "colored lights" (phosphenes).
- Mirror to the All: Each individual monad (human) reflects a "holographic" image of the total organism.
- Witnessing as Purpose: Our prime function is to be "memory units" (spools) for God, recording reality so it can be retrieved or restored.
- Anamnesis as Playback: 3-74 was the "playback" of high-fidelity memories stored in the DNA, which effectively "restored" the historical event and identity.
- The Artist's Role: Artists (like Beethoven or Schubert) are high-fidelity recorders whose work bridges the gap to the "next world."
- Beauty in Reality: Beauty is an objective property of reality produced by God's "arranging," but we are often "asleep" or "blind" to it.
