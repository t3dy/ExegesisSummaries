# The Two-Person Board Game and the Tesla Anti-Christ
## concise_summary
Dick explores the "Absolute" through the lens of process philosophers like Bohme and Driesch, viewing history as a dialectic where God "makes himself." He describes a cosmic board game where a wise, benign power unerringly defeats a blind, dark counterplayer. He also identifies the "other universe" as a sentient mind (Logos) that communicates through informational "words," and warns that Soviet technology might be building a "Tesla grid" as an anti-christ mimicry of this Logos.
## key_claims
- History as Divine Self-Development: Evil may be an "earlier stage" of a God in process, which He is working to overcome.
- The Cosmic Game: PKD's 1974 vision revealed a game where the "Good" side wins every trick because it possesses absolute wisdom against a blind opponent.
- Logos as Information: The real universe is an electromagnetic flux or "mind" that impinges on our world via "words" (as seen in *Ubik*).
- Tesla Grid/Anti-Christ: The Soviets may be attempting to create a man-made ionospheric "Logos" or mind-control grid that mimics the true divine wisdom.
- Augmented Corpus Christi: A trans-temporal group mind that spans human history; PKD identifies as a "terminal" of this mind.
