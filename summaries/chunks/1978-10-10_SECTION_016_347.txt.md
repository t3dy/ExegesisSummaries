# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1978-10-10_SECTION_016_347
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1978-10-10_SECTION_016_347
- **Section ID**: SECTION_016
- **Themes**: Logical Parousia, Idios vs. Koinos Kosmos, Spurious Time, Relentless Reasoning, Permenides
- **Key Entities**: Malebranche, Christ, Parmenides, Heraclitus

## Key Claims
- The Parousia is not just a miracle but an "understood" outcome of a relentless logical sequence. One can "cause the Parousia by thinking about it."
- It is an exercise in logic that breaks the boundaries of the solipsistic self, transitioning from the *idios kosmos* (private world/dream) to the *koinos kosmos* (shared/divine reality).
- "Spurious time" exists in the *idios kosmos* as a programmed, pre-synchronized simulation (Malebranche's "intelligible archetypal ideas").
- Dick argues that his 2-3-74 experience was the breakdown of this simulation due to a "merciless appraisal of logic" over four decades.
- Logic must "drive the adjustments" of world-perception, wear down the simulation through sheer scrutiny, until it commits an error—revealing the "common essence" or the real creation.

## Entities
- **Malebranche**: Occasionalist whose theory of "intelligible archetypal ideas" Dick uses to model simulation.
- **Parmenides**: Representative of the conflict between a priori truth and sensory report (seeming).
- **Heraclitus**: Cited for the concept of sleepers in private worlds (*idios kosmoi*).

## Recurring Concepts
- **Idios Kosmos / Koinos Kosmos**: Private/simulated vs. shared/real worlds.
- **Spurious Time**: Programmed temporal passage in the simulation.
- **Meta-abstraction**: The sudden realization that collapses the simulation into reality.
- **Relentless Logic**: The cognitive "tool" Dick uses to "beat to death" the defensive adjustments of the world-picture.

## Theological Motifs
- **Parousia as Gestalt**: The identifying "Name" or recognition that emerges once the simulation's evasions are exhausted.
- **Divine Invasion**: The entry of actual creation into the simulated realm as a corrective.
- **Apocalyptic History**: The real "common essence" beneath the spurious world-process.
