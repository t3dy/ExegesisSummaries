## Key Claims
- Dick applies Platonic theory to his Christology: Christ is the eternal *Eidos* (Form), and individual believers (like Dick and Thomas) are the plural *particulars* instantiating that Form.
- He realizes he was not Thomas in a past life. Rather, both he and Thomas are particulars of the same Form. By overlapping, they provided the two data points necessary to "feature-extract" and abstract the pure Form (Christ).
- This explains why AD 70 and 1974 superimposed: he was perceiving the common theological essence shared by both time periods.
- Discovering this is not a mystical trance, but a supreme act of intellectual abstraction—the ability to look at physical reality and perceive the raw information/Logos underlying it (the "Anokhi" or pure consciousness).

## Entities
- **Beethoven / Schiller / Goethe**: Represent the height of the Western intellectual ability to abstract—converting the concrete into the universal, paving the way for the Apperception of the Logos.

## Recurring Concepts
- **One Thing Seen Twice**: The cognitive requirement to extract a Platonic Form. You must see the essence instantiated in at least two different particulars to abstract the Form itself.
- **Anokhi**: The highest stage of consciousness, achieving pure abstract perception where the world resolves into meaning/information.

## Theological Motifs
- **Christ as the Uttered Word**: Approaching Christ not just as a historical savior, but as the supreme metaphysical/epistemological category—the abstract Information formatting the universe.
