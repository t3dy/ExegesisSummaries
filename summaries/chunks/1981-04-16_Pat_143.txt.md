## Key Claims
- Christianity, like Buddhism, addresses the problem of suffering, but solves it through a unique miracle: it alchemically converts suffering into its 180-degree opposite—ecstasy. This was the authentic payoff of Dick's 1974 experience.
- The Christian via (path) demands the voluntary taking on of suffering (*Agape*) in imitation of Christ. Only by going to the deepest layer of suffering can it undergo *enantiodromia* into joy.
- The true Christian exists in the "tiny interval" between Christ's departure and His imminent return. The soul acts as the Bride, joyfully preparing for the wedding (hierogamy) with the Bridegroom.
- Birth in the Spirit provides direct knowledge of Christ and initiates this spiritual union before death.

## Entities
- **Dionysus-Zagreus**: equated with Jesus in the context of being the divine figure who converts suffering (the passion) into ecstatic joy.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Enantiodromia**: The psychological mechanism by which extreme suffering and grief necessarily reverse into their absolute opposite polarity (ecstatic joy). 

## Theological Motifs
- **Hierogamy**: The spiritual marriage between the human soul (the Bride) and God/Christ (the Bridegroom), consummated inwardly via the Holy Spirit.
