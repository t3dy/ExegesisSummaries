# The Cooked Material: Tears as a Supernormal Interpolation
## concise_summary
Dick argues that *Flow My Tears* contains "cooked" (divinely inspired/interpolated) material that parallels the Book of Acts with impossible precision. He concludes that Jesus is "here" in a state of amnesia within us, waiting to be "awoken" by these archetypal scenes.
## key_claims
- Scriptural Mirror: *Tears* reflects Acts 1:1. The "all-night gas station" scene mirrors Philip's baptism of the Ethiopian eunuch.
- Supernormal Interpolation: Dick believes an entity (Holy Wisdom/Sophia) "cooked" his novel by inserting these parallels while he was writing it in 1970–73.
- Christ is Amnesiac: The "Risen Christ" is present among us (the suffering/helpers) but is "self-unaware." He must be "awoken to his own identity in us."
- Thomas as Awakening: Dick's "Thomas" persona was a partial reawakening of Christ within him.
- Transubstantiation of Time: The 1974 world is a "spurious interpolation" (Satanic blur). Authentic reality is Rome AD 70.
- The 2 Tracks: (1) Left track: Acts/A. (2) Right track: 1974/B. We track both but usually only remember the right.
- St. Sophia's Birth: The final "Second Advent" will be the conscious birth of St. Sophia, who will openly destroy Satan's power.
