# The Trial of Choice and the Release from Karma
## concise_summary
Dick identifies his 1974 ordeal with the Xerox letter as a "Great Assize" or trial of choice. By choosing "correctly" (choosing the light), he believes he cancelled his "Karmic Burden" (Sin) and obtained "vertical ascent" (liberation) from the wheel of rebirth. He equates his 3-eyed visions with Lord Krishna.
## key_claims
- The Xerox Trial: The Xerox letter was a test of choice sent by "Satan."
- Cancellation of Karma: His "correct" response to the letter cancelled his lifelong karmic burden (expressed as fear of capture).
- Vertical Ascent: The I Ching (Hexagram 46) confirms he achieved "vertical ascent" (Moksha), leaving the wheel of rebirth.
- Memory as Reward: Maya lifted because he chose correctly; memory returned as a reward for service.
- Krishna/Orpheus: Equates his 3-eyed visions with Lord Krishna (8th incarnation of Vishnu), also a "dancer and flute player."
- Past Life Trauma: His "life-long fear" originated from a past life as a martyred Christian in Rome. 3-74 broke this "astral determinism."
- God's Script vs. My Script: God's will (to write) defeated Dick's own subconscious plan for martyrdom.
- The Judge: He saw his name written in the "great ledger" and felt judged and released.
