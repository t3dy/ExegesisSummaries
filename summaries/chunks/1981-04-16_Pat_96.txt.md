## Key Claims
- Dick reflects on his dual confusion regarding 1974: he was confused by *how* he was perceiving (the machinery of the twin psyches) and *what* he was perceiving (Valis/the Plasmate).
- He stresses that secular psychologists (like Ornstein) and philosophers (like Plato) miss the ultimate point: the goal isn't just to see reality accurately; the goal is the ontological transformation of man into God. 
- He refines the definition of "Ditheon": using a Greek dictionary, he realizes "di" means double/twofold (not sundered) and "theon" means "according to God" or "in the likeness of God."
- By superimposing the "A.D. 70" world and the "1974" world into a single unified field, the twin psyches perform a "radical new kind of feature extraction." They discard subjective illusions and isolate the absolute, objective reality (Valis).

## Entities

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Twin Psyches of *A Scanner Darkly***: Dick realizes Bob Arctor/Fred was an example of "species evolution gone wrong"—antithetical psyches destroying the host rather than integrating into a Ditheon.

## Theological Motifs
