# Warrior of Light: The Infiltrator Mission
## concise_summary
Dick embraces a Zoroastrian/Essene dualism where he is a "warrior of light" or "infiltrator-agent" from the supralunar world. He views his novels as a "disinhibiting signal" and argues that God must remain hidden (Deus Absconditus) to catch the "fallen angels" in the act of being themselves.
## key_claims
- We are Infiltrators: Christ and his "agents" (including Dick/Thomas) are from the supralunar world, sent to earth as "sleepers" to dismantle the prison (BIP).
- Mission Activation: The "Golden Fish" was a disinhibiting signal that triggered recollection of Dick's true identity and "destruction mission."
- Detection of Evil: God allows evil to flourish unhindered so the "fallen angels" (true ontological evil) can be detected and judged via their actions.
- Deus Absconditus: Global theophanies would "tip off" the evil ones, causing them to hide. They must be allowed to "act out their natures" in ignorance.
- The Prison City (Babylon): The Black Iron Prison manifests in every age (Rome, Nixon's DC) as the instrument of the adversary.
- The Battle outside Time: 3-74 was a head-on confrontation between the "Garden" and the "Prison" occurring outside of linear time.
- The Poor in the Kingdom: Christ didn't end linear time for everyone; he provided the *experience* of it ending for those he "lifted out."
- "Thomas" as the Form: Dick is a "temporal manifestation of a form" (Thomas) that has printed out before and will again.
