# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_95
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_95
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: Cartesian Proof of Existence, The Unused Evolutionary Potential, "Thinking" as Preceding the "Thinker"
- **Key Entities**: René Descartes, Plato

## Key Claims
- Dick questions whether the adventitious psyche (Thomas) actually existed as an independent spirit, or if the "logical operation" (the meta-abstraction) actually *created* the second psyche.
- He proposes an inversion of Descartes' *Cogito ergo sum*: "I think, and by thinking I am caused to be." The act of performing the meta-abstraction generates the secondary self in the right hemisphere.
- Up until the meta-abstraction occurs, the second psyche (the right hemisphere's consciousness) is merely a biologically latent potential. Once activated, the person ceases to be human and becomes a two-souled God-entity (Ditheon).
- This is a massive evolutionary quantum leap: the two-souled human is not human at all, just as a thinking animal is no longer an animal but a man.

## Entities
- **René Descartes**: Dick reinterprets the famous maxim to mean that the cognitive act creates the existential reality of the observer.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Epiphenomenal World**: The physical world is only an illusion cast to hide the underlying structure of reality. Once Ditheon is achieved, the structure becomes visible.

## Theological Motifs
- **Birth in the Spirit**: Dick equates his biological/neurological theory of hemispheric parity directly with the Christian doctrine of being "born from above." The theological phrasing describes the activation of the second self.
