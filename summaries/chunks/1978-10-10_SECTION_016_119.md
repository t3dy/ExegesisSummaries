# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-119

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-119
- **Themes:** The Ur-Story of the Deposed King, Hamlet/Bacchae/Gospel Parallel, Ritual Doom of the Usurper, Second Advent.

## Key Claims
- **The Four Print-outs:** *Hamlet*, *The Bacchae*, the *Gospels*, and *Flow My Tears* are all "print-outs" of the same primordial DNA story: The Murdered King and the Secret Revolt.
- **Dionysos vs. Pentheus:** The "King of Joy" (Young King) vs. the "King of Tears" (Old Usurper). 3-74 was the "actual, historic re-enactment" of this ritual outside time.
- **1974 is the Second Advent:** The deposing of the tyrant (Nixon/Empire) in 1974 represents the *completion* of the story that failed in the 1st Century.
- **The King must die:** The "Old King" is a usurper whose marriage to his "sister" (incest) brings down doom. The "Young King" is the instrument of this doom.
- **Crucifixion as Legitimization:** The cross/humiliation is the proof of the God's identity (like pulling the sword from the stone). Having "died," he returns as judge and Lord.

## Entities
- **Taverner:** The "Young King" archetype in *Tears*.
- **Buckman:** The "Old King / King of Tears" (the usurper).
- **The White-Haired Father:** The supreme God (Abba) who hears the stranger's call.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Ritual Re-enactment:** Performing mythic actions on a historic stage without conscious ideation.
- **Dream Time:** The temporal domain outside of linear history where the "pageant" occurs.
- **Secret Xtians:** Citizens of the hidden civilization acting as "underground" revolutionaries.

## Theological Motifs
- **Dionysos-Christ:** The fusion of the "God of Intoxication/Joy" and the "God of Love/Resurrection."
- **Transfigured Victim:** The stranger who is victimized by the tyrant but turns into an "instrument of doom."
- **Second Chance:** 1974 as the "second chance" for the story to end with the tyrant deposed rather than the God crucified.
