## Key Claims
- *VALIS* intentionally traps existentialist readers: it accurately portrays the realistic "thrownness" (Verfallenheit) of the human condition, but then offers an absurd, transmundane, Gnostic solution that deeply offends their nihilism.
- Dick realizes that *VALIS* was written backward: he knew the solution (soteriological rescue) firsthand, and constructed the problem of Fat's madness to account for it.
- Tagore is identified as the ultimate Gnostic/Manichaean entity: Jesus patibilis, the suffering form of Primal Man, who pervades the world and suffers our sins until forced to withdraw.
- The hyper-field AB is the *mechanistic means* by which two 180° coaxial mirror worlds can simultaneously co-exist; the Gnostic covert world is carried as a secondary signal on the same sine wave as the official world.

## Entities
- **Galbreath**: Literary critic whose analysis of *VALIS* forces Dick to clarify that the book stops short of preaching its Gnosticism as absolute truth due to Dick's own hesitation.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Jesus Patibilis**: The Manichaean concept of the divine light scattered into darkness, suffering patiently across all creation until unmixed.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Gnostic Truth as a Sine Wave**: The official world (A) and the Gnostic inversion (B) operate as equal and opposite phases of reality's fundamental sine wave, ensuring stasis and parity.
