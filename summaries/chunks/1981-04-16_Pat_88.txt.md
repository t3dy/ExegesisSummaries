# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_88
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_88
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: The Evolution of God, The Atman as Trans-corporeal Mind, Religious Anarchism, The Intelligible World
- **Key Entities**: Meister Eckhart, Philo, Paul the Apostle

## Key Claims
- The three stages (Torah, Christ, Spirit) represent the evolution of the unitary God as He interacts with humanity. Each stage is revolutionary but logically evolves from the previous one.
- The Torah was sublime in its own context but became ossified. Dick predicts the New Testament will eventually be viewed the same way Paul viewed the Torah: good, but superseded by the direct, individualized Spirit, justifying his "religious anarchism."
- The "Plasmate" (Holy Spirit) acts as a deciphering lens, revealing the abstract, pre-existent ideas in God's intellect (Philo's Logos) that underlie physical reality.
- The mind that recombines the metamorphemes isn't an external deity, but the individual's *Atman*—a trans-corporeal, ubiquitous world-psyche. 

## Entities
- **Philo**: Cited for the concept of the Logos as the "idea of ideas" structuring the intelligible realm.
- **Meister Eckhart**: The concept of the unchanging "Godhead" (Urgrund) that underlies the evolving three stages.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Auto-Generated Karma**: Because the Atman is both the individual and the universe, an individual's karma is actually their own judgment upon themselves. The information seems to arrive *ex nihilo* but is actually generated by the self outside of time.
- **Temporal Sucking/Tug**: The perfected Omega Point (Christ/Valis) acts like Aristotle's Prime Mover, functioning retroactively to pull all of creation toward itself. The closer history gets to this point, the faster time accelerates.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Self-Creating Universe**: God / The Universe is an entity whose blood is living information, constantly completing itself through organizational flux.
