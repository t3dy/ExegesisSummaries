# Claudia
## concise_summary
Dick explores the "Daimon of Socrates" (verified by Rollo May) as the source of his 1974 "Voice" and Attic Greek. He identifies this entity as "Eros" (Life/Joy) and concludes that he was "possessed" by a superior Will that replaced his own "learned helplessness."
## key_claims
- The "Voice" that spoke Attic Greek is the "Daimon of Socrates" (Eros), a mediator between mortality and immortality.
- This "Daimon" is real, creative, knows the future, and acts as a "gadfly" against the state (Social Vanguard).
- Dick recovered the "Highest Greek Worldview" (Athenian philosophers) utterly in 3-74.
- "Knowing" (Eros) is superior to "Thinking" (Logic); the Daimon knows everything from the "inside out."
- Each person has an immortal "Third Soul" (Pure Thought) that the Daimon "retains" and carries into the collective demiurge.
- Social forms (Athens/US) are destroyed by the Daimon to build values for the future.
