# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1978-10-10_SECTION_016_355
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1978-10-10_SECTION_016_355
- **Section ID**: SECTION_016
- **Themes**: Translating Binary to World, Plasmate, Irrationality vs Reason, Dionysos vs Apollo, The Paradox of *VALIS*
- **Key Entities**: Dionysos, Apollo (Sophia/VALIS), Horselover Fat, Christ, Mick Jagger

## Key Claims
- Reality fundamentally processes as 0-1 (rest-motion / yin-yang / enantiodromia).
- The human mind functions as a biocomputer: the "linking-relinking wheels" within the mind convert binary (0-1) quantitative information into words (quality), which are then "hypostatized" into 3-D objects.
- Dick believes VALIS was the portion of his own mind responsible for translating binary codes/words into objects within the space-time matrix. He briefly "caught sight" of this underlying processing level rather than the final illusion.
- In writing *VALIS*, Dick paradoxically stigmatized his own religious visions: any god who takes over a mortal by "enthusiasmos" is Dionysos (madness).
- Since Horselover Fat claims possession by a god, Dionysos is effectively the narrator of *VALIS*. Only this "mad god" possesses the capacity to perceive the invading "rational god" (Sophia/Apollo).
- Therefore, *VALIS* deplores the very madness that makes the perception of the saving Logos possible.

## Entities
- **Dionysos**: The god of irrationality, enthusiasm, madness, the counterculture, Mick Jagger, suicide. 
- **Apollo / Sophia / VALIS**: The antitoxin, the rational god who invades and "sobers the landscape."
- **Horselover Fat**: Functionally identical to the mad god Dionysos serving as the narrator warning against his own madness.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Linking-Relinking Wheels**: The processing sectors of the human biocomputer that translate digital data into the qualitative words that become empirical reality.
- **The Plasmate / Living Information**: Information that can think; words structuring reality before they are hypostatized.
- **Enthusiasmos**: Possession by Dionysos.

## Theological Motifs
- **Madness vs Wisdom**: The dualistic battle between the Dionysian forces governing the physical world and the Apollonian forces (VALIS/Sophia) undertaking a cosmic rescue mission.
- **The Cresting Wave / Circe's Swine**: A biblical metaphor (Matthew 8) Dick uses to compare the irrational mass movements destroying themselves versus those protected by the Logos.
