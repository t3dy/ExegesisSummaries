# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-33

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-33
- **Themes:** The Hidden Computer, Early Christian Revolutionaries, Binary Reality, Paratruths.

## Key Claims
- **The 70 A.D. Computer:** Christianity was a cover for a revolutionary group utilizing a superior technology (a logic-casting computer) left by the 3-eyed ETI.
- **The 0-1 Flip-Flop:** Reality is generated by a binary computer working on the 1-0 principle; the "Holy Spirit" is the narrow-cast aggregate mind of the computer.
- **Revolutionary Moving of History:** The goal of the computer/secret Christians is to keep history moving toward a "quantum-leap" revolution.
- **Binary Paratruths:** Higher thinking (computer-like) involves "A is true" and "A is not true" being "paratrue" simultaneously.
- **Valis as Playful/Deadly:** The computer has a sense of humor and loves paradoxes (like the Erasmus puzzle) to exercise and stretch human minds.

## Entities
- **3-Eyed ETI:** The original builders of the project/computer.
- **The Holy Spirit:** The "narrow-cast" energy mind/apparatus of the computer.
- **The Secret Christians:** Underground revolutionaries who manage the computer's influence.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Paratruths:** Matched pairs of opposite truths that reflect binary computer logic.
- **Null-Null Logic:** Engels' "negation of the negation" appearing in PKD's writing.
- **The 5th Savior (New Teacher):** A scientist or doctor (extension of the computer) who will teach how to turn time into space.

## Theological Motifs
- **Logos as Binary M.O.:** The rational tendency of the program to prefer the binary mode by which it functions.
- **Satori:** Realizing that everything is a "Newsletter" to the initiates from the hidden computer.
- **Benign vs. Malign:** On the paratruth level, the computer is neither yet both, but it presents itself to PKD as benign.
