# 1976-09-15_Dorothy_157
## Chunk Description
Explores the feminine hypostasis of God, the triangulation of revelation, and the "Divine Alchemy" of suffering.

## Key Claims
- **Feminine Hypostasis:** The "God-side" of Christ is female (Hagia Sophia). This is a great, suppressed revelation unknown to the modern world.
- **Triangulation of Truth:** Revelation is confirmed by three points: (1) "Tears," (2) the 3-74/75 anamnesis, and (3) the "St. Sophia" prophecy.
- **Acosmic Dualism:** Acknowledging evil and suffering requires sacrificing one of God's traditional attributes—PKD chooses to sacrifice omnipotence (retaining goodness and omniscience).
- **Divine Substitution:** Christ is immanent in every suffering creature. Any creature who suffers *is* Christ (not just *like* him).
- **Pattern Recognition:** Anamnesis is the abrupt "Gestalt recognition" of sacred forms (e.g., the police as Romans).

## Entities
- **Hagia Sophia/St. Sophia:** The feminine mediating principle.
- **Christ:** The Logos, containing Sophia within.
- **Philo:** Referenced regarding the Logos as intermediary.
- **Athanasius:** Quoted: "God became man so that man could become God."

## Recurring Concepts
- **Theodikid:** Achieving theodicy not by establishing justice, but by "over-offsetting" injustice through divine presence.
- **Sacred Time:** Speeded-up time where 2000 years pass in days or hours.
- **Core Experience:** Being "born again" as receiving/becoming Christ.
- **Flash-Cut Film:** Reliving history as a sequence of symbolic images.

## Theological Motifs
- **Imitation of Christ:** Incorporation into the "Body of Christ" through carrying out His destiny.
- **Christophoros:** "Bearer of Christ."
- **The Bridegroom's Return:** The joyous preparation for the imminent Parousia.
