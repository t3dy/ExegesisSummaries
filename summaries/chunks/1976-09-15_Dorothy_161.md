# Chunk 161 Summary - 1976-09-15_Dorothy_161

## Description
This chunk investigates the nature of divine intervention and the failure of secular philosophy to account for a living God who acts in history. PKD explores the "occlusion" of human faculties as a result of an "evil virus" and posits the universe as a sentient organism (Noös/Sophia) currently undergoing a process of "rebirth."

## Key Claims
- **Failure of Secular Thought**: Philosophers like Will Durant fail to understand that the purpose of religion is contact with a God who *intervenes* and changes fate, not just one who explains suffering.
- **Vicarious Atonement as Shield**: Christ enters a "syzygy" with the individual to absorb the "crisis blow" (nemesis) that fate would otherwise deliver.
- **The Evil Virus**: Human occlusion is a deliberate impairment by an "evil virus" (linked to the God of the Old Testament) that cuts us off from perceiving the divine.
- **St. Sophia as World Soul**: If the universe is a living soma/psyche, St. Sophia is the mediatress/soul currently "persuading" the irrational/chaos elements into a rational order.

## Entities
- **Will Durant**: Representative of secular/intellectualized theology.
- **Zebra**: The divine intruder/Logos imparting sentience to the inanimate.
- **St. Sophia**: The feminine World Soul; the "holy Wisdom" of Proverbs 8.
- **Xenophanes / Parmenides**: Pre-Socratic sources for the universe as a thinking totality.
- **Cornford**: Scholar cited regarding the "irrational" elements within the world soul.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Syzygy**: Union between the human and divine.
- **Noös**: Divine Mind or Reason.
- **Panpsychism / Hylozoism**: The belief that all matter is alive/sentient.
- **The Fall**: Interpreted as the World Soul falling into irrationality or "sleep."

## Theological Motifs
- **Divine Intervention**: God as an active shaper of history and personal life path.
- **Reawakening of Brahman**: The universe as a "sleeping" entity that is beginning to wake up and communicate.
- **Mediatress**: The role of Sophia/Logos as the bridge between man and a transcendent God.
