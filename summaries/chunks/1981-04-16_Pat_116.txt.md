# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_116
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_116
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: The Heroic vs The Tragic, Conquering Fate/Heimarmene, Wisdom's Ruse
- **Key Entities**: Beethoven, Wagner, Oedipus, Hitler, Hagia Sophia

## Key Claims
- Dick contrasts the Antique Tragic (succumbing to fate/heimarmene) with the Modern Heroic (seizing your death and making it your own). Seizing fate transforms destiny from your master into your artifact.
- Christ's vicarious atonement was not a passive defeat; it was a trick or ruse devised by Holy Wisdom (Hagia Sophia) to ensnare the demonic powers. The sacrificial lamb was bait swallowed by evil.
- The "Heroic" entered the world meekly as this sacrificial Lamb, successfully tricking the archaic powers of fate.
- By surviving 2-3-74 and writing *VALIS*, Dick believes he achieved his own heroic victory over death, devouring his fate as "Zagreus ate the Titans."

## Entities
- **Hitler**: Cited as the perfect example of the archaic tragic figure who willed his own grand defeat (Gotterdammerung) because he remained fundamentally enslaved to fate.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Parity of Defeat and Victory**: In the 180° reversed world, ostensible grand tragedy (the Empire's power) is actually cosmic defeat, while meek, invisible sacrifice is ultimate heroic victory.

## Theological Motifs
- **Manichaean Soteriology**: The Light tricked the Darkness into swallowing it; the sacrificed Light pacified and poisoned the Darkness from within, ensuring ultimate victory.
