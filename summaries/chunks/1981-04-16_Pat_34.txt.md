# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_34
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_34
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: The Third Age of the Spirit, Absolute Correspondences between Bible and World, The Abolition of Spatio-temporal Concreteness
- **Key Entities**: Joachim of Fiore

## Key Claims
- Drawing on Joachim of Fiore, Dick outlines three stages of consciousness: 
  1) The Age of the Law (Babylonian/schizophrenic concreteness).
  2) The Age of the Son (Normal abstracting, grace replacing legalism).
  3) The Age of the Spirit (Pure consciousness/freedom where the Bible and World become identical).
- In the Third Age of the Spirit, the dichotomy between the Bible as information and the World as physical reality vanishes. The World is read as Scripture, and the Bible is experienced as the current World.
- If one were to write an accurate ontological description of the modern world as it truly is, it would exactly match the text of the Bible (explaining why the Book of Acts superimposed itself over 1974 California).
- The Holy Spirit is the active agent that forces this absolute correspondence, unifying the inner (intelligible) and outer (sensible) realms into a single narrative.

## Entities
- **Joachim of Fiore**: His medieval prophecy of the "Three Ages" provides the framework for Dick's understanding of human spiritual evolution.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Record Groove to Music**: Dick uses this metaphor to explain the leap from experiencing the physical spatio-temporal world (the groove) to experiencing its underling meaning/Logos (the music).
- **Anokhi**: The Hebrew word for "I am," used here to denote the state of pure, absolute consciousness and freedom in the Age of the Spirit.

## Theological Motifs
- **World as Book, Book as World**: The ultimate Christian conceptual synthesis where the phenomenal world is revealed to be pure information (the Logos) constructed from the same eternal archetypes as the Bible.
