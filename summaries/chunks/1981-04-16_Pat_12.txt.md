# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_12
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_12
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: Independent Discovery of Process Theology, The Epistemology of the Zero Phase, Trial and Error Deity
- **Key Entities**: A.N. Whitehead, Charles Hartshorne, Xenophanes, Jakob Boehme

## Key Claims
- Dick asserts that while his model perfectly mirrors A.N. Whitehead's "process deity," he arrived at the exact mechanics of the binary switching system independently, based purely on his 1974 encounter with VALIS.
- He realizes this model solves his lifelong philosophical search: it successfully abolishes causality and replaces it with divine, serial decision-making.
- A radical implication of his binary model is that God is omnipotent but *not omniscient*. God must tentatively test a "false" (zero) move to evaluate it before cancelling it and selecting the superior "one" move.
- God does not have "thoughts" or "words" like a human; God thinks via *arrangements* and patterns (nonverbal language). VALIS operates as an existential deity—it *is* what it *does*, and what it does is perpetually choose.

## Entities
- **A.N. Whitehead / Charles Hartshorne**: Process theologians whose definitions of God match Dick's vision.
- **Xenophanes**: Quoted to support the view that God is radically non-anthropomorphic and does not think as humans do.
- **Jakob Boehme**: Credited as the only other thinker to propose a God that utilizes false/negative stages to achieve higher development.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The "Off" Phase**: The micro-instant of nonbeing during which the system evaluates the provisional track and makes its decision.
- **Anamnesis as Survival**: Dick realizes that remembering the discarded zero phase is what allowed him (and the alternate personality, Thomas) to survive a timeline that normally would have led to his death.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Existential Deity**: God's essence is identical to the act of perpetual, branching choice.
