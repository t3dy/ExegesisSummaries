## Key Claims
- Dick maps his experience perfectly onto the orthodox Christological formula: "Two Natures, One Person." He concludes that "Thomas" was the adventitious human psyche that combined with his own innate psyche.
- Overturning his earlier reincarnation theories, he realizes he was never Thomas in a past life. Instead, Thomas (a literal 1st-century persecuted Christian) invaded his mind, combining with him to form a singular Christ-entity.
- He reasons that this requires a literal, unbroken line of apostolic succession—a sequence of true Christian psyches dying and returning to merge with living hosts, perpetually recreating the Ditheon across history.
- The resurrection of Jesus was simply the paradigmatic first instance of this; Jesus returned to his disciples as the Paraclete, launching the chain reaction that continues through history to individuals like Dick.
