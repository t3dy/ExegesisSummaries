# The Network of Impulse and the Hobart Effect
## concise_summary
Dick theorizes that the return of St. Sophia manifests as a "network" of individuals acting on divine impulse. He connects the Parousia to a "Time Reversal" (the Hobart Effect) where entropic time becomes accumulative, and interprets the Xerox letter as a "signal" within this vast cosmic network.
## key_claims
- The Network of Sophia: St. Sophia is not born as a physical infant but as an "internal rebirth" across a concerted network of people.
- Divine Impulse: People act "on impulse" (e.g., writing the Xerox letter) to fulfill ends they cannot see.
- Initial Trigger: The "girl with the gold fish necklace" was the actual "disinhibiting" first step for his anamnesis.
- VALIS as "Early Christian": VALIS is identified with the authentic, Hellanized/Iranian/Hebrew "Sons of Light."
- The Time of Reversal: Connects the Parousia to a "Time of Reversal" where the dead rise (The Hobart Effect from *Ubik*).
- System Dialectic: A two-opposed system (dialectic) where "bad refines good" and energy sum remains constant.
- Idios vs. Koinos: Salvation is leaving the private world (*idios*) for the shared world (*koinos* / Noosphere).
