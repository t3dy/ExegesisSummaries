# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_104
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_104
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: The Threat of God's Abandonment, The Parousia Dream, Hypostasis, The 85 Letters
- **Key Entities**: Horselover Fat, Ed Meskys, Robert Anton Wilson

## Key Claims
- The ultimate doom facing humanity is not that God will punish us, but that our continuous cruelty will force God to *abandon* us. Because God is the sustainer of life, His withdrawal from the world means absolute physical doom.
- Dick interprets his March 1981 "Parousia dream" (the wicked rushing off a cliff while the elect are blocked) as a Gnostic separation of the Sparks. God is identifying the true Christians and stopping them from joining the general evil.
- Dick views Tagore not just as an external savior, but as a hypostasis of himself (like Horselover Fat or Thomas)—the highest spiritual incarnation of his own psyche that speaks for the ecosphere.
- He reflects on mailing 85 packets of "secret" Exegesis material to specific individuals (like Ed Meskys and Robert Anton Wilson), realizing he did it in a dissociated state as the "PKD" personality from 1974 to alert the Christian Underground about the revolution.

## Entities
- **Robert Anton Wilson**: Noted with shock as one of the recipients of his 85 letters, proving to Dick that an unconscious "mastermind" (the 1974 self) was guiding the mailing.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Empire vs. The Revolution**: The true goal of Christ (the master revolutionary) is to overthrow the forces of darkness (the Empire) that are toxifying the ecosphere.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Two Sides of God**: A tension between Christ's voluntary, infinite capability to endure suffering (Love) and the ultimate limit after which God will execute the "Day of YHWH" (Wrath/Abandonment).
