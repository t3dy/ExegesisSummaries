# Claudia
## concise_summary
Dick plots his next novel (*To Scare the Dead*), revolving around the "Possession" of a government agent (Nicholas Brady) by an ancient Essene spirit. He defines "Christian Anamnesis" as the "Remembrance of the Real Presence" and concludes that the "Parousia" is beginning as a secret, individual event.
## key_claims
- *To Scare the Dead* Plot: A bishop (Pike-based) dies while trying to receive an Essene mind; a government agent (Brady) inherits the "Experience" instead.
- "Possession" in the novel is a "Resurrection" to restore lost, secret arcane truths about Jesus.
- Christian Anamnesis = Remembrance facilitated by the Holy Spirit (John 14:26) which allows one to *see* the transubstantiation.
- The 1974 experience was "Anamnesis" out of nothing: the Holy Spirit teaching what was truly forgotten.
- Rome "won" 2,000 years ago because the Word/Wisdom was rejected ("Not acceptable").
- The Second Advent is another "try" by God's Wisdom (St. Sophia) to be accepted by man.
