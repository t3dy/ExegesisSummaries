## Key Claims
- Dick outlines an evolutionary axis of human emancipation: from the deterministic Babylonian-Greek cosmos, to the rebellious Gnostic worldview, leading finally to the radical autonomy he achieved in 1974.
- In this ultimate, unfallen state, the roles of God, Man, and World revert to their proper order: God provides informational advice, Man acts as the empowered creator/regent, and World is the construct shaped by Man.
- The Fall occurred when World slipped between Man and God, turning Man into the created object subjected to the physical world, rather than acting as creator of it.
- By extracting the divine "Set" out of the Mundane "Ground" via the meta-abstraction, Dick successfully bypassed the world and re-established direct contact with God.

## Entities
- **Hans Jonas**: Cited for his analysis distinguishing the classical Greek cosmos from the Gnostic view of radical alienation, validating Dick's evolutionary model.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The True Gnosis**: The realization that humanity was originally meant to act as the Logos—the creative intermediary between God and the material world.

## Theological Motifs
- **Man as Pantocrator**: The radical assertion that in the restored, unfallen state, humanity governs creation as God's regent, relying on God's "information" for advice rather than taking orders from deterministic laws.
