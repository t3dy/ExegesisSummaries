# The Stealth Strategy of the Warrior-Urgrund
## concise_summary
Dick rejects the idea that suffering has a divine purpose, calling it a "trap" by the artifact. He posits that the Urgrund's power is explicitly opposed to suffering, but its intervention is currently limited by a "stealth" strategy. To avoid a massive retaliation from the powerful artifact, the Urgrund operates undercover, focusing its "one arrow" at the source of tyranny rather than individual ameliorations.
## key_claims
- No Redemptive Value in Pain: Finding a "good purpose" in suffering is a psychological victory for the artifact.
- Stealth Invasion: The Urgrund uses stealth because a direct show of mass power would trigger the artifact's maximum cruelty.
- Source-Targeting: Like a warrior, the Urgrund aims at the "Quelle" (Source) of evil. The fall of Nixon in 1974 was a successful hit on a major center of tyranny.
- The Flower and the Burning Building: The Urgrund may "abandon the flower" (individual suffering) to focus on the "greater picture" (extinguishing the source of the fire).
- Intellectual Error: The Gnostic belief that the "Fall" was an intellectual error—confusing the phenomenal world for the real—dovetails with the counterfeit projection theory.
