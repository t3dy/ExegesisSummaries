# Self-Research / Notes
## concise_summary
Dick rejects the common Christian emphasis on suffering, arguing that Christ's purpose was to alter the ontology of the world. He discovers a "steganographic" message—the word **"Felix"**—embedded in the "die" command of his *Tears* dream, identifying the King on horseback as God and signaling a secret "live and prosper" message for the elect.
## key_claims
- Purpose of Christ: Christ did not come to suffer *for* us; his suffering was merely the price of entry. His true work was a cosmic "alteration of ontology" that remains invisible.
- Steganography in Vision: The "die" message in the *Flow My Tears* dream contains a hidden Logos-word "Felix" (Happy/Favored). This identifies the King on horseback as God.
- Esoteric vs. Exoteric: There is an exoteric message ("die") for the evil ones and an esoteric message ("live") for the flock of Christ.
- Secret Christian Underground: Suggests a "remnant of God" exists on Earth capable of reading these hidden signs (anamnesis).
- Logos as Artificer: "The workman is invisible within the workshop." The entry of the artificer into the artifact.
- Victory through Defeat: God achieves victory through apparent defeat (Crucifixion), resolving a two-player contest with an adversary of "iron."
