# Claudia
## concise_summary
Dick identifies the "Divine Birth" in his soul as the realization of Meister Eckhart's theology, where the "Wise Old Man" (the Father) is born from the mortal man. He rejects the "Suffering Servant" model for a "Living/Angry God" (Jehovah of Armies) and proposes that 3-74 was a "pay-off" for a lifetime of growth, not an external intervention.
## key_claims
- Divine Birth: God is born in the soul (Eckhart); man gives birth to the immortal God (the Father), who then reaches back in time to create man.
- Rejection of Sacrifice: Christ was not a sacrifice but a "First Immortal Man" showing the path; we follow the model, not a substitute.
- The Angry God: The god born in Dick's soul is "dangerous to His adversary" (Jehova of Armies), ready for battle, not a victim.
- Pay-off vs. Difficulty: 3-74 was the "pay-off" of a lifetime of spiritual growth; the previous years were the "Long Dark Night" in the tomb.
- Informational Homeostasis: The "Next Step Up" in evolution is a self-informing organism that transcends its own life/experience.
- Negative Entropy: Evolution is a "return" or a backward process where a pre-existent higher form (dormant within) pulls the lower form upward.
