## Key Claims
- Dick shifts his focus to the "inner-outer" feedback loop rather than "present-future." He realizes his experience was the assimilation of world into self, expanding his identity to cosmic proportions by realizing the outer world was just his own "prior thought-formations."
- This leads to a profound Neo-Platonist revelation: anamnesis is the immortal soul remembering its own eternal, unfallen reality among the Forms. It is not just remembering past lives, but remembering what one *is*. 
- Because all concentric realms of emanation exist simultaneously *here* and *now* (as Plotinus taught), remembering oneself automatically transports one from the spatial/temporal realm to the eternal realm.
- Valis is ultimately identified as Plotinus' "One," the transcendent unity above and behind the multiplicity of the Forms.

## Entities
- **Plotinus**: The Neo-Platonist philosopher whose model of concentric rings of emanation coexisting in one space provides Dick with the framework to explain how he accessed eternity without physically moving.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Firebright**: Re-introduced here as the immortal soul, ascending and descending through the realms, who finally wakes up to her true vastness and eternal nature.

## Theological Motifs
- **Acosmism/Buddhist Idealism**: The concept that the self and the world are fundamentally the same; to know the world is only to know the projections of one's own mind.
