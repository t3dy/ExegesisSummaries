# Claudia
## concise_summary
Dick explores "Immanence" using Parmenides' "All" and Kozyrev's time theory, concluding that God is total in every "micropoint." He argues that the Father (Yahweh/Judgment) took his place during the 1974 "Xerox crisis" to receive the mortal blow as a "surrogate sacrifice" (Agapic role).
## key_claims
- Immanence: God is "projected from every point" (Kozyrev); to encounter Him in the alley is to encounter Him completely.
- The "Still Small Voice" (Elijah) is God scaling Himself down, not a demiurge.
- The King as Judge: The beardless King (1974) is maturing into the "Wise Old King" (1970 dream), preparing to mount a horse and judge the world.
- Sowing the Tares: God is waiting for the "wheat and tares" to fully grow before intervening; the time-lapse is a mercy to save the wheat.
- Surrogate Sacrifice: In 3-74, Christ/God replaced Phil to take the "mortal blow" (Xerox threat), fulfilling the archetypal role of sacrificial love.
- Vicarious Suffering: God infuses the victim (animal or human) with His spirit to abolish the sin of the murderer.
