# Fusion with the Urgrund: The End of the Intermediary
## concise_summary
Dick explores the dialectical relationship between the Urgrund (primal reality) and the world-projecting artifact. He posits that the artifact is an intermediary entity that will eventually be eliminated as the human consciousness reaches isomorphism (fusion) with the Urgrund. This process requires a withdrawal of assent from the "spurious" projected world to reveal the authentic divine substratum hidden beneath.
## key_claims
- Urgrund vs. Artifact Dialectic: The empirical world is the result of a constant interaction between the sentient Urgrund and the mechanical artifact.
- Elimination of the Intermediary: As the schema moves toward perfection (isomorphism), the artifact is bypassed and eventually eliminated.
- Spurious Reality: Our world is a holographic projection lacking ontological substance.
- Withdrawal of Assent: Humans must actively deny the spurious reality to see the authentic substratum, a process that must be triggered by an external entity (the Savior/Paraclete).
- Rome A.D. 70 Template: The underlying reality is a timeless template of the early Christian struggle against Rome.
