## Key Claims
- Gnosticism is essentially a profound "ontological psychology." The primordial crisis (the Fall) was an internal schism within the self, not just a spatial fall from heaven. 
- We experience the world as alien and hostile because we are alienated from our own missing halves. A partial self experiences a partial, broken world.
- The "in-gathering of the scattered light" is actually the restored self rescuing itself. The saving Gnosis is intrinsic; the self already knows everything it needs once reunified.
- Anamnesis (remembering) occurs when the partial self recognizes something familiar (a Platonic Form) in an alien landscape, abruptly proving it once belonged to a unified, total self.

## Entities
- **Plato**: His doctrine of Forms (universalia ante rem) is the mechanism by which the estranged halves of the self, existing in different spatiotemporal realms, share a constant.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Salvator Salvandus**: The saved savior; the total intact self operating externally to rejoin its own severed internal parts.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Theophany at Sinai vs. Christ**: Dick briefly notes the Torah ratifies retributive causal law (karma) and is thus a product of the fallen world, whereas Christ reverses the fall by lifting that retribution.
