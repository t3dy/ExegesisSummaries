## Key Claims
- Christianity is presented as the epitome of realism and sanity: it is the full, unflinching recognition that mankind is helpless in the grip of vastly superior worldly forces, and defeat is inevitable.
- The true Christian does not evade this truth through fantasy, false heroics, or stoicism. By recognizing world-power as invincible, the Christian becomes the "first truly sane man."
- The mystery of Christ’s crucifixion is paradoxical: "Kill him and he lives." The act of being destroyed by worldly power generates total consciousness (knowing), which is the essence of humanity.
- Heidegger stated the "Fall" of man was when *noein* (knowing) and *einai* (being) parted company due to man fleeing his own mortality. Christianity rejoins them.
- What makes this capitulation to inevitable defeat possible is the trust that God's *einai* is *agape* (unconditional love). The horrors of the world do not offset the eternal love of God.

## Entities
- **Heidegger**: Philosopher whose concepts of the Fall (parting of knowing and being) and authentic being are employed by Dick to explain Christian salvation.
- **Jesus**: Validates the human experience: on the cross, he doubted as man but was sustained by God's eternal love.
- **W.B. Yeats**: Quoted ("the world of old on dreaming fed") to contrast past illusions with the Christian's new, harsh consciousness.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Noein and Einai**: Greek concepts of "knowing" and "being," separated in the Fall but reunited when the Christian faces inevitable destruction without fleeing.
- **Agape**: Unconditional love; the true essence/nature of God that overrides the temporary ordeal of the world.

## Theological Motifs
- **Weakness as Power**: The Taoist and Christian principle that by accepting defeat and remaining meek, the human soul wins ontologically.
- **The Theodicy of Agape**: The guarantee that the finite bad of the world will be more than offset by the infinite good of return to God.
