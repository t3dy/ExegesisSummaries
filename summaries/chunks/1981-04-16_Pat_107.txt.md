# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_107
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_107
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: *VALIS* as Unconscious Prophecy, The Spears of Heavy Metal, The Wound that Gives Birth
- **Key Entities**: Francie Steiger, Amfortas (Fisher King), Zagreus, Zeus

## Key Claims
- Rereading *VALIS*, Dick is shocked to find "predatory origins" of the Tagore revelation already embedded in it. The "heavy metal particle" that wounds the macro-mind directly parallels the radioactive waste poisoning the ecosphere.
- He analyzes a hypnagogic vision of a massive slit-like wound on his thigh. He links this to Amfortas (the wounded Fisher King), the spear wound in Christ's side, and the red spot on Jupiter.
- Drawing on the myth of Dionysus/Zagreus being born from Zeus's thigh and the Church being born from the water flowing from Christ's side, Dick realizes the wound is not just death—it is a vagina-like opening giving *birth*.
- Thus, Tagore gives his life voluntarily so that out of his fatal burns, new spiritual life can be born into the world. He acts as the canal or channel for the divine.

## Entities
- **Francie Steiger**: A correspondent whose remark ("You are a Christ-child") reinforces Dick's feeling that he is acting as the host/channel for the divine.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Voluntary Sacrifice**: Tagore's essence *is* taking on suffering voluntarily. If he stopped taking on the injuries of the world to save himself, he would cease to be Tagore; he would lose his Buddha-nature.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Wound of the Fisher King (Amfortas)**: The macrocosmic wound that strikes the divine body, poisoning the wasteland (the ecosphere), which must be healed by the Parousia.
