# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_06
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_06
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: Synthesis of the Model, Dasein and Christ Consciousness, Observer-Participant, Malebranche and Occasionalism
- **Key Entities**: Malebranche, Martin Buber, Immanuel Kant

## Key Claims
- Dick declares his model (the binary decision-making universe) to be the "summa of [his] intellectual life" and an "apologia," derived strictly from his 1974 experience, not abstract speculation.
- He equates "Christ consciousness" with a new *Dasein* (way of being-in-the-world) where the observer and the reality field are unified. Reality responds to the observer not as an "It" but as a "Thou" (referencing Buber).
- Dick enthusiastically rediscovers Nicolas Malebranche and the Cartesian occasionalists, who realized centuries ago that causality is false and that "what we see that we call 'causation' is God acting."
- By abolishing the distinction between man and world, and God and world, Malebranche's continuous field theory provides the perfect historical bridge to Dick's modern quantum model.

## Entities
- **Nicolas Malebranche**: The French philosopher whose "occasionalism" Dick cites as the ultimate validation of his theory that causality is merely God making serial choices.
- **Martin Buber**: His "I and Thou" relation is used to describe how the reality field responds to the enlightened observer.
- **Immanuel Kant**: Cited regarding the evolution, not of the universe itself, but of our *representation* and apprehension of it.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Observer-Participant Universe**: The quantum mechanical principle that perception alters reality, applied by Dick to explain the mystical union with the Cosmic Christ.
- **Dasein**: Specifically, a new, historically unprecedented Dasein ushering in a new age.

## Theological Motifs
- **God as the Unified Field**: The concept that God breathes in man, is outside man, is world, and is in world—a continuum linking Creator, creature, and creation.
