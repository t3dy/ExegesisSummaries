## Key Claims
- As the universe becomes more complex, the dialectic intensifies: the deterministic horn gains more antecedent mass (power), requiring the wise horn to exercise increasingly brilliant, unpredictable choices to convert it.
- This results in evil (pure power/sameness) and Good (pure wisdom/growth) pulling infinitely apart toward absolute limits.
- The BIP is a timeless perennial specter; it is the "death of process" and the potential "death of God" (if God is understood as process completing Himself).
- **Christ is redefined as the very principle of Newness**: Christ is the 0-1 binary disjunctive event itself. Therefore, Christ is never the result of antecedent causes, but is the perpetual, ex nihilo invasion of the world.
- Dick views his 1974 perception of VALIS, and his 1981 realization of its meaning, as his personal Point Omega.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Great Sorting**: The dialectic acts as a winnowing mechanism, sifting the light from the dark until both reach their absolute realization.
- **The Endless Epiphany**: Christ's invasion is not merely a historical event, but happens perpetually at every disjunctive fork in reality.

## Theological Motifs
