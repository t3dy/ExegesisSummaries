## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_24

- **Themes**: The Ontological Argument from Belief, Cosmogenesis as Compelled Assent, The Constant Renewal of Creation
- **Key Entities**: Descartes, Baruch Spinoza, A.N. Whitehead

## Key Claims
- Dick formulates a vital amendment to Descartes' *Cogito, ergo sum*: "I know I believe [in the world], and I know I cannot compel my own belief. Therefore, an agency beyond myself with the power to compel belief exists."
- For human functionality, the metaphysical existence of the universe is irrelevant; what matters pragmatically is that *the universe seems real to us*. God, acting as Cosmogenator, enforces this seeming.
- Dick sharply distinguishes his view from Spinoza's pantheism: God is *not* the reality field. God is the transcendent agency that ceaselessly perturbs the reality field by introducing ex nihilo newness.
- God revealed the binary switching model to Dick because it was the specific "Gestalt" (information-processing mechanism) that Dick's modern mind could comprehend and assent to, thus inferring God from the mechanism.

## Entities
- **Descartes**: His foundational modern philosophy is the explicit springboard for Dick's existential proof of God via the mechanics of belief.
- **Baruch Spinoza**: Explicitly rejected here; Dick insists God must be transcendent to the world-order to be capable of perturbing it, rather than entirely immanent within it.
- **A.N. Whitehead**: Quoted again ("God is a principle of selection...") as the perfect definition of the deity Dick encountered.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Pretextual Cause**: Re-emphasized as the miracle of creation occurring inside normal mechanical causality—the "effect" exceeding its putative deterministic "cause" because God has covertly injected new telemetry.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Constant Creation**: God does not merely sustain what exists; via the zero-one binary switching, God constantly creates the universe anew from nothing.
