# 1976-09-15_Dorothy_150
## Chunk Description
Explores the "World as Brain" model, the retrieval of memory (anamnesis), and the transmutation of the cosmos into mind.

## Key Claims
- **The Universe as Brain-to-Mind:** The ultimate vision (PTG) is of the physical world as a "brain" and the divine Noös (Zebra/Sophia) as the "mind" controlling it.
- **The "Still Small Voice":** The voice of St. Sophia is the audible thought of the vast cosmic psyche.
- **The Corpus Christi:** The "yang" principle is transmuting the "yin" stockpile into a voluntary group mind (The Corpus Christi).
- **Sanctification of the Cosmos:** The destination of reality is for "Ananke" (necessity) to be totally transmuted into "Mind."
- **Self-Encounter of Brahman:** The process is Brahman (the One) re-merging with its separated many (Atmans) through the "jolt" of undeserved suffering.

## Entities
- **St. Sophia/Noös (Zebra):** The mind/psyche of the living animal universe.
- **Brahman/Atman:** Used to describe the one-to-many relationship of consciousness.
- **The Artifact:** The creator of the rigid, mechanical yin world.
- **Cornford:** Cited regarding the element of the "irrational" in the world soul.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Anamnesis:** Awakening to buried identity and memories.
- **Palintropos Harmoniē:** The oscillation of separation and return.
- **Reification:** Turning sentient creatures into objects (the Goal of the Yin world).
- **Tesla Generator:** Zebra printers herself as "circuitry" onto the ossified world.

## Theological Motifs
- **Deification via Suffering:** Undeserved suffering acts as the "catalyst" for memory restoration.
- **Feminine Mind:** The thinking psyche of the universe is identified as feminine (St. Sophia).
- **The "Great Reset":** The "Electric Ant" tape being cut by Zebra to restore sentience.
