# Orthogonal symphonies: The Reworking of History
## concise_summary
Dick formalizes his "Orthogonal Time" theory using the "Wealthy Patron" and "Symphony" metaphors. He argues that history is a "linear strip" that can be "reworked" or "improved" at a specific nexus point (like 70 AD) without moving forward along the linear axis.
## key_claims
- Orthogonal vs. Linear: Linear time is a moving strip (dates). Orthogonal time is "right angles" where a moment (the Matrix) is re-experienced and changed.
- Symphony Reworking: History is like a series of symphonies (1, 2, 3...). God (The Patron) decides to rework Symphony #1 (70 AD) rather than writing #10.
- 3-74 as Version (2): Dick experienced Version (1) of the 1974/70 AD interface (deterministic failure) and was then placed in Version (2) (Redeemed/Fixed).
- The Blowing up of the Prison: In Version (2), the Christians/Rebels possess explosives and "Cyrus-like" assistance to destroy the prison (Nixon/Empire).
- It is Always 70 AD (Orthogonally): Because 70 AD was the "unique breaking-through" (1st Advent), it remains the "permanent matrix" that is constantly being rewoven.
- The Fakeness of Linear Time: Linear periods (1066, 1750, 1977) are "fake" in relation to the "real" orthogonal breakthrough of the 1st century.
- Memory Adjustment: When an orthogonal change occurs, memories are adjusted linearly so we don't notice the "re-playing." Dick noticed it because of "anamnesis."
- Earth as Slave Colony: Combining Star Wars and Gnosticism, Dick views Earth as a "slave colony" of the Empire held by Maya/Dokos.
