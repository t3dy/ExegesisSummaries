# Claudia
## concise_summary
Dick explores the distinction between the "God of the structure" (the Creator) and the "God of the discarded bits" (Christ). He details his experience of the "mirror opposite universe" where time runs backward and processes reveal the Logos through a "reversed" perception.
## key_claims
- There are two Gods: the Creator (concerned with the total structure) and Christ (who rescues the rejected/sacrificed pieces).
- We seek the God of the defeated and powerless (Christ) to avoid perishing "ant-like" in the service of the final gestalt.
- Reality is a "mirror opposite" of its true essence; our perception is "reversed" or "inside-out."
- The "mirror opposite universe" is a weak, living, intelligent mind that benefits the forward flow of our reality.
- The 1974 experience was a "waking up" from a state of electronic thought control.
