# 1975-02-27_Claudia_01
# SECTION_001
# February 27, 1975
# Claudia

## concise_summary
The author writes to Claudia, interpreting the "decay backward of forms" seen by the character Joe Chip in the novel *UBIK* as movement along the "orthogonal" axis of time. He claims this retrograde motion is actually forward progress toward completion (the Parousia), not entropic decay. He correlates this with his own spiritual experience in March 1974, where he felt he was moving forward to the completion of time and saw the Parousia/Final Things, equating the fictional form-reversion to regaining a childlike state of innocence as described by Christ. 

## key_claims
- The "decay backward of forms" in *UBIK* is not entropic decay but movement along an "orthogonal" time axis toward the Parousia.
- The author personally experienced this orthogonal forward movement to the "completion of time" in March 1974.
- The Christian concept of becoming "as little children" refers to this orthogonal movement, which appears as the past in linear time but is the future in orthogonal time.
- The events of March 1974 (the fall of Nixon) had a macro-historical/escatological reality ("the Prince of this World fell").

## tensions_contradictions
- The author claims the March 1974 experience was an actual realization of the Parousia ("I saw the Parousia"), yet he also questions it ("Did they take place? Has the Kingdom arrived? I say Yes it has...").
- He states the 1964 acid trip was just a "head trip," whereas the 1974 vision was identical but "authentic" because it was existentially validated by the death of Pinky the cat.
- He feels guilty for killing the rat in the 50s but claims it was a necessary existential lesson on the moral necessity of death given by God.

## uncertainty_flags
- The timeline of the rat's death is slightly unclear; he says the rat died in "March 74" but then describes killing it in the '50s. It appears he killed a rat in the 50s, but is associating the event with the mystical death/rebirth cycle activated in March 1974.
- The distinction between whether he uniquely experienced the end of his personal time (entelechy) or if the whole world did ("what befell our world in March 1974").
