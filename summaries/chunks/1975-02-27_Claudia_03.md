# Claudia
## concise_summary
Dick explores the "Fallen" state of humanity through the metaphor of a fairy child in the mortal world (*The Red Feather*). He concludes that orthogonal time leads back to a childhood state where the universe was the teacher, and that the "Final Things" involve a restoration to this original, uncorrupted perception.
## key_claims
- We are moving away from a moment of completion/perfection (the "explosion" into linear time).
- To enter the Kingdom of Heaven, one must regain the perceptual state of a 3-6 year old.
- Through objects like "Tunny" (a canned fish billboard), the living Archetypes (Savior) speak to children.
- We must eventually "become our own parents" (Archetypal King/Queen).
## recurring_concepts

- Childhood as completion

- "Tunny" / Fish Sign
