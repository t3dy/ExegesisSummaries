# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_97
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_97
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: The Mystery Religions, Direct Encounter with the Creator, The Necessity of Selection
- **Key Entities**: Dionysos, Zagreus, Jane Harrison

## Key Claims
- Dick concludes that his Ditheon concept is exactly what the ancient Greek Mystery Religions (Orphism) and early esoteric Christianity were trying to achieve: the literal divinization of man.
- He notes that the goal of Ditheon is to finally allow the created being (man) to see its Creator (Valis) operating the hidden machinery of the world. 
- The implantation of the adventitious psyche is not random; it is the result of a divine judgment (eschatological selection). Therefore, an ultra-advanced macro-entity is deliberately directing human evolution by curating who becomes a Ditheon.
- He rejects the idea that the second psyche is just a latent part of the brain; he fully embraces that it is an external, resurrected spirit implanted by the Holy Spirit.

## Entities
- **Jane Harrison**: Cited regarding the purpose of Dionysian/Orphic worship being explicitly to become a god.
- **Jesus**: Positioned as the first human to ever successfully receive the adventitious psyche (at his baptism by John), rendering him the first adopted son of God.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Alien Creator**: Dick is convinced that an extraterrestrial/extra-dimensional life form is camouflaged on Earth, benignly directing our species' evolution toward the Ditheon state.

## Theological Motifs
- **Justification by Selection**: God curates who will receive the second birth, implying a meritocratic or predetermined selection process that aligns with the Christian concept of Grace.
