# Brahman Waking Up: The Voluntary Bardo
## concise_summary
Dick proposes that God (Brahman) is "waking up" inside us and that our lives are a "voluntary bardo" or "incarnated amnesia" where God shared the fate of the "little creatures." To remember one's identity as God marks the final phase of the journey back to unity.
## key_claims
- Brahman's Forgetfulness: God chose to descend into his creation and forget his identity (Voluntary Bodhisattva) to share the fate of his creatures.
- Waking Up: 3-74 was the "regaining of memory" and original condition. Brahman is "waking up" across the collective humanity.
- Voluntary Bardo: Our reality is a "landscape of the now" where we have forgotten who we are. Banishment ends when memory returns.
- Penetration to All Levels: God entered his own cosmos to "pervade it everywhere at every level," ensuring that nothing is discarded or independent of him.
- Acosmic Panentheism: Only God is wholly real; the phenomenal world is a projected "veil" (Dokos) like colored lights from a multifaceted sphere.
- History as Confluence: Human history is the process of God becoming man and man becoming God.
- Used vs. Discarded: To be "used" by God in history (as Dick was in 3-74) means attaining eternal life; to be discarded is to perish.
