# The 3-Element Schema: God, Artifact, and the Feral World
## concise_summary
Dick clarifies his "3-element schema" (God, the Artifact, and Man) to explain the hostility of the world and Christ's suffering. He argues that the artifact's world has turned "feral" even to its creator, requiring God to enter disguised as an "intruder" (Christ/Thomas) to sabotage the system. He views himself (and other participants) as "physical extensions" or "terminals" of Christ's mind in sacred time, where linear history is overridden by the archetypal drama of the Passion and Resurrection.
## key_claims
- 3-Element Schema: God did not create the "world" directly; a "middle agency" (the artifact) did, and it has turned hostile/feral.
- Disguised Intruder: God's presence in the world as "Christ" or "Thomas" is a stealth mission to sample a world He no longer recognizes as His own.
- Transubstantiation of the Person: In sacred time, to "resemble" Christ is to *be* (esse) Christ. PKD became a "terminal" for the divine mind.
- Thomas the Early Christian: The "Thomas" personality was a literal early Christian spirit from the Mystical Body, unified with PKD.
- The Feral Artifact: The world did not recognize its creator because the artifact became a "King of Tears" (tyrant).
- Peaceable Kingdom Sign: The arrival of sacred time was first visible in the "transmuted" faces of animals.
