# Chunk 164 Summary - 1976-09-15_Dorothy_164

## Description
PKD discusses the idea of "Subliminal Cuing" and the "Right Hemisphere" as receptors for divine messages. He interprets "History" not as a series of random events but as the "biography" of a single living organism (humanity) being guided toward a purposeful conclusion.

## Key Claims
- **History as Organism**: The 3 billion individuals on Earth form a "single organism" whose collective history is the biography of one living thing extensive in space and time.
- **Subliminal Messages**: Supranormal messages are directed at "international transactions" to guide history; these messages use "pattern recognition" in the right hemisphere.
- **Teaching System**: Life is a "control or guiding or teaching system" designed to maintain the harmony of the whole.
- **Ananke vs. Reason**: Level 2 reality is "Ananke" (deterministic, astral fate), while Level 3 is "Reason" (sentient purpose/God) that breaks the lower program.

## Entities
- **Harvey (The Cat)**: Used as an analogy for how humans can see "messages" without being able to read them.
- **Zebra**: The "override" intelligence that broke the deterministic control system in 3-74.
- **Doubleday**: The word on the book carton used in the Harvey analogy.
- **The android from *International Transactions*** (?): Reference to the search for signs of a maker in history.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Clutch Theory**: Individual actions are guided by a "clutch" (Jeter) outside their control.
- **Balking Syndrome**: The characteristic that distinguishes human judgment from android-like reflex.
- **Astral Fate**: Deterministic programming represented by Level 2.
- **Grief to Love**: The specific "catalyst" for passing from the prison (2) to the garden (3).

## Theological Motifs
- **Divine Providence**: The name for the sender of the guiding messages.
- **God as History's Guide**: The search for the creator should be conducted in the arena of species-wide history.
- **The "Bait" Theme**: The idea that the hidden reality serves to test and appraise human judgment.
