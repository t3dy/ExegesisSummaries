## Key Claims
- Dick observes that what Christians call "The Law" or "Heimarmene," the East calls Karma. Jesus was fundamentally a great Yogi or Magician teaching humanity how to break the absolute hold of Karmic determinism.
- He realizes that his Ditheon concept (two psyches merging into pure consciousness) aligns more perfectly with Pan-Indian philosophy (Buddhism/Advaita Vedanta) than traditional Christianity. 
- The "Anokhi" (I Am) state Dick experienced is synonymous with Atman-Brahman—the underlying, non-dual, impersonal absolute consciousness that generates the illusion (Maya) of multiplicity and causation.
- He re-evaluates VALIS: Valis inside him (Atman) and Valis outside him (Brahman) are identical. Both the individual self and the external physical world are ultimately illusory.
- The 1974 meta-abstraction was an awakening from the Matrix of Maya—realizing that both the internal ego and the external deterministic universe are merely prior thought-formations of the Absolute Mind.

## Entities
- **Buddha and Sankara**: Referenced to validate the realization that sensory reality and individual selfhood are ultimately illusory constructs (Maya).

## Recurring Concepts
- **Tat Tvam Asi**: ("Thou art That") The core Hindu realization that the individual soul is identical to the ultimate reality, matching Dick's internal/external Valis experience.
- **Ajna Eye**: The opening of the third eye (spiritual enlightenment) that allows perception of the underlying unity.

## Theological Motifs
- **Christianity Subsumed by Buddhism**: Dick momentarily replaces his Judeo-Christian dualistic dialectic with a monistic Pan-Indian framework, seeking a better fit for the "merger" aspect of the Ditheon.
