## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1978-10-10_SECTION_016_352

- **Themes**: Cartesian Occasionalism, Constants and Variations, Space vs. Time, Unity of Field
- **Key Entities**: Malebranche, Leibnitz, Spinoza, Parmenides, Kant

## Key Claims
- The "reality field is at any given time complete and cannot act on a later reality field," leading to an extreme form of Cartesian occasionalism whereby only God bridges the static frameworks.
- Space is real (a unitary field), while Time (the variations or modulations of the field) is not.
- Dick perceives reality as analogous to a script (mentioning Fancher's *Blade Runner* script ideas), wherein an android passes through variations while remaining the same core entity ("he causes things to look different so it'd appear time has passed").
- There is no fundamental difference between 70 A.D. (Acts) and USA 1974; they are variations on stable "constants" ("not 3 mothers, but 1 mother seen 3 times").
- The concept of "God" (as creator of a separate world) was an erroneous cosmology stemming from antiquity. Malebranche's and Spinoza's unified views align much closer to the modern physics perspective of reality as a field.

## Entities
- **Fancher (Hampton)**: Screenwriter referenced in relation to android variations passing through time but retaining essential form.
- **Malebranche**: Cited for his doctrine that God constantly acts to bridge events that lack independent causal power.
- **Kant**: Mentioned regarding his supposition that space, time, and causation cannot be truly separated.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Linking-Relinking / Set-Ground**: Mechanisms by which reality stores information and tries out juxtapositions like permutations of a program.
- **Constants**: The underlying, timeless eide/structures that persist despite all the frame-by-frame modulations.
- **Modulation**: The variations flowing through the carrier (reality field).

## Theological Motifs
- **Hyper-occasionalism / Field Theology**: The idea that God (or our minds under God's influence) supplies the connectives that animate completely static frames.
- **Unitary View of God**: Ideas of "afterlife" or a separate "heaven" are false because they rely on the duality of Creator vs Artifact.
