## Key Claims
- The Ditheon (the completed, two-psyche entity) possesses the cognitive capacity to "unmix" coaxial reality. It sees the physical world not as matter, but directly as pre-existent Ideas in the intellect of God (the Logos).
- Time is an illusion; there is only a "change-rate velocity" axis. The past is perceptually structured into the present. 
- Drawing on Mircea Eliade, Dick argues that accurately re-performing mythic sacraments (like the Eucharist) synchronizes the participant's "velocity" with the original event, plunging them into the eternal "Dream Time" (the MMSK).
- Reincarnation is completely discarded: Thomas was not a past life, but an adventitious spirit retrieved from the coaxial "Dream Time" by Dick's encounter with the Christian sacrament (the Jesus fish).
- The "Fall" was not a moral transgression, but an epistemological catastrophe: as Heidegger noted, humanity fell from *being* the Logos (Parmenides) to merely *having* it as an abstract category (Aristotle). The 2-74 event reversed this Fall for Dick.

## Entities
- **Mircea Eliade**: His theories on mythic time ("illo tempore") and the re-performance of sacraments explain how Dick "travelled back" to 70 AD.
- **Heidegger and Parmenides**: Used to explain that true faith (Pistis) is an ontological *awareness* that unity and reason (Logos) are currently present, not a mere intellectual assent to doctrine.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Dream Time / Coaxial Reality**: The concept that all historical epochs exist simultaneously; specific triggers (sacraments) allow consciousness to slide between them.
- **Pistis (Faith)**: Redefined as a lightning-bolt cognitive realization (meta-abstraction) of reality's true nature, reversing the ancient philosophical split between *noein* (thinking) and *einai* (being).

## Theological Motifs
- **The Sacrament as Time Machine**: Religious rituals are not symbolic commemorations, but literal, operational mechanisms for exiting local time and accessing the eternal Forms.
