# Adoption and the Return to the Garden
## concise_summary
Dick explores the "Adoptionalist" theory of his experience: that he was "raised" to a superhuman level and "incorporated" into the building of the New Earth. He reflects on the "Thomas" personality as his "saved self" and the "psychopomp" who bridges the gulf back to God.
## key_claims
- Adoption to Superhuman Level: 3-74 was an "adoptionalist" elevation where Dick was raised to an intermediate level between man and God.
- Incorporation into the Edifice: He was "assimilated" into the "Kingdom of Christ" (the new building Zebra is constructing) for safekeeping.
- The Saved Self (Thomas): "Thomas" is the "perfected man" at the beginning of Dick's genetic line. To be saved, Dick must be "transformed into Thomas."
- Form Reversion (Ubik): Reinterpretation of *Ubik*'s regression: Dick "regressed" to a perfected ancestor rather than just a younger version of himself.
- The Fall of Wordsworth: Cites the "Ode" to show we have "lapsed" from completeness; history is not progress but "unwinding" toward entropy.
- Christ as Restorer: Christ's task is to "restore what was lost," not to evolve us into something new. Thomas is the result of this restoration.
- The Parousia is Here: Evidence of 3-74 and the presence of Thomas/Zebra proves that Christ has already returned invisibly.
- The Garden and the Golden Gate: The final "frame" of his 11-month "high speed" experience was the Golden Rectangle Gate and the Garden, signifying the end of linear time.
