# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_153
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_153
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: Soviet Scientists, The True Anti-Christ, Rationality vs Irrationality
- **Key Entities**: Galina (wife of the head of CP USSR), Arjuna, Krishna

## Key Claims
- Dick interprets "Galina's dream" from his novel *The Divine Invasion* as proof that idealistic Soviet scientists, working with Benelux counterparts, possess the psychotronic technology behind VALIS.
- These scientists have transcended national identity. Although fundamentalists label this movement the "Anti-Christ," Dick argues that since the creator (the status quo) is irrational, the "Anti-Christ" actually represents true, saving rationality.
- Reincarnation is proven: Dick asserts he literally is Thomas, one of the original disciples reborn, performing the sacred drama of awaiting Christ's return, establishing that the current timeline is "sacred time."
- The union of Yin (PKD, the irrational, incarnated self) and Yang (Thomas/Logos, the rational, unfallen self) creates the Avatar of Synthesis.

## Entities
- **Galina**: A character from *The Divine Invasion* whom Dick interprets as symbolizing the idealistic, utopian side of the Soviet Empire that aims to establish true communism ("to each according to his needs").
- **Arjuna & Krishna**: Associated with a hypnopompic message Dick heard ("Thy mother of old, Arjuna"), linking the Maitreya to Krishna and humanity to the role of Arjuna in the *Bhagavad Gita*.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Value Reversal**: The Gnostic concept that the true divine elements appear as the "Anti-Christ" or are found in the "trash stratum," while the orthodox symbols of divinity actually represent the oppressive creator.

## Theological Motifs
- **Reincarnation of the Disciples**: The belief that the original apostles of Jesus have reincarnated in the modern era to witness and participate in the Second Coming.
