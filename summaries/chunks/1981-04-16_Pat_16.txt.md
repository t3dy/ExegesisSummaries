## Key Claims
- Dick reflects on his 30-year pursuit of understanding the illusion of causality, concluding that his 1974 experience provided the empirical answer (the binary/dialectic model).
- He views his philosophical inquiry not as abstract theorizing but as essential investigative work seeking to explain a literal "perturbation in the reality field" that he witnessed.
- The dialectic model clearly explains the existence of evil: evil is a deterministic, karmic "no" stage generated by the system's past, which the system mind (Noos) then edits, subsumes, and converts into a higher "yes" stage.
- The Black Iron Prison (BIP) is not a conspiracy but the natural, entropic tendency of the system toward stagnation, mechanical rigidity, and heat-death. 
- Sentient, disjunctive choices (newness/0-1 switching) are required to thwart this natural decay into the BIP.

## Entities
- **Descartes / Kant**: Referenced as framing the modern epistemological problems that Dick believes he has uniquely solved via revelation.
- **Pat Wilson**: Mentioned as having yelled at Dick 32 years ago for his presumptuous questioning of standard causality.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Anti-Soul**: The BIP is described as the anti-soul of the whole system, the aggregate of all mindless "zero" moves tending toward entropy.
- **The Inevitability of the BIP**: The rigid deterministic state will always reassert itself by default unless actively thwarted by divine choosing.

## Theological Motifs
- **Evil as Ephemeral, Good as Cumulative**: Evil has a finite lifespan and is always converted, whereas Good is cumulative and never revised.
