# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_81
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_81
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: The Acts Lens/Grid, Universal Language vs. Schizophrenic Meaning, Reality as Modulated Carrier, The Secret Christian
- **Key Entities**: Plotinus, James Joyce

## Key Claims
- Salvation occurs when the mediator acts as a two-way lens between the self and the world. Normally, the world's "language" is foreign and closed (Alienation/Geworfenheit). The mediator renders the world's objects into clear, declarative syntactical signs rather than ambiguous riddles.
- Dick proposes that the Holy Spirit's primary function is not just human glossolalia, but reversing Babel universally, allowing the percipient to distinguish the latent "coaxial" world from the spurious physical one.
- The physical universe is merely a "carrier" that is being modulated by a purely informational world. The relationship of the Kingdom to the physical world is like music to the grooves of an LP record.
- In 1974, Dick remembered his identity as a "secret Christian" reacting to the fish sign. He realized he was a citizen of the latent, invisible kingdom.

## Entities
- **James Joyce**: The sudden, total conceptual understanding of the mediator and world language is likened to Joyce's "epiphanies."

## Recurring Concepts
- **Alienation vs. Comprehension**: The schizophrenic "spread of meaning" is replaced by direct, deciphered assertions.
- **The Modulated Carrier**: Information processing metaphor for how the divine kingdom "rides" on top of physical reality.

## Theological Motifs
- **Ditheon Redefined**: Dick begins to suspect "Ditheon" means the existence of two selves: the ephemeral empirical ego and the ubiquitous, absolute pure consciousness (Atman).
