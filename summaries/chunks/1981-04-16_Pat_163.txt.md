## Key Claims
- In his strange feedback loop, Dick gained control of his own DNA, allowing his ontogeny (individual self) to program his phylogeny (species blueprint). He effectively jumped to the end of human spiritual evolution, becoming a perfected, absolute entity (Valis/Christ).
- Recognizing the world as entirely one's own "prior thought-formations" (as in Tibetan Buddhism) is the prerequisite to escaping Heimarmene and finding the true "Other."
- Christ is not a single object within the mind's library, but the unified structural pattern of the entire library. Perceiving Christ requires a hyper-abstracting capability matching an innate, internal template against the total structure of reality.
- Dick fully aligns himself with Malebranche and the Occasionalists: we never experience physical objects, only representations generated from the archetypal ideas in the mind of God; thus, the only two things we directly know are our own minds and God (Infinity).

## Entities
- **Malebranche**: The Occasionalist philosopher whose theories of representationalism and our direct knowledge of God Dick joyous accepts as the perfect explanation for the 3-74 experience.
- **Spinoza**: Dick equates Malebranche's experience of God with Spinoza's attribute of "Infinity"—knowledge of God as an infinite field of meaning.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Inner Matching Template**: An innate, a priori cognitive structure that matches the macrocosmic Gestalt of reality, allowing the individual to extract the hidden figure of Christ from the background noise.

## Theological Motifs
- **Christogenesis**: The process by which the mind, turning inward in absolute self-reflection, bursts the prison of solipsism and gives birth to (or discovers) the true, absolute Other—Christ.
