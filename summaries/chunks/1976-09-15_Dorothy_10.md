# The Black Iron Prison vs. The Palm Tree Garden
## concise_summary
Dick introduces the theory of the "Black Iron Prison" (BIP) as the true, underlying state of the world, concealed by the "James-James" demiurge. He contrasts this with the "Palm Tree Garden" (the real world of Brahman) and argues that "salvation" is the act of remembering our origin in the Pleroma.
## key_claims
- James-James (J-J): A "genius magician" or demiurge who created the "phenomenal world" as a bedazzling illusion to conceal the truth.
- The Black Iron Prison (BIP): The actual, intolerable state of our world (Rome c. 70 A.D.) that we are "dreaming" away from.
- The Palm Tree Garden: The "Kingdom of God" or "Brahman's world" which lies behind the BIP.
- Ubik as Savior: The intrusions by Runciter/Ubik are information from the "Good Deity" to help us escape the BIP.
- Salvation as Remembering: "We are 'not here' really;" we are in the Pleroma and have forgotten our true identity. Remembering *is* the escape.
- To Arms!: The "tutulary spirit" (VALIS) called him to act against the BIP (Watergate overthrow).
- Civitas Dei vs. Civitas Mundi: The Palm Tree Garden (Augustine's City of God) vs. The Black Iron Prison (City of Man).
- The Essence of Christianity: A "restoration" to God's image that allows us to see the two realms (Supralunar/Sublunar).
