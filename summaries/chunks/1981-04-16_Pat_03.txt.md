# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_03
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_03
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: Retrograde Reasoning, Dasein Breakthrough, Time vs. Space, Medieval Worldview
- **Key Entities**: Hume, Malebranche, Heidegger, Kant, Krishna

## Key Claims
- Linear causality is a perceptual tautology and logical fallacy (echoing Hume). The system is actually negentropic because the effect is greater than the cause—"more comes out of each forking than is put in."
- By replacing causality with binary elective decision-making, the universe ceases to be a deterministic machine (Ananke/Fate) and becomes a realm of freedom and sentient choice.
- Dick conceptualizes this paradigm shift not just as a theory, but as a new *Dasein* (way of being in the world) where the Kantian ordering category of 'causation' is discarded.
- He equates time and causation with the Black Iron Prison (bondage, necessity), and equates space with freedom and God.
- During 3-74, he exchanged horizontal temporal space for medieval "vertical space" (the Gothic vault), converting the flow of time into pure spatial reality.

## Entities
- **Hume**: Referenced for pointing out the logical fallacy of inferring backward from effect to cause (retrograde reasoning).
- **Malebranche**: Specifically credited by Dick as the first to figure out that causality is an inadequate and blinding modem.
- **Heidegger**: His concept of "authentic Being" (true destiny) is contrasted with determinism (fate).

## Recurring Concepts
- **Dasein**: Heidegger's term for human existence, which Dick uses to describe his total cognitive restructuring in 3-74.
- **Ananke / Heimarmene**: Fate, astral determinism, necessity—the oppressive system generated by believing in mechanical cause and effect.

## Theological Motifs
- **Spatializing Time**: The theological assertion that God exists in the spatial void between frames, and that converting time into space is necessary for liberation.
- **The Observer-Participant**: The idea that by withdrawing the inward projection of causality, the observer actively transubstantiates reality.
