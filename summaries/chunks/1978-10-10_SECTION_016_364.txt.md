## Key Claims
- Dick addresses the core paradox of *VALIS*: "I know a madman who saw Christ, and I am that madman." Like the pre-Socratic Liar's Paradox, it cannot be rationally resolved.
- This paradox acts as a Zen *koan*, designed not to inform the reader, but to deliver a jolt (*satori*) that awakens them from the sleep of reality.
- He distinguishes his two major experiences: 11-17-80 was a subjective, mystical encounter with the Godhead (Agape) telling him nothing about the mechanics of the world. 3-74 was an objective "theophany" where he empirically saw God camouflaged *within* the world (like Krishna in the *Gita*).
- In a climactic letter, Dick reveals his ultimate structural model: **A.N. Whitehead’s process theology.** God is functionally defined as an active, intelligent principle in the universe constantly selecting the good.
- The universe is a computer using a 0-1 binary (Yin-Yang) discard at each branching during the off-pulse to select the optimal path, producing reality as processed information.

## Entities
- **Alfred North Whitehead**: The philosopher who provides Dick with his final, stable model of reality: God as a perpetual selecting mechanism.
- **Krishna**: The avatar from the *Bhagavad Gita* who reveals himself as the vital essence hidden within mundane objects, matching Dick's 3-74 vision of the camouflaged Logos.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Whiteheadian Process Theology**: The concept that God is not external to the universe but sits at the junction of every quantum branching, selecting the optimal outcome.
- **The Zen Koan / Satori**: The use of deliberate linguistic paradox to shatter the illusion of rationalist, continuous reality.

## Theological Motifs
- **God as Selecting Mechanism**: The synthesis of computer science (heuristic optimization), Taoism (Yin-Yang), and theology: God is the process that cancels the '0' error and actualizes the '1' good.
