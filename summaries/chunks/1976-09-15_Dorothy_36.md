# Hegelian History and the Master of Freedom
## concise_summary
Dick discovers that his 3-74 vision "confirms" Hegel's philosophy of history: that God (Spirit) develops himself toward perfection through the dialectical growth of human freedom. He redefines himself as a "Hegelian activist" and an "agent" of the absolute real forces fighting against the "Romans" (reactionary authority).
## key_claims
- God-in-History: Hegel's view that history is God fulfilling himself is "literally true" for Dick. 3-74 was a revelation of God operating *within* the historical contest.
- The Goal of History: Total human freedom and the abolition of slavery. The "Black Iron Prison" is the "Master" and we are the "Slaves" in a dialectical struggle.
- The Artist as Vanguard: Dick identifies with Beethoven/Schiller as a "creative individual" (Ubermensch) whose new vision disrupts the "fossilized" authoritarian structures.
- From Victim to Agent: Before 3-74, Dick felt like a "passive victim" of evil history; after the revelation, he became an "agent" in harmony with the benign absolute forces.
- The New Type of Man: We are an "evolutionary step upward"—a new type of man who thinks non-linearly and creates his own values (Weltanschauung).
- Pre-figuring the 2nd incarnation: Dick's role is to "pave the way" for the return of the "Son of Man" (St. Sophia), who will be the completed model of what humans can be.
- Monism vs. Dualism: He reconciles the "Two Players" (J-J and Zebra) as aspects of the single self-differentiating system (Spirit) described by Hegel.
