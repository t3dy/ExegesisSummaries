# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-101

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-101
- **Themes:** Final Cause (Aristotle), Goal-Seeking, Teleology as Deity.

## Key Claims
- **Teleology = Deity:** Since modern science denies "final cause" (purpose in nature), anyone who experiences indubitable teleology (as PKD did in 3-74) has experienced "Deity."
- **External Stimulation:** The "goal-seeking" PKD felt was not his own but "imprinted" by an external agency (God as Mover).
- **The Rational Invader:** Valis is "rationality invading the irrational" (Creation). It doesn't reward/punish but "inspires goal-seeking."
- **Biological Striving:** God is equated with the biological drive of an organism to "excel and overcome" its obstacles/death.
- **God as Bastion:** God is the "final bastion against non-being," organizing the organism for a "quantum leap" in complexity.

## Entities
- **Valis:** The "entelechy of entelechies" seen "completing itself as form."
- **Aristotle:** The philosopher whose definitions of the "Unmoved Mover" provide the best match for 3-74.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Formal Cause:** The objective/blueprint (Kosmos) toward which everything is being stimulated.
- **Efficient Cause:** The "mechanical" causal trains that God sometimes overrules.
- **Resetting/Remastering:** The subjective experience of being "melted" by a goal-oriented force.

## Theological Motifs
- **Pragmatic God:** A deity defined by its function (producing the result) rather than its attributes (omniscience, etc.).
- **Indubitable Evidence:** The Preparation for the Xerox crisis (years in advance) counts as "proof" of an intentional mind.
- **Essence in Process:** The idea that a thing's true nature is the form it *achieves* through transition.
