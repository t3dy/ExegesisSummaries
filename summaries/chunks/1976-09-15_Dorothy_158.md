# 1976-09-15_Dorothy_158
## Chunk Description
Discusses the unique 1st Advent, the "3:5 Door" to resurrection, and the "Divine Substitution" in suffering.

## Key Claims
- **Uniqueness of Christ:** Unlike Indian avatars, God became man *only once* (A.D. 70). To witness Him truly, one must travel backward in time/space (Dissolve time).
- **Christ as Sufferer:** Christ is present *in* and *as* every suffering creature. He "takes the blow" for the creature.
- **The 3:5 Door:** A door in time leading to resurrection, laid down by Christ through his own stages of life.
- **Undeserved Suffering as Catalyst:** Puzzlement over undeserved suffering is the "jolt" that forces the organism to search for and find the "answer" (God).
- **Voluntary Return:** We have all returned to this world voluntarily to perform tasks in a vast entelechy, even if our memories were blocked.

## Entities
- **Pinky:** Discerned as Christ because he was "mocked, humiliated, and killed."
- **Joe Chip:** Used to illustrate form-regression and the man containing the "former man."
- **Will Durant:** Criticized for failing to account for divine intervention in history.
- **Hegel:** Mentioned regarding God acting upon history as material.

## Recurring Concepts
- **3:5 Door:** The threshold from suffering/death to joy/resurrection.
- **Winnowing Process:** The trial of life that separates the "styli."
- **Ananke (Plato):** Purposeless disorder; the good God can't compel it but can substitute Himself for the sufferer.
- **Tat Twam Asi:** "That Thou Art"—rejoining the Urgrund.

## Theological Motifs
- **Divine Paradox:** To be "broken" by the world is to be reshaped by Christ.
- **Champion of the Cast Down:** Christ as the advocate for the wreckage of the world.
- **Sacred Ordeal:** The systematic stripping away of worldly accretions as "birthpains."
