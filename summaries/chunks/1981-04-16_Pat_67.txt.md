## Key Claims
- Dick formalizes the mechanics of Ditheon: Psyche A (the past, deterministic machine mind / Inferno) + Psyche B (the present, autonomous human mind / Purgatorio) mathematically fuse to create Psyche C (Ditheon, future, divine / Paradiso).
- Schizophrenia is an evolutionary misfire: the B psyche correctly abreacts into the phylogenic unconscious to retrieve the lost A psyche, but *fails to achieve the alchemical fusion*, resulting in madness (being trapped in the past/machine world).
- In 1974, Dick successfully performed this "heroic journey": he descended into the archaic collective unconscious, retrieved "Thomas" (Psyche A), and fused him with PKD (Psyche B), completing Christogenesis.
- The resulting Ditheon (Psyche C, personified as Angel Archer) acts as the psychopomp or guide, leading the two halves toward integration.

## Entities
- **Carl Jung**: The entire process is mapped as a descent into the collective unconscious to retrieve an archetype, followed by successful individuation.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Salvator Salvandus**: The Gnostic concept of "the saved savior." Psyche A, B, and C are ultimately the exact same entity separated across time, rescuing itself.
- **Dante's Three Realms**: Used to represent three entirely different ways a mind processes time and causality.

## Theological Motifs
- **Descending into the Underworld**: The psychological equivalent of Christ's harrowing of hell; rescuing the trapped, determined pieces of the self from antiquity.
