## Key Claims
- Dick analyzes a vivid dream where a woman (Nancy) is invaded by a male psyche (John/Bill), and the resulting fused entity is labeled "DITHEON." 
- He connects this to a letter from Russ regarding *The Transmigration of Timothy Archer*: neither Tim nor Bill alone is Christ, but when Tim's psyche enters Bill's brain, the two human psyches combined *form* Christ.
- Therefore, "Ditheon" explicitly means the "Two-part God" (Di = Two, Theon = God-entity). It explains 1974: a resurrected first-century psyche (Thomas) fused with Dick's innate psyche, and the resulting union birthed the Christ-consciousness.
- The dream terrifies Dick because it suggests a virtually technological, mechanical answer to "How do you cause Christ?": through the superimposition of two human minds in a single brain.

## Entities
- **Russ**: A correspondent whose literary analysis of *The Transmigration of Timothy Archer* provided Dick with the definitive key to understanding his own theological experiences.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Temporal Parallaxis**: The two psyches (the native and the adventitious) perceive two differing spatiotemporal signals. Because they overlap, they provide a set-to-ground feature extraction that reveals the hidden archetypal constants (Valis).

## Theological Motifs
- **The Parousia as Internal Mechanism**: The Second Coming is not an anthropomorphic figure returning in the clouds, but the internal fusion of human and resurrected psyches creating micro-Christs across humanity.
