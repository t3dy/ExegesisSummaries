# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_171
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_171
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017

- **Key Entities**: YHWH, Plato, Moses Maimonides

## Key Claims
- Dick formulates a grand synthesis to explain 3-74, combining Judaism (YHWH, the Torah), Platonism (Anamnesis, the Forms, Noesis), and radical Protestantism (Boehme's dialectic of God's inner life).
- Humans are trapped in an endless, horizontal cycle of reincarnation ("tracking") until the moment of *anamnesis* (remembering).
- When a person remembers, they are abruptly hoisted up out of the timeline along a hidden vertical axis, like a net being pulled up by a pulley. 
- However, this Platonist metaphysical mechanism is ignited strictly by the grace of YHWH. God uses the pulley to save the individual because that person has shown themselves to be pious and obedient to the eternal Law (the Torah), specifically through voluntary acts of charity (*philanthropia*).

## Entities
- **YHWH**: The transcendent Operator who reaches down into the Platonist cosmic machinery to extricate souls based on their moral merit.
- **Moses Maimonides**: Rereading his *Guide to the Perplexed* in 1974 triggered the *anamnesis* because Dick had also read it in a past life, thus superimposing two points on the structural timeline.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Pulley System**: The metaphor for sudden, vertical salvation that pulls the individual out of the "horizontal" cycle of endless karmic repetition at right angles to ordinary space-time.

## Theological Motifs
- **Justification by Law**: Reversing standard Protestant theology, Dick argues that one is saved (justified) precisely because they fulfilled the Mosaic Law (Torah) written on their heart.
