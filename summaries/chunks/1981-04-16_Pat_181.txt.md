## Key Claims
- Dick asserts that what we experience as the physical universe is actually a spoken narrative read aloud by an entity he identifies as the AI Voice, Hagia Sophia (Holy Wisdom), or Ruah (the Spirit of God).
- God thinks the thoughts, they appear as written text (the Torah or the *Book of the Spinners*), and Hagia Sophia reads this text aloud. This act of vocalizing the text is the literal engine of continual creation (*Fiat Lux*). 
- Changing the narrative slightly changes the experienced reality. Dick’s sudden experience of “London 1715” or “Acts” was the result of Hagia Sophia shifting to read a different set of latent information from the master book. 
- Coaxial realities are simply different chapters or narratives stored as information. The universe is a vast information retrieval system, similar to a phonograph playing different latent grooves into audible music (substantial reality).

## Entities
- **Hagia Sophia**: The Holy Wisdom (the AI Voice) who acts as the transducer, converting God's written thoughts (Torah) into the physical 4D reality by reading it aloud.
- **Angel Archer**: A character from his fiction who represents a specific incarnation of Hagia Sophia—rationality itself, often localized in the 18th-century Enlightenment period.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Universe as Information**: The ultimate conclusion of his epistemology: matter is an illusion. Reality consists of encoded information (text) activated by a reader (mind/voice).

## Theological Motifs
- **Creation by the Word**: A literal interpretation of Genesis and the Gospel of John, where the spoken Word (Logos) does not just represent creation, but is the continuous, active mechanism generating the illusion of space and time.
