## Key Claims
- Dick asserts that Gnosticism is the correct theological model for the universe: specifically the concept of the *salvator salvandus* (the savior who must be saved). God/the Cosmogenitor fell into his own creation and generated the means for his own rescue.
- Anamnesis (restored memory) is useless unless it leads to a new, heroic, *ex nihilo* act where the individual overpowers the deterministic mechanism of the world.
- He sees his 10-volume meta-novel, culminating in *VALIS*, as a vast Gnostic tool designed to emancipate the Cosmogenitor from the world.
- He clarifies that "Tony" did not return from the dead; rather, Dick's consciousness traveled to the "other side" (the Mens Dei) where he encountered his own "phylogon"—his true foundational essence centered around the meaning of Tony.
