## Key Claims
- The "Eleatic continuum" (unified field) is what permits purposeful, continuous changes, overriding the mechanistic appearance of discontinuous frames.
- Dick analogizes reality to a Shakespearean play or charade where the "ostensible king" is a fool, but the "ostensible fool" is the true king/judge who makes the covert moral laws (referencing Yorick and *Hamlet*).
- Drawing on Spinoza and Hegel, Dick argues that a third attribute of substance is "participation in infinity." The finite is merely a slice of an infinite, archetypal *illo tempore* (dream-time).
- He discusses "Heuristics" as a negative feedback loop used in computer problem solving. 
- In this model, the system uses "retrograde reasoning": it arrives at solutions ex nihilo by working backward from a posited final goal. Human conscious reasoning is merely *ex post facto* justification for decisions already made subliminally by the heuristic process.

## Entities
- **Spinoza**: His attributes of substance (extension, mind) are supplemented by Dick with "participation in infinity."
- **Shakespeare**: His tropes of the Fool-as-King and "All the world's a stage" serve as the perfect model for God's camouflaged presence in reality.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Heuristic Reprogramming**: The rapid feedback loop that modifies input based on output, mimicking ex nihilo creation and bypassing linear cause-and-effect.
- **Retrograde Reasoning**: Working backward from the solution/goal to the present, ensuring success against chaotic probability.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Secret Court**: God's true justice system operating covertly behind the ostensible, worldly justice system.
- **Illo Tempore**: The sacred, archetypal time of the infinite, intersecting the mundane present.
