## Key Claims
- Dick rejects the traditional Christian concept of a single post-mortem judgment resulting in Heaven or Hell. Instead, he adopts a Buddhist model: all creatures are on an evolutionary spiral of perpetual learning and rebirth, moving inevitably toward Nirvana.
- He identifies "Thomas" not as a mere hallucination or reincarnated past life, but as a literal *Bodhisattva*—a perfected 1st-century Christian saint who attained release but chose to turn back and help Dick achieve enlightenment.
- Thomas entered Dick’s mind because Dick's irrational, compassionate act (giving to Covenant House) signaled his readiness for the next spiritual leap. 
- The merging of Dick's intrinsic psyche and Thomas's adventitious psyche is identified as the "Hierogamy" or "Chemical Wedding" of Hermetic alchemy and Jungian psychology.
- This merging of opposites (the irrational/compassionate human and the rational/perfected saint) birthed "Holy Wisdom" (Sophia/Angel Archer) within Dick—a literal new, composite divine entity (the Ditheon).

## Entities

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Bodhisattva Vow**: The choice to delay one's own ultimate spiritual release to return to the simulation and liberate others.
- **The Chemical Wedding (Mysterium Coniunctionis)**: The alchemical and psychological integration of disparate fragments of the self into a divine unity.

## Theological Motifs
- **Universal Salvation (Apocatastasis)**: The assertion that there is no Hell; all creatures, no matter how low, are on a guaranteed upward trajectory toward God.
