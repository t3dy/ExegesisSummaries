# Claudia
## concise_summary
Dick explores the "Lived Myth" (Joseph Campbell) and "Ma'at" (Right Order) as keys to his 1974 experience. He identifies the "Xerox Letter" as a ritual repudiation of materialism and a transformation into the "Archaic Ego" that repeats the passion of Osiris/Christ outside of linear time.
## key_claims
- "Lived Myth": The ancient ego was "open behind," repeating past archetypes to give them "presentness."
- 3-74 involved "Mythic Identification" with the death/resurrection of Osiris/Dionysos/Christ.
- *Ma'at* (Order/Truth) generates herself by using the space-time universe as an interface for ritual acts.
- Transubstantiation: God doesn't "step down" to man; He draws man "up" into His realm (the consecrated world).
- The "Xerox Letter" was an external focus for an internal threat: it revealed Dick's status as a "machine" (reification) and allowed him to repudiate it.
- During the "Possession," Dick was "no longer the man" but a ritualized enactment through which God (Ma'at) worked.
