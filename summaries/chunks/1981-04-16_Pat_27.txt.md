# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_27
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_27
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: The Vertical Spatial Axis, Return to the Medieval Cosmos, World as Exile, The Illusion of Causation
- **Key Entities**: Dante, Rudolf Otto, Martin Buber

## Key Claims
- Dick maps our situation to Dante's *Commedia*: Hell is total encapsulation (no world); our current reality is Purgatorio (a generated representation); Paradiso is experiencing God directly replacing the world.
- He realizes that in 1974, he "abreacted" out of the modern, linear-temporal world back into the intact, medieval Gothic worldview, which features a vertical spatial axis connecting Earth directly to Heaven.
- We live in a state of exile from the Garden. Our generated world is an oxymoron: the very fact that we perceive it as "world" means we are trapped by our own thought-formations coming back at us as Fate.
- By confronting annihilation (Heidegger's *Sein*), time stops, and the generated world gives way to the "wholly other"—a true encounter with God, breaking the solipsistic loop.

## Entities
- **Dante**: The *Commedia* is used as the exact architectural map of consciousness spanning from encapsulation (Hell) to mystical union (Paradiso).
- **Rudolf Otto**: His term "the wholly other" perfectly describes the breakthrough of true Reality dismantling the fake, self-generated world.
- **Martin Buber**: The shift from the generated "It" to the authentic "Thou" is cited as parallel to breaking out of the Maze.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Golden Fish Sign**: Re-evaluated as the precise trigger that broke into his private world from the outside, proving the existence of a true "other" and initiating the vertical ascent.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Sacred Body and Blood**: Dick speculates that under Malebranchian mechanics (where we and the world are *in* God), his vision of the universe may have literally been a vision of the Cosmic Corpus Christi.
