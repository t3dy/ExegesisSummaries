# The Inner Citadel: Christ's Salient into the Corpse
## concise_summary
Dick explores the Zoroastrian/Gnostic conflict between Hyper-universe I (Life) and II (Death). He views Christ as a "salient" (an invasive wedge) sent from I into the "corpse" of II to expand and destroy it from within.
## key_claims
- II as a Corpse (Premature Birth): Hyper-universe II (BIP) is an "unhealthy twin" that burst from the "egg" prematurely. It is a "deceitful corpse" that simulates life.
- The Salient (Christ): Christ (the Salient from I) allowed himself to be murdered in II (the 1st Advent/Passion) so he could "detonate" like a plasma and fill the whole of BIP II.
- The Heat Exchange of Love: In *Tears*, the "heat exchange" of Buckman's grief/love causes the BIP to "wither away." The inner citadel (Rome) has been penetrated by Agape.
- Two-Source World: Dick compares reality to Edwin Land's two-source color theory: one source is Life (Eros/I), the other is Death (Thanatos/II). The "admixture" is our finite, finite world.
- Zebra's Weeding: Zebra's current task is "weeding out" the spurious interpolations of II and "abolishing our memories" of them so we only see the emerging I.
- St. Sophia as Discorporate Ghost: The "soul" of I is currently present as a "discorporate spirit" (St. Sophia), waiting to be embodied.
- Dokos (Seeming): Hyper-universe II is "Dokos" (pure seeming/forgery). It possesses attributes but no "ontology" (being).
- God's Mercy as Pruning: The "pruning" of the vine is God's way of caring for the individual "cuttings" (us) to ensure we grow into replications of Christ.
