## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_08

- **Key Entities**: Michael Bishop, Cartesians, Heraclitus

## Key Claims
- Following the Cartesians, Dick realizes we experience a *representation* of the world, not the world itself. Thus, an evil demon or power could insert a false picture of reality (the BIP) into our minds.
- Therefore, Christ does not invade the universe; **Christ invades our perception of the universe**. The occlusion is in us, not in the world.
- He recalls a terrifying dream of "the Spinner," a child who goes blind staring at the sun due to heavy metal poisoning—representing humanity's fall and loss of spiritual vision.
- Dick theorizes that the Christian fish sign triggered anamnesis of *Track A* (the BIP / Acts / *Tears*).
- Track A is not the past; it is the "zero phase" sequence—the deterministic alternative present that the universe tentatively ran and then discarded. Dick somehow remembered this cancelled reality.

## Entities
- **Michael Bishop**: Dick references a letter he wrote to Bishop describing Gnosticism as "paranoid Cartesianism."
- **Heraclitus**: Cited for the concept that grasping true reality is like guessing a riddle or translating a foreign language—a capacity we have but fail to use.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Track A (Acts / Tears)**: The discarded, zero-phase Yin reality of strict causality. 
- **The Fish Sign**: The catalyst that removed the amnesia regarding the cancelled timeline.
- **The Spinner Dream**: A symbolic vision of the Fall and the loss of the "bright nature."

## Theological Motifs
- **Christ as Phagocyte / Physician**: Christ acts as a detoxifying agent, lifting the internal occlusion and restoring the original, paradisiacal vision.
