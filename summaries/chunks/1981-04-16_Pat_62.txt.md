## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_62

- **Key Entities**: Carl Jung, Dante Alighieri, Joachim of Fiore, Arthur Schopenhauer

## Key Claims
- Dick asserts that his *Valis* trilogy (*VALIS*, *The Divine Invasion*, *The Transmigration of Timothy Archer*) is a complete study of what Christ is and how he is generated. Christ (Sophia/Angel Archer) is the character common to all three.
- He clarifies that he *did not become Christ* in 1974. Rather, he and the adventitious psyche were combined like base metals in an *alchemical transmutation* (the Mysterium Coniunctionis), and the *resulting compound* was Christ (Ditheon).
- He establishes a strict hierarchy of 3 Ontological Levels corresponding to Dante's *Commedia*: 
  1) Android/Machine (Past/Inferno)
  2) Authentic Human (Present/Purgatorio)
  3) Ditheon/Christ (Future/Paradiso)
- Freedom consists of the human (the true self) triumphing over Schopenhauer's blind, impersonal "Will," which formerly controlled the empirical ego.

## Entities
- **Dante Alighieri**: His three realms explicitly map to Dick's evolutionary states of the soul.
- **Joachim of Fiore**: His three Ages map to Dick's timeline: Age of the Father (Machine), Age of the Son (Human), Age of the Spirit (Ditheon).

## Recurring Concepts
- **Spiritual Alchemy**: The realization that Hermetic alchemy was a coded method for combining mundane psychic elements (left and right hemispheres) to produce a divine entity (Gold/Christ).
- **Christogenesis**: The ongoing evolutionary process of building God out of human parts.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Chemical Wedding**: Framing Christian salvation/ensoulment in Hermetic terms as a fusion, rather than a mere moral correction.
