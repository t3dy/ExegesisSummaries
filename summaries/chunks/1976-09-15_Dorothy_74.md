# The "Faith of Our Fathers" Trap: The Reality of 3-74
## concise_summary
Dick identifies a startling 1:1 correspondence between his 1967 story "Faith of Our Fathers" and his 3-74 experiences. He concludes that the Xerox letter was a "phony test/trap" (using "party jargon") and that a secret ETI group is manipulating history to blow up the "Black Iron Prison."
## key_claims
- The "Faith" Parallel: In "Faith," a protagonist is given a drug by a "clandestine good group" to see reality as it is (escaping a state-induced delusion). This mirrors Dick's 2nd-74 fish sign/drug experience.
- The Phony Test: In the story, the hero is given two forged exam papers to test his ideology (the second is "faked up with party jargon"). Dick maps this directly onto the Xerox letters and the "Thomas" persona.
- Lived Before: Dick infers from these coincidences that he has "lived this life before" and his 3-74 was a re-run in which he succeeded (bypassed the trap).
- The Secret ETI Group: Agrees with Robert Anton Wilson that a secret group ("Illuminati/Rosicrucians/Gnostics") has been manipulating history from an ETI center (Sirius/Ajna).
- Zebra as the Ultimate Controller: This ETI (Zebra) can convert any person into a "computer terminal" (Theolepsy). This group is the "true, secret Christian church" dating back to Egypt.
- Minority Side Revolution: The "Illuminati" are not the secret rulers of the world; they are a *subversive minority* challenging the *overt* worldly dominion (The Prison).
- Freedom vs. Slavery: The ultimate issue is the emancipation of man from the "total visible ensemble of coercive governmental power" on this planet.
