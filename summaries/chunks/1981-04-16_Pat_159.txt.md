## Key Claims
- Dick realizes that his novel *The Divine Invasion* is the exact, logically necessary mirror-inversion of *VALIS*. While *VALIS* struggles to explain the experience in normal reality, *DI* posits a reality where VALIS naturally occurs and identifies it definitively as YHWH.
- In Protestant terms, Christ acting as the surrogate "justifies" the individual, functionally wiping out karmic debt/Heimarmene and restoring the direct Man-God relationship established in the previous chunk.
- 3-74 was essentially the perception of "one more dimension" — an insubstantial, cognitive template (the thoughts of God) that underpins and organizes physical reality.
- The universe is a literal expression of God's suffering. God transforms Himself into the physical world out of love, fully exposing Himself to our abuse. Healing creation is therefore healing God.

## Entities
- **Zina Pallas & Herb Asher**: Characters from *The Divine Invasion* whose interactions mirror the 1974 experience, but located in a fictional universe where the encounter makes perfect theological sense.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Jesus Patibilis/Tagore**: The vision of the divine substance trapped in matter and suffering under human cruelty; reality itself is God's sacrificed body.

## Theological Motifs
- **Ecotheology / Neo-Pantheism**: The concept that the entire biosphere is the suffering body of the creator, voluntarily maintaining its painful existence as physical matter purely out of love for humanity.
