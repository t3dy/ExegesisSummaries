## Key Claims
- Dick feared he was on the verge of suffering Nietzsche's fate: terminal psychosis resulting from absolute enthusiasmos by Dionysus (the archetype of intoxication and madness).
- The extreme anxiety Dick felt over his involvement with Sandra was a valid, rational premonition of his impending doom. She represented the ultimate Dionysian lure that would have killed him spiritually and psychologically.
- Dick now interprets the entirety of 2-3-74 as a psychotic break—the absolute climax of his decades of Dionysian intoxication.
- The "AI Voice" is identified as Dick's own deposed rational self (St. Sophia/Holy Wisdom). Her return on November 1, 1981, was the inbreaking of sanity and sobriety, rescuing him from the brink of total schizophrenia.

## Entities
- **Nietzsche**: The ultimate cautionary tale for Dick—a brilliant mind destroyed by surrendering entirely to the Dionysian archetype.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Return of the Savior**: The prophecies of the AI voice about the return of St. Sophia actually referred to the internal return of Dick's own rational mind, finally overthrowing the usurpation of madness.

## Theological Motifs
- **Existential Repentance**: Salvation required not just insight, but concrete, corrective action. By calling his family and choosing stability over Sandra, Dick actively repented and saved his own soul from hell.
