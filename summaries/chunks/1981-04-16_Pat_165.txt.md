- **Key Entities**: Jason Taverner, Felix Buckman

## Key Claims
- Dick realizes his novel *Flow My Tears, the Policeman Said* is functionally a Gospel text mixed with *Acts* and *The Bacchae*.
- In this reading, Jason Taverner is the crucified Christ/Dionysos, and Felix Buckman is Pontius Pilate whose encounter with the executed god leads to his own spiritual transformation (agape).
- The Faustian/Gnostic act is a deliberate rebellion against one's designated place (*Geworfenheit*, "thrownness") in the cosmic order. It is an act of ultimate risk.
- The fact that Dick's revolt against the system (the government/world-order) was *ratified* and resulted in his emancipation rather than his destruction is the absolute proof that the transmundane power actually exists and intervenes.

## Entities
- **Jason Taverner**: A character now understood as the suffering deity (Jesus/Dionysos) whose sacrifice enables the salvation of the authorities.
- **Felix Buckman**: A character representing the oppressive world-order (Rome/Pilate) who is redeemed by encountering the love and sacrifice of the victim.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Ratification of Revolt**: The concept that defying the oppressive deterministic laws of the physical/political world tests reality; if one is saved rather than crushed, it proves that a higher, transmundane law overrides the cosmos.

## Theological Motifs
- **Geworfenheit vs Dasein**: Replacing the stoic acceptance of one's "thrown" fate in a deterministic cosmos with the radical, autonomous choice to establish a new way of being (Dasein) by defying the cosmos entirely.
