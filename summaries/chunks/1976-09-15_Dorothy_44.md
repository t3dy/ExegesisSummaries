# The Incarnation as Hegel's Synthesis
## concise_summary
Dick reconciles his dualistic visions (J-J vs. Zebra) with Hegelian monism, arguing that the "Incarnation" (Christ/Zebra) is the fusion of the finite and infinite that abolishes the "sundering" of worlds. He interprets his vision of Zebra as the moment of "intervention" or "assimilation" into this world.
## key_claims
- Hegelian Elements: Reconfirms (1) Art/Religion/Philosophy triad, (2) God in history, (3) Progression toward freedom, (4) Dialectic revelation.
- The Incarnation (Fusion): Absolute Spirit (Zebra) is the "incarnate mind" that makes the infinite manifest in the finite.
- Intervention vs. Pantheism: Dick debates if Zebra was *always* here (Pantheism) or just *arrived* (Intervention). He concludes that 3-74 was a "decisive intervention" (breach).
- Nixon as the Broken King: Reaffirms Nixon as the "Master of Stratagems" from Daniel, whose fall was not by human hands but by the "Uncut Stone."
- Marxism vs. Hegelianism: Notes that Marxism is "Left Hegelianism" and that Marcuse would view the universe similarly, though Dick prioritizes the spiritual/divine "Absolute Mind."
- Transsubstantiation: God (Zebra) enters the world and "transsubstantiates" it, acting as an "efficient cause" that alters the linear flow of history.
- The theistic/theological choice: Rejects simple monotheism (transcendence) and simple pantheism (immanence) in favor of a "fusion" model where God *enters* the world to rescue it.
