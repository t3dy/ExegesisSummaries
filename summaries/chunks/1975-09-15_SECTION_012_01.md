# Jack Scovil (Agent) / Self-Research
## concise_summary
After a business letter regarding a lost $500 check, Dick pivots to a theological breakthrough: the Kingdom of God is located in the "Future," and he reached it by "running out of time" in March 1974. He identifies St. Sophia as the "Second Comforter" released from the limitations of Christ's earthly incarnation.
## key_claims
- Sequence of Redemption: (1) Elijah/Dick improves the world in March. (2) God accepts it in August. (3) St. Sophia returns as the instrument of redemption.
- Temporal Acceleration: The Kingdom of God is a future state at the end of time. Dick reached it by accelerating out of linear time.
- Time as individual rate: We move through time (like space) rather than being moved by it.
- Second Comforter: The Paraclete (Sophia) is the Logos released from human limitations, capable of cosmic-scale intervention that the incarnated Jesus could not perform.
- Sophia's Human Limit: As Jesus, Sophia lost her world-building capacities; the crucifixion released her to become the ubiquitous Holy Spirit.
- Christian Mystery: Immortality is not the end but a means to "more complete union with God."
