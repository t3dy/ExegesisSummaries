# Zebra: The Shy God of Mimicry
## concise_summary
Dick formally introduces the name "Zebra" for the mimicking, tutelary entity that camouflages itself as the phenomenal world. He defines Zebra as the "Deus Absconditus" (Shy God) who arranges causal chains from within to achieve justice, truth, and liberation.
## key_claims
- Zebra Defined: A higher life form, all-intelligent and plasmatic, that mimics phenomenal objects to intervene in our world.
- Ubiquitous Mimicry: The veil is not projected by us (Jungian) but is an "outside" camouflage used by Zebra to remain hidden while acting.
- Arrangement vs. Creation: Zebra does not create matter (the role of the blind J-J) but "arranges" it into "Noos/Kosmos" (wisdom/order).
- Steganographic Maintenance: Zebra uses coded messages in the "trash" (songs, ads) to maintain its own unity among its "phagocyte" parts (humans).
- The Shy God: Reinterprets the "Ikhnoton dream" (architect with claws) as a vision of the "Shy God" hiding behind the buildings.
- 3rd Eye Perception: Detecting Zebra requires a "depth sense" or "3rd eye" to see through the color-coded superimposed systems.
- 2-Player Game: Zebra (wisdom) is pitted against J-J (blind mechanism) in a trick-by-trick battle within the causal chains.
