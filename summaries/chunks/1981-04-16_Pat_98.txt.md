## Key Claims
- Dick has an "extraordinary revelation": his 2-3-74 experience cannot be viewed in isolation. It is the climax of an authentic 12-year religious cycle that began with a "ritual death" (mescaline and personal tragedy) in 1970.
- He maps this cycle onto his novels: *Flow My Tears* (crucifixion), *A Scanner Darkly* (death in the tomb), *VALIS* (sudden resurrection), *The Divine Invasion* (rebirth), and *BTA* (Christogenesis as Ditheon).
- The Gospels are not historical biographies but a "paradigmatic recipe" for how any human can become Christ. The Passion and Crucifixion are necessary prerequisites for the ultimate goal: the Nativity (the birth of Christos within the individual).
- He finds the ultimate "benchmarks of God" in absolute beauty, describing beauty as an "infinity of infinities" that proves the universe is the product of divine design.
