# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_92
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_92
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: The Assembly of the Divine, Sub-assemblies, The Plasmate, The Dialectic as Pattern Completion
- **Key Entities**: Pierre Teilhard de Chardin, Pythagoras, Jane Harrison

## Key Claims
- Dick links his Ditheon concept to Teilhard's Omega Point: the divine macro-organism (the Cosmic Christ) is quite literally assembling itself out of sub-divine, mundane, disparate pieces over millions of years of evolution.
- The "Ditheon Dream" (two human psyches joining to form Christ) is the micro-model for how the entire macro-universe operates: disparate parts converging to form a unified, conscious whole.
- The forces of Darkness (the BIP) are blinded to this because they only see the disparate parts and cannot understand how they will fit together; only the evolving intelligence (Valis) can see the final pattern.
- This assembly involves the mentalization and spiritualization of matter itself (the Noosphere). The universe is a "circularity seen," where the divisions into discrete objects are an epiphenomenal illusion.

## Entities
- **Teilhard de Chardin**: His concept of planetary evolution and the Noosphere provides the exact scaffolding for Dick's idea that Christ is an evolving macro-organism composed of sub-assemblies.
- **Pythagoras**: Referenced as someone who achieved the meta-abstraction when he heard the anvils struck, instantly perceiving reality as musical/mathematical structure.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Camouflage**: God/Valis is not hiding behind a fake world; Valis *is* the world's disparate parts, we just lack the perceptual apparatus (temporal parallaxis) to see how the parts connect into a single sentient brain.
- **The Plasmate**: Defined as the living information or nervous system that links the spatially separated constituents of the universe together.

## Theological Motifs
- **Transubstantiation**: Not a localized church ritual, but a global evolutionary process where the mundane world is progressively assimilated into the body and blood of the Cosmic Christ.
