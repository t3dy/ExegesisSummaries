# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_160
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_160
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: Cartesian Representationalism, Reality as Information about Christ, The Abolition of Time
- **Key Entities**: Malebranche, Anselm

## Key Claims
- The sacramental act (and the meta-abstraction) abolishes time, meaning all instantiations of the sacrament occur synchronously in eternal time. 
- The fact that the meta-abstraction accurately maps to reality confirms Cartesian Representationalism and Malebranche's proposition: we experience ideas and representations in the mind of God, not material objects in a void.
- Dick refines the statement "the universe is information" to explicitly state that the information *is about Christ*. By seeing idea, word, and object as one, the true Gestalt of reality is revealed to be Christ Himself.
- Scripture, specifically the Book of Acts, is not just a description of reality; it *is* the sacred reality itself existing in an informational mode. Therefore, when the world is correctly perceived, Christ is literally present in the arrangement of reality.

## Entities
- **Malebranche**: Dick confirms his philosophical model—since God is infinite, God cannot be represented by a finite idea, meaning any experience of God (VALIS) must be immediate, direct, and non-representational.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Meta-Abstraction**: Now fully equated with the sacramental miracle that abolishes the distinction between conceptual arrangement and physical reality.

## Theological Motifs
- **Scripture as Reality**: The Jewish cosmological concept that the Torah is not a text about the world, but the actual structural blueprint and continuous substance of the world itself, hiding the presence of Christ.
