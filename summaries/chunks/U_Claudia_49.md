# Claudia
## concise_summary
Dick identifies "Astraea/Virgo" (Justice) as the entity that healed his blood pressure and promised judgment by fire for the despoilers of Earth (Vietnam). He reinterprets reality as a "Two-Player Game" between God and an adversary, where God wins by having the "Final Victory" (laughing last).
## key_claims
- Astraea (The Virgin of Justice): The 3-74 entity identifies as a "Nameless God/Astraea" (Roman Virgin); she oversees justice and judgment.
- Judgment for Vietnam: Astraea promised "fire" for those who despoiled the Earth (specifically mentioning defoliation in Vietnam).
- Healing by Association: Dick’s blood pressure became normal immediately after the "Advent" of Astraea; proof of physical intervention.
- Life as a 2-Player Game: Reality is a series of "Player One" (The Adversary) serving bad scenes and "Player Two" (God) triumphing over them.
- Time as Sliced Cheese: Linear time is a "thin wire" cutting through an eternity (cheese) that contains both the past and the future.
- Future existing "Now": Future events are "unsubstantiated/real" (Minkowski block) in an unbounded universe surrounding our matrix.
