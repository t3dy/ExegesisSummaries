# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_09
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_09
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: Superimposition of Signals, Plato's Eide, The Body and Blood of Christ, Conceptualization
- **Key Entities**: Plato, Ludwig Wittgenstein, Martin Heidegger

## Key Claims
- Dick explains the 1974 experience as the superimposition of two signals (Track A/zero-phase and our current Track B/one-phase), causing him and "Thomas" to combine.
- The "plasmate" or living information only becomes visible when both channels are superimposed into a unified signal.
- The coaxial realities are not parallel dimensions from sci-fi, but rather plural sets of spatiotemporal accidents projected onto a common, eternal matrix: **Plato's Forms (Eide)**.
- The great binary, information-processing system is the Cosmic Body of Christ, and the information it processes is His blood.
- The barrier to seeing this is not physical blindness, but a lack of *conceptualization*—our brain's inability to organize the incoming representation into an adequate model (referencing Wittgenstein).

## Entities
- **Plato**: His eternal Forms (*eide*) are identified as the common matrix anchoring the coaxial alternate realities.
- **Ludwig Wittgenstein**: Referenced regarding the brain's necessity to construct an inner analog/model equal to the sensible perception.
- **Heidegger**: His concept of *Dasein* is invoked to explain how world can be "tuned" into different versions (*Ur-, Mit-, Eigenwelt*).

## Recurring Concepts
- **Coaxial Worlds**: Alternate presents that occupy the same space and share the same Platonic essence but are modulated differently.
- **The Information Analog**: The mental model one must painstakingly build over years to properly decode and retain the divine experience.

## Theological Motifs
- **Body and Blood of Christ**: Reinterpreted as the cosmic hardware (the binary choosing system) and software (the living information traffic).
