# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_146
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_146

- **Themes**: The Three Attributes, Infinity as an Attribute, The Error of Plato
- **Key Entities**: Jakob Böhme, Spinoza, Plato, Plotinus

## Key Claims
- Combining Kabbalism, Malebranche, and Spinoza, Dick formulates a three-attribute reality: Idea (Mind), Word/Writing (Physical Extension/Information), and Object (Infinity). All three attributes are equally and ontologically real.
- Plato's error was assuming the physical world (Extension) was only "semi-real" (a copy) because he missed the third attribute (Infinity).
- When the attribute of Infinity is introduced, the physical universe is simultaneously seen as the informational register ("writing" or language) of the eternal Object.
- The experience of VALIS was the apprehension of all three attributes at once, culminating in the realization that the physical universe is an informational expression of God's Mind.

## Entities
- **Jakob Böhme**: His vision of a dynamic dialectic operating *within* God is cited as explaining the evolving hypostases that Dick experienced functionally as VALIS.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Res Extensae vs Info**: The realization that the physical universe of extended matter is actually the second stage of the triad—it functions as the "Word" or informational code for the ultimate true Object.

## Theological Motifs
- **Deus sive Natura**: Spinoza's monistic concept that God and the natural universe are one single, self-generating substance (*Substantia*) expressing itself through multiple attributes.
