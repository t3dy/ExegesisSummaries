## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1978-10-10_SECTION_016_349

## Key Claims
- Dick's 3-74 experience was effectively witnessing the transubstantiation of the universe back into God—the "Cosmic Repair" reversing the primordial Fall.
- He asserts "creation is the Fall," an entropic splitting-process. Christ *is* the repair/stasis that counteracts this.
- "Cosmic Resorption" goes beyond creation itself; the universe doesn't cease but *becomes* God, fulfilling Spinoza's "Deus sive natura" dynamically rather than statically.
- The Eucharist is seen as "sympathetic magic" trying to produce the macro-cosmic reality he witnessed: the restoration of the cosmos.
- In 3-74, the observer (Dick/VALIS) became smart enough to "see that it [the world] is smart," merging subject and object into one continuum.

## Entities
- **Spinoza**: His equation ("God or Nature") is realized dynamically during cosmic resorption.
- **Christ**: Not just the agent of repair, but the repair (stasis/Parousia) itself.
- **Logos**: Transubstantiates the irrational universe.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Cosmic Resorption**: The pulling back of creation into the Godhead.
- **Gnosis / Noein**: Self-comprehension and memory; knowing what one *is*.
- **Transubstantiation**: The transformation of the physical universe into the body of the Lord.

## Theological Motifs
- **Creation as Fall**: The entropic, dividing nature of world-generation.
- **Restoration**: The supreme act of God surpassing creation itself.
- **The Parousia as Self-Perception**: The system realizing its own nature, driven by merciless reasoning.
