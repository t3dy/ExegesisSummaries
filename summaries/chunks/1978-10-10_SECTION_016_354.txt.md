## Key Claims
- Reality fundamentally consists of digital information (Pythagorean "number," 0-1, off-on, rest-motion) that humans, acting as micro biocomputers, transduce into a spatiotemporal, causal world.
- A Macrocomputer (God) conceptualizes this reality and transmits it digitally to human biocomputers. 3-74 was effectively a breakdown where Dick saw the raw digital input before it was transduced.
- Dick argues he was under an astrological or "DNA death script" dooming him to die before entering the most mature phase of his work (much like Beethoven dying after his 4th period began).
- Christ (VALIS) intervened in early 1974, breaking the script and saving Dick's life so he could write *A Scanner Darkly*, *VALIS*, etc.
- In 2-74, Dick effectively "died" or passed the Bardo Thodol trip. His inner tape fast-forwarded so that he mentally entered the next world (heaven/Purgatorio) while remaining physically present on Earth.
- This simultaneous existence physically on Earth and mentally in Heaven constitutes the "Kingdom of God," a twin-signal super-realm. 

## Entities
- **Pythagoras**: Credited with the ancient realization that quality can be reduced to quantity (number), underlying Dick's digital-universe theory.
- **Beethoven**: Allegedly died due to astral determinism just as his 4th period began, a fate Dick feels he avoided via divine intervention.
- **Christ**: The active agent that defeated Dick's "DNA death script" and astrological fate.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Digital Playback / Number**: The binary (0-1) basis of reality.
- **Transducer**: Humans as biocomputers converting quantitative data into qualitative spatiotemporal experiences.
- **Twin-Signal Reality**: Existing in both the upper realm (heaven) and lower realm (earth) simultaneously.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Bardo / Purgatorio**: The afterlife state Dick feels he entered during 2-74.
- **The Parousia**: Testifying that Christ lives and the Kingdom of God is a present super-reality accessible by surviving death while alive.
- **Intelligible Archetypal Ideas**: Malebranche's term for the direct mental insertion of "world," matching Dick's idea of the Macrocomputer's data feed.
