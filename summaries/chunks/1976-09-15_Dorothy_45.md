# Teilhard de Chardin and the Heart of Matter
## concise_summary
Dick finds a perfect theoretical match for 3-74 in the philosophy of Pierre Teilhard de Chardin. He sees Zebra as the "Cosmic Christ" who "immerses" himself in things to unify them organically, leading toward "Point Omega" (the consummation of the universe).
## key_claims
- Teilhard's Organic Unity: Christ unifies the world by becoming "element" and "assuming leadership" from within the "heart of matter" (Zebra's mimicry).
- Point Omega: The 3-74 experience was a synchronized moment where both the Universe (macro) and Dick (micro) reached "Point Omega" simultaneously.
- Consummation: "St. Sophia being born again" is the culmination of this immersion process where God becomes "all in all."
- Assimilation vs. Separation: Reconciles Transcendence and Immanence: God starts separate (Transcendent) and "assimilates" the world to himself via "insertion."
- The History of Insertion: The process began with the 1st Advent and is hidden because "she was not acceptable before." The 2nd Advent will complete the assimilation.
- Plasmatic Infiltration: Zebra uses a "plasmatic infiltration" (Transsubstantiation) to get inside objects and processes.
- Divine commander: Dick sees himself as a "cell" or "objct" of the historical process being led by Christ.
