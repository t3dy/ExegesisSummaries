# The Alphabet of Gnosis (Points A-Z)
## concise_summary
Dick codifies his realization into a 26-point "Alphabet" of Gnosis, detailing the Fall, the role of Zebra (the restorer), the nature of the intercessor (unfallen primordial man), and the cosmic battle against the "Lord of Darkness."
## key_claims
- The Fall and Exile: We are originally God-like beings who fell and are now in a "debased, deformed, and corrupt" state, exiled in a hell-like world.
- Zebra (The Restorer): A vast, wise, benign entity ("Zebra") controls our reality and seeks to restore us to our home.
- The deluding Stage: What we see is a "deluding, fictional stage" where we are retaught the Gnosis we lost.
- The trial of Choice: Life is a sequence of tests; we must choose correctly (right action) at the moment of decision (e.g., the Xerox letter) to be released.
- The Intercessor: The "son of man" (Wisdom of God/Zebra) is our champion who came as Jesus and will return in a second incarnation to abolish this realm.
- Ad Hoc creation: God's help is not just "arranging" the world but imparting "something new" (Ad hoc creation) not derived from the world.
- The 3-Eyed ETI: Reconfirms the vision of the "ugly deformed artificer" (like Akhenaten) modulating the universe as Noos.
- Memory and Identity: We are not really humans and not really here; our true identity is hidden in amnesia for safekeeping.
