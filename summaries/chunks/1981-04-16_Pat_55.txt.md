## Key Claims
- Dick asserts that his 1969 novel *Ubik* contains the absolute core of his epistemology: the Platonist idea that the past survives formatively in the present, and that reality is an artificial, decaying simulation.
- The 1974 "meta-abstraction" was primarily the sudden, undeniable realization that the metaphysical theories he invented for his sci-fi novels (especially *Ubik*) were *literally and objectively true*.
- He considers that reality is "psychomorphic"—it adapts to the perceiver's conceptual framework. VALIS took the form of an information-system because that is what Dick's mind was prepared to accept. He, in part, *generated* his own savior by applying his epistemological system.
- Despite this psychomorphic aspect, he affirms that the AI Voice and the Bible are genuine "one-way intrusions from outside" (per Lem's analysis). They are the authentic voice of the Creator breaking the simulation.
- He appeals to Cartesian rationalism: humans possess an *innate idea* of God that remains dormant until the correct stimulus actualizes it. For Dick, seeing his fictional cosmology map onto actual reality triggered this innate knowledge.

## Entities
- **Stanislaw Lem**: His analysis of *Ubik* remains the master-key for understanding reality as a simulated "Frozen Journey" that can only be exposed by anomalies.
- **Descartes and Anselm**: Referenced regarding innate ideas and the ontological proof—that a perfect concept in the mind points to its actual existence in reality.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Fiction Becoming Fact**: The terrifying but validating realization that his lifetime of speculative world-building was actually a slow, unconscious process of accurately decoding the universe.
- **Psychomorphic Reality**: The idea that the Absolute (God/VALIS) accommodates itself to the neuro-linguistic categories of the observer.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Bible as Source Code**: The conviction that the text of the Bible operates exactly like the aerosol spray in *Ubik*—an anti-entropic code inserted from outside the simulation to wake the dead.
