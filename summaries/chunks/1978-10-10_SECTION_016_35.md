# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 16-35

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 16-35
- **Themes:** Hyper-Time, The Great Rerun, Extrication from the Program, Postmortem stage.

## Key Claims
- **Time 3 (Hyper-time):** The computer's time is incredibly slow (2000 years = one present slot). Human time is "speeded up" for study/outcome testing.
- **Reprogramming the Winner:** In Track A (the BIP world), the police state won. The computer retracted and altered variables to create Track B (where the BIP is weakened).
- **Thomas' Memory Restoration:** The Golden Fish sign triggered the "resurrection" of the Track A Thomas personality to deal with the Track B consequences of *Tears*.
- **Postmortem Perspective:** In 3-74, PKD was lifted out of the program into the computer's time (Real Time), seeing the BIP as a trans-temporal constant.
- **The Judge's Verdict:** 1974 was the "Day God acted," judging the world of Track A and reassigning PKD to Track B to expiate his "plush indifference."

## Entities
- **The Computer (VALIS):** The programmer outside the simulation.
- **Thomas:** The "replaced self" from Track A, bourgeois and unaware of the cause.
- **PKD (Writer):** The Track B variable introduced to subvert the police state.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Koinos Time:** The objective shared time of the program (Time 2).
- **Hyper-time:** Real time/Eternity (Time 3), where Acts AD 45 is the present.
- **Astral Determinism:** The "closed system" requirement of the program; intervention (Grace) breaks this.

## Theological Motifs
- **Expiation:** PKD's broken life in Track B (poverty, anxiety) as payment for his comfortable life in the slave-labor Track A society.
- **Rescind/Rerun:** The divine process of altering history to avoid nuclear annihilation.
- **The "High Most Coil":** A reference to Thomas' cabin altitude/mountain retreat.
