## Key Claims
- Dick connects his horror at the burning victims of the Vietnam War (e.g., the Japanese soldier, Buddhist monks immolating themselves) to his horror at radioactive waste causing cancer. The Tagore dream beautifully unifies these two spiritual-emotional traumas.
- "Coaxiality" is identified as the great secret of all soteriological (salvation) systems, both Eastern and Western. It explains how orthogonal, value-reversed realities can occupy the exact same space.
- The combination of coaxial worlds and the meta-abstraction ("not two mothers once but one mother twice") provides the ultimate access key to the Pleroma.
- Dick believes no single religious system fully encompasses this vast discovery; Platonism and Gnosticism both grasp different facets of the coaxial truth.

## Entities
- **Plotinus**: Referenced for his concept of emanation and the One, though Dick notes Plotinus did not fully grasp the 180° value-reversal of the coaxial worlds.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Ditheon**: The fusion of two tracking psyches that allows the perception of the hyper-field AB (the true, combined reality).

## Theological Motifs
- **Finitum Capax Infinity**: The finite is capable of holding the infinite; the "Kingdom of God" is right here, stacked coaxially onto our spatiotemporal world.
