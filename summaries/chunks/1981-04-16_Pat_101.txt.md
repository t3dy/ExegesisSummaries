# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_101
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_101
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: Rabindranath Tagore as Krishna/Christ, The Third Dispensation, Ecological Crisis, Prophecy
- **Key Entities**: Rabindranath Tagore, Pierre Teilhard de Chardin, John the Baptist, Elijah

## Key Claims
- Dick introduces a major new vision concerning "Tagore" (a figure representing Lord Krishna/Christ), who comes to warn humanity about the ongoing destruction of the ecosphere.
- The dumping of radioactive nuclear waste is identified as the ultimate sin against the ecosphere. Wounding the ecosphere is literally crucifying God, because the ecosphere is the physical body of the Cosmic Christ.
- Dick views himself in the role of an Old Testament prophet, specifically the spirit of Elijah or John the Baptist, whose purpose in writing the VALIS trilogy was to prepare the way for this new savior/dispensation.
- The "Third Dispensation" fuses Christianity and environmentalism: we must cease destroying nature not merely for our own survival, but because nature is God incarnate, and our pollution is causing Him to suffer and die.

## Entities
- **Tagore**: A dream figure blending Lord Krishna and Christ, representing the spiritual savior who suffers voluntarily for the sins humanity commits against the ecosphere.
- **Teilhard de Chardin**: His concept of the Noosphere/Point Omega directly correlates with Tagore being the *anima* of the ecosphere.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Kerygma**: The new good news (warning) is that God has assimilated nature. To pollute it is to kill Him. 

## Theological Motifs
- **Voluntary Passion**: Like the Passion of Christ, Tagore voluntarily takes on the toxic burns of the ecosphere to awaken humanity to its crimes. If we do not stop, God will abandon the world.
