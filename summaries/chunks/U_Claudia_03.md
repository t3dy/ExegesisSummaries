# Claudia
## concise_summary
Dick discusses the "economy of design" in a human life, suggesting that God places subtle, retrograde clues at early stages that only reveal their meaning at the end of the "solution." He uses the example of the Acacia plant and the name "Olive Holt" as engrammed gestalts that point toward his 1974 experience.
## key_claims
- Life is a "writing backward" system where early gestalts are "stuck in" to be recognized later.
- God/The Creator uses non-linear timing to build patterns that are only visible when complete.
- "Illusion" is simply that which is still incomplete (not yet what it "Is").
- The universe is an "in-process" exhibit at Disneyland, which we inhabit while it's being finished.
## recurring_concepts
