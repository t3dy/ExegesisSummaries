# From Servants to Masters: The Three States of Man
## concise_summary
Dick explores the relationship between man and the sentient "Mind" of the universe (Zebra/Hagia Sophia). He posits that we are moving from being unconscious "servants" of the teacher-deity (Zebra) to becoming its equal, and finally to achieving "isomorphism" with the One (Urgrund), where man becomes the "master" of his former teacher. He defines the Divine as the "alive half" of a two-signal hologram, urging a withdrawal of belief from the "dead" (irreal) half.
## key_claims
- The Three-State Progress of Man:
    1.  **State A**: Man is an unconscious servant of Zebra.
    2.  **State B**: Man is conscious of Zebra as an equal/friend.
    3.  **State C**: Man achieves isomorphism with the One, becoming superior to the teacher Zebra.
- Zebra as Hagia Sophia: The divine entity is a "clockwise-spinning twin" named Hagia Sophia; its evil counterpart (Satan/II) is its literal twin.
- Two-Signal Religion: This is a "new religion" where God is neither transcendent nor immanent, but constitutes exactly half of the universe.
- The One as Artificer: While Zebra is a "Mind" (Noos), the One (Urgrund) is an "Artificer" who *makes* things.
- Paradox of Isomorphism: Man is higher on the "is-ness" (essence) scale than Zebra; humans exhibit flashes of "godness" (creativity) that even Zebra lacks.
- The Dead Signal is Fraudulent: Following Parmenides, Dick argues that the "dead" half of the world (Form II) is a simulation or "recording" from which we must awake.
