## Key Claims
- Dick analyzes *Flow My Tears* through the archetype of the old usurper king on the throne (who has illusory power) vs the young rightful king (who appears as a fool/madman but holds all true power). He connects this to Jesus before Pilate and Hamlet.
- Our world is a ritual drama or pageant designed to test the onlookers. If you make the correct moral choice despite being deliberately misled by the "usurper's" illusions, the masks drop, revealing the true state of affairs.
- The 2-3-74 event was an experience of absolute Justification (in the Lutheran/Pauline sense). Dick's philanthropic act led God to judge him as "saved," permanently breaking the power of fate and entering his name in the Book of Life.
- Coaxial World B (the hidden world) is actually the apocalypse—the continuous eschatological judging of souls, presided over by the Ancient of Days/Christ. 

## Entities
- **Jason Taverner & Felix Buckman**: Characters from *Flow My Tears* who enact the drama of power, conversion, and divine intervention, proving the novel was an inspired, apocalyptic text.

## Recurring Concepts
- **180° Mirror Opposite**: The ostensible ruling power is illegitimate and powerless; the persecuted "fool" is the legitimate, omnipotent ruler.

## Theological Motifs
- **Absolute Justification**: Once God justifies a person through Grace (Charis), it is a final, eschatological judgment that cannot be withdrawn or lost; it is the ultimate rescue from the eternal self-condemnation of Hell.
