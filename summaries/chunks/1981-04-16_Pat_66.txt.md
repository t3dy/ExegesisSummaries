# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_66
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_66

- **Key Entities**: Baruch Spinoza, Empedocles

## Key Claims
- Dick equates the meta-abstraction (seeing Acts hidden inside 1974) with the highest possible perception of the Eucharist. The physical universe is continually undergoing a macro-transubstantiation into the body and blood of Christ.
- He proposes a new model of physics/causation: Objects do not impact one another directly (no kinetic transfer). Instead, all objects are connected by an invisible "Third Thing" (a unified field), and changes are ripples of "valence" moving through this field. 
- Spinoza and the Occasionalists are wrong because the field itself is just "plastic matter" following natural law. VALIS is a sentient mind *outside* the field that perturbs it.
- This external mind presses the "Christ Story" onto the plastic reality field like a magnetic template structuring iron filings. We perceive invisible VALIS by reading the Christological perturbations in the world.

## Entities
- **Baruch Spinoza**: Critiqued because he leaves no room for a transcendent God perturbing the universe from the outside.
- **Empedocles**: His concept of *Krasis* (universal mixture driven by Love) is likened to the coordinated motion of reality.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Third Thing**: The invisible ontological link connecting all seemingly discrete phenomena, abolishing spatial multiplicity.
- **The Imposed Template**: The realization that the gospel drama does not emerge from history, but is stamped onto history from above.

## Theological Motifs
- **Macro-Transubstantiation**: Extending the miracle of the Catholic mass to encompass the entirety of the spatial universe.
