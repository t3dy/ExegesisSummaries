## Key Claims
- Dick refines his ontology moving away from strict Trinitarianism toward an Alexandrian/Philonic emanationist model: God $\rightarrow$ Logos (Blueprint/Idea) $\rightarrow$ The Forms $\rightarrow$ Spatiotemporal Universe.
- He maps this process perfectly onto John Scottus Eriugena's four stages of nature:
  1) Uncreated & Creates (God the Father)
  2) Created & Creates (Logos/Forms)
  3) Created & Does Not Create (The Physical Universe)
  4) Uncreated & Does Not Create (The Holy Spirit returning creation to God)
- The universe is not an idea in the mind of God; it *derives from the Logos*, which is the first thought of God. Christ (Logos) is the living information blueprint.
- Instead of the soul ascending out of the fallen world (Neoplatonism), Dick's model emphasizes that the Godhead descends to the lowest, most fallen level to rescue the soul and return it to unity.

## Entities
- **John Scottus Eriugena**: His *De divisione naturae* provides the exact theological framework Dick needs to explain the fall, the Logos, and the return of creation to God via the Holy Spirit.
- **Philo**: Credited with the intermediate Logos theology that anchors Dick's vision.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Word (Logos)**: Understood not as sound, but as the complex formula, schema, or blueprint on which physical reality is based.
- **The MMSK (Macro-Micro Symmetrical Key)**: The dialectic process of the Logos structuring and assimilating reality.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Salvific Descent**: Contrasting Christian grace (God comes down to the fallen) with Neoplatonist ascent (the soul must climb back to God).
