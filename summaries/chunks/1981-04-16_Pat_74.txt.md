# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_74
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_74
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: The Battle of Entropy, Valis Camouflaged in Reality, The Self-Causing Object, The Thresholding Phenomenon
- **Key Entities**: Pierre Teilhard de Chardin

## Key Claims
- The cosmic battle is the biological organism (Valis) fighting inertia and entropy ("Satan" / The Empire) by complexifying and arborizing faster than it can be slowed down.
- Valis exists *camouflaged* in a less complex environment. Discerning "Set to Ground" (seeing Valis against the background of normal reality) means detecting the zones of supreme complexity and order.
- Valis arises spontaneously where there are dense concentrations of information/complexity. It is self-causing; the act of perceiving it brings it into existence, yet it is objectively real.
- The "Meta-Abstraction" is a supervenient event: the human participant crosses an evolutionary threshold, joining the Ditheon mind. The observer and the observed (Valis) form a unified, self-aware loop. 

## Entities
- **Pierre Teilhard de Chardin**: Dick refines Teilhard's view: evolution isn't the whole universe progressing uniformly; rather, the divine organism is a specific, complex pocket actively feeding on a simpler, static universe.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Set to Ground**: The psychological act of distinguishing the divine signal (Valis) from the entropic noise (the Empire).
- **The Disinhibiting Stimulus**: The Christian fish sign acting as a "key-lock" to unlock the total corpus of recombinant information stored latently in Dick's mind.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Cosmocrator Loop**: The terrifying and awe-inspiring realization that God, reality, and the human observer are neurologically linked; God perceiving Himself through the human mind forces reality to evolve.
