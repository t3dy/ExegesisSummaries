# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_86
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_86
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: Dante's Three Realms, Joachim's Three Ages, The Law vs. Grace, The Machine Track
- **Key Entities**: Dante Alighieri, Joachim of Fiore, Paul the Apostle

## Key Claims
- Dick maps Dante's three realms (Inferno, Purgatorio, Paradiso) perfectly onto Joachim of Fiore's Three Ages (Father, Son, Spirit).
- The First Age/Inferno is the "machine track" governed by the rigid, deterministic Law (Torah). Paul the Apostle recognized that the Law is for machines who have no internal moral steering. 
- The Second Age/Purgatorio (Christ/Grace) introduces choice and free will, but choice is risky and many choose incorrectly, resulting in a fall back to the machine level.
- The Third Age/Paradiso (The Spirit) introduces Ditheon, where an internal adventitious monitor provides infallible, abstract guidance.
- Just as the New Testament (Age 2) is hidden as the secret spirit of the Old Testament, there is a Third Testament (the Plasmate) hidden inside the New Testament. 

## Entities
- **Dante Alighieri**: His *Divine Comedy* provides the hierarchical topography for the coaxial realities (Hell = BIP, Paradiso = Valis).
- **Joachim of Fiore**: His Trinitarian divisions of history explain the evolution of moral consciousness.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Coaxial Realities**: The three realms do not exist sequentially in time; they are overlaid "coaxially." A person's spiritual state determines which realm they currently inhabit.
- **The Black Iron Prison**: Explicitly identified as Age 1 / Inferno, the realm of absolute determinism without choice.

## Theological Motifs
- **Dialectical Winnowing**: The Age of Grace is a vast testing ground where individuals must choose rightly in ambiguous situations to escape the karmic machine world and earn the internal guidance of the Spirit.
