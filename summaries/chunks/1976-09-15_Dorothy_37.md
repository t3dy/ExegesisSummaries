# Whitehead and the Process of Zebra
## concise_summary
Dick continues his philosophical integration, finding that A.N. Whitehead's metaphysics perfectly describes "Zebra." He accepts the idea of God as an "arranger" rather than a creator, and as an entity "involved in process" rather than timelessly transcending it. He sees himself as a "cell" within this cosmic organism.
## key_claims
- Whitehead's God: Zebra is the Whiteheadian "God" who provides "initial impetus" and "repeatable patterns" (eternal objects) but does not create actual entities (that's J-J).
- Process Metaphysics: The universe consists of "becomings"—a process of integrating the past into novel unities. Dick's vision of the "parts-pile" was this process in action.
- The Organism: The universe is a "vast animal" or organism; God is the "Nous" of this totality. Humans are "colonies of monads" (Leibniz) within this organism.
- Transsubstantiation: God "pervades" his own body (the world) at every level (Transsubstantiation), which Dick saw as objects coming alive with luminosity.
- Selection aiming at Good: God acts as a "principle of selection" aiming at the realization of the good in the world process.
- Conscious Purpose: The fact that Zebra *spoke* to him proves that this process is not a mere force but a "conscious purpose" that selected him.
- History as Evolution: Evolution has transitioned from the biological realm to the "evolution of man within his social/historical context."
