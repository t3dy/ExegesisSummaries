## Key Claims
- Dick solidifies his Taoist understanding: the cosmos consists of Yang and Yin (two coaxial worlds). His self-individuation is an alchemical "hierogamy" (wedding) of Yin and Yang.
- He hypothesizes that the Tao only intervenes when the system veers too far off balance, exactly as Plato describes the demiurge acting in the *Timaeus*. This explains the intervention against Nixon in 1974.
- VALIS wasn't just a static perturbation; it was an influx of information from the *future* affecting the past, creating a positive feedback "runaway." The introduction of novelty breaks the causal determinism of Heimarmene.
- He sees a parallel with Frank Frink from *The Man in the High Castle*, who inexplicably acts to save himself. Such actions only make sense when viewing reality as a total unified field where the future can causally loop backward into the present.

## Entities
- **Frank Frink**: A character from *The Man in the High Castle* whose spontaneous, seemingly causeless act of saving himself exemplifies the Taoist concept of an integrated feedback field.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Two Coaxial Worlds**: Described here as the immutable cause-and-effect Yin world layered symmetrically with the dynamic, rational Yang world.

## Theological Motifs
- **Positive Feedback Runaway**: An eschatological process where a tiny influx of future information triggers an expanding loop of self-awareness and mutual adjustments, ultimately resulting in the Kingdom of God overwhelming temporal causality.
