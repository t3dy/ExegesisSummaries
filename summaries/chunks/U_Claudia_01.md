# Claudia
## concise_summary
Dick elaborates on the "Kingship of God" as a subtle, behind-the-scenes seizing of power. He uses metaphors of rescued animals and a "Lord of the Flies" scenario to explain the intervention of Truth and Justice in a world previously ruled by tyrants.
## key_claims
- The "Kingship of God" has already subtly arrived, lifting an oppressive fear.
- This world is now under the control of Truth and Justice, though their effects unfold in time.
- Reality is a "concentric-ring society" where the innermost ring (darkness) has been replaced by light.
- Elijah played a role in "shaping up" those to be saved before the judgment.
## recurring_concepts
- Kingship of God / Kingdom of God
- Truth and Justice as reigning spirits
- Concentric-ring reality
- Anamnesis (becoming conscious of forgotten origin)
