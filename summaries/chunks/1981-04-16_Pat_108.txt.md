## Key Claims
- Dick applies pure Gnosticism (via Hans Jonas) to the Tagore vision: The toxified ecosphere is the physical prison. Tagore is the divine *pneuma* trapped within it. The "burns" symbolize the suffering of the pneuma in the material world.
- He views his own 8-year labor on the Exegesis as the deliberate, successful methodological effort to unmix and extricate his spiritual self (Tagore/pneuma) from the earthly mixture of Light and Dark.
- He realizes Tagore is *not* God the creator (Valis). Tagore is the crown of creation—the absolute perfection of a finite human being. He is the *result* of the divine driver, analogous to the Buddha.
- Tagore’s spiritual perfection is defined not primarily by intellect, but by an "ineffable sweetness" that transcends even love and beauty. He proves that the purpose of the universe is for the finite to reach this perfected state.

## Entities
- **Hans Jonas**: His scholarly writings on Gnosticism perfectly explain the mechanism of the pneuma's reluctant withdrawal from a toxic material universe.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Separation of the Sparks**: The apocalyptic moment when the divine fragments are identified, separated from the dark, and extracted from the world before its destruction.

## Theological Motifs
- **Salvator Salvandus**: The Gnostic concept of the "saved savior"; the pneuma that remembers its divine origin, becomes unmingled, and is thus able to ascend, leaving the dead world behind.
