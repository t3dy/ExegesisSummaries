## concise_summary
Dick concludes Section 15 by reinforcing the idea that a "superself" (Thomas) was awakened in him by an "external-to-me symbol" (The Fish). This reawakening was part of a "coordinated sequence of events" to radically deflect human history and end the "pseudo-government" of Nixon.
## key_claims
- Thomas as Superself: Thomas is a "dormant superpersonality" that can perceive the divine universe. He "shut down" after the Apostolic age and was "re-born" in 1974.
- Nonvolitional Programming: Humans can be "programmed" to perform complex acts (like the Xerox missive) upon "Their" return. Choice is irrelevant until the "spear is thrown."
- Fifth Columnist: Dick identifies as an "unknowing Fifth Columnist." His writing identified him to "They" (the rulers) and "They" (the authorities).
- The House Hit: Dick suspects the 11/71 robbery was a "national security" operation by authorities trying to find evidence of his link to the "ETI/Secret Church" underground.
- Valis as Superior Life Form: Valis is not God in the traditional sense, but a "superior life form" that manipulates reality and history.
- Benign/Protective Power: Despite their vastness and the fear they induce, "They" are "terribly protective" and benign.
- Restoration of Order: The reversal of the "primordial rebellion" has begun. Rightful order between Man and God is being restored through this covert takeover.
