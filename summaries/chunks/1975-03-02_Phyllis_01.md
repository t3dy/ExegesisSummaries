## concise_summary
A personal letter to Phyllis Boucher accompanying an essay about Tony Boucher. Dick discusses his current life in Fullerton, his family, and his religious development.
## key_claims
- Dick has developed "strongly religious convictions" that God ("Our Good Friend") intervenes to save those who fall behind.
- *Confessions of a Crap Artist* is science fiction "in the broadest sense."
- Dick refuses to "deny s-f" like Kurt Vonnegut.
