# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_82
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_82
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: Atman and Brahman, The Ubiquitous Self, Ditheon as The Cosmic Christ, The Transmigration of Timothy Archer
- **Key Entities**: Shankara (Sankara)

## Key Claims
- Dick discovers that Advaita Vedanta (Shankara) provides the precise framework for Ditheon: we have an empirical ego, but underneath it is "Atman" (pure consciousness), which is identical to "Brahman" (the absolute ground of reality).
- The meta-abstraction is therefore seeing Shankara's *Tat Tvam Asi* (Thou art that). The "one mother seen twice" realization applies to the inner self and the outer world sharing a single conscious essence.
- In *The Transmigration of Timothy Archer*, the transferable psyche joining Bill's mind is not just a human ghost, but Christ (the ubiquitous divine unitary psyche). Thus, Ditheon is the human psyche + God.
- "Valis" is Brahman, seen externally, camouflaged as the universe of plurality. Recognizing it breaks the fetters of Karma and time, achieving Moksha.

## Entities
- **Shankara**: His non-dualism perfectly articulates Dick's realization that there are not plural selves, only one ubiquitous Self.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Transferable Psyches**: The mechanics of Ditheon involve a divine psyche overlaying a human one, allowing temporal parallaxis and the breaking of causality.
- **Moksha**: Sudden realization/enlightenment that frees one from the karmic wheel.

## Theological Motifs
- **Acosmism**: The belief that the physical universe is ultimately unreal compared to the single divine reality (Atman/Brahman).
