# The Sui Generis Moment: Identification by Spark
## concise_summary
Dick argues that salvation requires a "sui generis" act of individual refusal that identifies the "divine spark" within a person to the celestial powers. He views'74 as a general "time quake" or "orthogonal invasion" that abolished certain pasts and replaced them with a new "Fullerton" track.
## key_claims
- The Sui Generis Act: One must act in total ignorance (the "Test") to refuse evil with no promise of reward. This "identifies" the divine spark within the mixture.
- Celestial Identification: God doesn't save everyone; He saves those who "identify themselves" by acting out their divine nature (e.g., the act of "balking").
- The Ignorant Test: To pre-know the nature of the test (Judgment) would be to fake it. True divine nature surfaces only when there is "no reason" to do good.
- Time Quake / Orthogonal Invasion: 3/20/74 was a "general historic intervention" (the Stone uncut). It was Christ's birthday and a massive shift in the "punched tape."
- Abolished Worlds: Speculates that "Thomas" came from a world that was just "abolished" and replaced by the current "Fullerton" track.
- Imperviousness to Stimuli: The "Thomas" personality was essential because it was "emancipated"—untouched by the deterministic "disinhibition stimuli" of this world.
- The 2 birds image: References the Hare Krishna image of the bird eating fruit vs. the bird witnessing (Atman/Brahman).
- Redemptive Writing: Dick's novels paved the way for the work of the redeemer by highlighting the "irreality" of the deterministic world.
