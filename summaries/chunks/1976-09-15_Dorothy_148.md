# 1976-09-15_Dorothy_148
## Chunk Description
Analyzes the scene in *Flow My Tears* with the Black man as a "master cypher" and discusses the "firing" of meta-circuits in the brain.

## Key Claims
- **The Gas Station Scene:** The scene where General Buckman (authority) hugs a Black man (the "other") is a total reversal of power structures; the Black man becomes a "father figure" and the police official a "helpless child."
- **Anathema to Authorities:** This scene is "absolute anathema" to the BIP/Police because it penetrates their secret self-image and undermines the bedrock of their authority.
- **Meta-Circuit Firing:** 3-74 was the disinhibition of "2nd stage meta-circuits" in the brain that could only fire after 1st stage tasks (publishing *Tears*) were complete.
- **Supratemporal Secret Christians:** Authentic Christians aren't in any one "when"; they span from A.D. 50 to the future in one continuous lifetime (The Fish Christians).
- **Tears as "Acts":** *Flow My Tears* is not just *like* the Book of Acts; it *is* a printout of the same eternal truth, a "ubiquitous contemporality."

## Entities
- **General Buckman:** The police official, representing the fallen power structure.
- **The Black Man:** The father figure/instructor at the gas station.
- **St. Paul:** Linked to the inner logic of *Tears*.
- **The Girl with the Fish Necklace:** The necessary trigger/stimulus for the meta-circuit firing.
- **Philip (Apostle):** Linked to the encounter with the Ethiopian (Black man) in *Acts*.

## Recurring Concepts
- **GABA-blocked circuits:** Inhibited neural pathways containing divine information.
- **The XOROX Missive:** A weapon of the "adversary" (Satan) fired in rage at the disclosure in *Tears*.
- **Hierarchy of Authority:** The "Ins" vs. the "Meek."

## Theological Motifs
- **Stupendous Reversal:** The meek being elevated and the powerful brought low (The Kingdom of God).
- **Supratemporality:** The immortality of the apostolic secret brotherhood.
- **Divine Inspiration:** The "accidental" genius of the author as a vessel for the divine message.
