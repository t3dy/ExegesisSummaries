- **Key Entities**: Dante, Descartes, Malebranche

## Key Claims
- Dick categorizes Dante's three realms as epistemic states regarding the knowledge of the "Other":
    - **Inferno** (Lower Realm): Total atomization, isolation, and solipsism. Beings here treat all others as objects ("It") and possess no empathy. This is the state of the "android" or psychopath.
    - **Purgatorio** (Middle Realm): The realization of Empathy and *Agape*. The self-other barrier fluctuates, recognizing the "You." The dualism is mitigated but not dissolved.
    - **Paradiso** (Upper Realm): Absolute knowledge that Self and Other are one, representing total unity and the collapse of solipsism entirely.
- The leap from Inferno to Purgatorio (gaining empathy) cannot be achieved via rational deduction; it must be "inserted" into the mind via the grace of God.
- "Androids" are not inherently evil, but tragically deprived: God has not gifted them the revelation of the Other, leaving them in the absolute prison of Inferno. 

## Entities
- **Descartes**: His premise that one can only truly know one's own existence is identified as the defining characteristic of Inferno (the Android condition).
- **Malebranche**: Providing the antidote to Descartes, Malebranche asserts we can know the Outer world because "we see all things in God," requiring divine aid to cross the self-other barrier.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Android**: Redefined not as a biological machine, but a soul trapped in the absolute solipsism and lack of empathy characteristic of Dante's Inferno.

## Theological Motifs
- **Agape as Epistemology**: Empathy (*agape*) is not just an emotion; it is the ontological substance of God. Experiencing empathy is directly participating in the Divine nature.
