# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_135
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_135
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: The Illusion of Space and Motion, The Moral Nature of Reality, Hell as Absolute Self-Condemnation
- **Key Entities**: Kant, Sandra, Honor Jackson

## Key Claims
- Space ("the void") does not exist; assigning reality to it is humanity's fundamental cognitive error. Motion is also illusory—it is merely a sequence of static states that "look different," giving the false impression of passing time.
- Dick recalls nearly succumbing to a "Faustian" sin (his affair with Sandra), viewing it as introducing chaos and disruption into the cosmos. He recoiled in horror when he realized the moral laws of the universe are built into its physical structure.
- Hell is defined as the moment of absolute, divine illumination where one is forced to comprehend exactly how their sinful acts have disrupted the harmony of the cosmos, leading to inescapable self-condemnation.
- The drive to create chaos and destroy existing structures (associated with Sandra and Anne) borders on nihilism and absolute malice, directly opposing the accrual and stability Dick requires.

## Entities
- **Sandra**: Dick's former lover, whom he now views as an agent of Dionysian disorder and chaos, actively seeking to inflict maximum disruption on Dick's carefully built life.

## Recurring Concepts
- **To On vs To Den**: The contrast between True Being (To On) and The Void/Nothingness (To Den). Dick argues the void has no properties, and thus reality is a solid block of being.

## Theological Motifs
- **Absolute Moral Insight**: The divine light that reveals the true consequences of one's actions. It brings total understanding of one's sins, which is far worse than mere guilt.
