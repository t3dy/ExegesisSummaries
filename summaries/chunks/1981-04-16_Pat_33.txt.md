# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_33
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_33

- **Key Entities**: John the Baptist, Jesus

## Key Claims
- Dick identifies the conferring of the soul as exactly what the New Testament means by being "born in the Spirit" or the "second birth."
- He adopts an "adoptionist" Christology: just as the Holy Spirit descended on Jesus at his baptism, adopting him as the Son of God, the Holy Spirit can descend on appropriate human "hosts" to generate new *Christoi* (plural Christs).
- The union of the human host and the Holy Spirit is a *Hierogamy* (sacred marriage), resulting in an immortal, composite entity that is part human and part divine. 
- He interprets the "Parousia" (Second Coming) not as Jesus returning from the sky, but as the Holy Spirit returning to generate *Christoi* within individual humans here on Earth.
- Dick realizes this theology perfectly explains elements hidden in his own novels (like *The Transmigration of Timothy Archer*). 

## Entities
- **John the Baptist**: Quoted regarding the coming of One who baptizes with the Holy Spirit and with fire.
- **Jesus**: Viewed here not as the exclusive Son of God, but as the paradigmatic *first* Christos, the model for subsequent composite human-divine beings.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Interspecies Symbiosis**: The biological/sci-fi metaphor for the theological mystery of the Holy Spirit merging with a human mind (the homoplasmate).
- **The Golden Flower / Mysterious Embryo**: Taoist alchemical terms Dick uses as synonyms for the immortal soul generated by the Hierogamy.

## Theological Motifs
- **Sons of God as Co-heirs**: The radical assertion that the goal of Christianity is for humans to literally become Christs, equal to Jesus through the indwelling of the same Holy Spirit.
