# The Internal Theophany and the Microscopic Kingdom
## concise_summary
Dick refines his view of theolepsy as an "Internal Theophany" where the immanent God in the "Atman" is proved identical to the Brahman without. He defines the Kingdom of God as our ancestral "Garden" home, currently invisible but reachable through the restoration of our original sight/faculties.
## key_claims
- Internal Theophany: 3-74 was a breakthrough of the immanent God (Brahman) within his own station, not a transcendent descent from the sky.
- The Razor Case: God is concealed in the world (and in Dick) like a razor in a case—invisible but present.
- Ubiquity: No objects are as close to us as God, who is the "ground of being."
- The Kingdom as Home: The Kingdom is the "original Garden" we were cast out of; it is our ancestral home where we are no longer "strangers."
- Gnostic Alienation: Ratifies the Gnostic theme that we are "strangers here" from a "far better elsewhere."
- Microscopic Analogy: God's presence is like microbes in pond water—invisible until the microscope (restored sight) is applied.
- The Coming St. Sophia: The words (prophecy) will be followed by the savior Himself, ending the rule of James-James.
