# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_133
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_133
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: God as Interface, Epistemology vs Grace, The Transfer of Knowledge
- **Key Entities**: Nicolas Malebranche, Kant, Socrates, Jesus

## Key Claims
- We do not experience the objective world directly; God acts as an interface/lens between us and reality. God selectively enhances and suppresses information to precisely curate our individual experience of the world.
- Dick's satoris (from the high school beetle epiphany to the 1974 revelations) could not have originated from his own mind or the ostensible objects themselves. They were transfers of knowledge from the Interface (God) via Grace.
- The 2-74 meta-abstraction was the sudden realization of this selecting Interface. Dick recognized that "world" was less the issue than the divine medium filtering it.
- Because true knowledge requires the lifting of an occlusion by the Interface, humanity cannot reason its way to truth. Epistemology ultimately collapses into theology: we are totally dependent on God's Grace to see reality.

## Entities
- **Nicolas Malebranche**: Cartesian philosopher whose theory ("we see all things in God") validates Dick's realization that we experience God directly, but the world only indirectly as representations.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Satori**: Moments of profound understanding (e.g., empathizing with a dying beetle, knowing Chrissy's birth defect) that are entirely dependent on divine grace, not rationality.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Interface**: Valis is the divine mechanism positioned between the human mind and external reality, modulating the "reality field" to communicate specifically with the observer.
