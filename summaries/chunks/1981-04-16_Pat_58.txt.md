## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_58

## Key Claims
- The merging of the two psyches is an extreme biological survival strategy. Facing death (thermal equilibrium/predictability), the organism deliberately allows itself to be invaded by its environment in order to acquire its massive negentropic energy.
- Dick contrasts this with the schizophrenic strategy: withdrawing from reality due to info-depletion, which only accelerates the organism's deterioration and death.
- By swallowing a massive "hunk" of reality, Martin Buber's "I-Thou" relationship (the encounter with the radically Other) is internalized. The boundary between organism and environment is erased.
- Crucially, the "invader" (the adventitious psyche) is recognized as *human*, making it isomorphic to the host. Because it is of the same kind, it can be assimilated without annihilating the host, resulting in a dual-center consciousness.
- This psychological/biological process is identical to the theological "Baptism in the Spirit," which executes the transfer from the Empire of Darkness to Christ's Kingdom—a total structural completion, not just a pardon for sin.

## Entities
- **Martin Buber**: His "I-Thou" concept of existential relation is applied to the two psyches now inhabiting a single brain.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Info-Scarcity vs. Massive Incorporation**: The dichotomy between withdrawing from the world (death) and radically swallowing the world (life/Ditheon).
- **Isomorphism**: The necessity that the savior/invader be human (Thomas) so that the host can achieve union by similarity while benefiting from the differences.

## Theological Motifs
- **Completion vs. Exculpation**: Re-emphasizing that Christian salvation is an ontological upgrade (ensoulment), not merely a legal acquittal.
