## Key Claims
- Rudolf Otto's comparison of Shankara and Meister Eckhart validates Dick's experience. Eckhart's "fourth level of the soul" (the *scintilla* or divine spark) allows a person to see things abstractly as they pre-exist as forms in God's intellect.
- The realization of Atman-Brahman requires a higher rational faculty (intelligence), not just emotion. Order, intelligence, and abstraction are directly proportional.
- The cosmic dialectic is a self-winding clock that constantly accrues a negentropic surplus. This takes the form of reticulated, arborizing complexity (Boehme's *sic/non*).
- Seeing the world abstractly places one in the "antechamber" of experiencing God, reversing the ontological fall and returning multiplicity to unity.
