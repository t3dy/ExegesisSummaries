# The Ham Sandwich Model of Reality
## concise_summary
Dick develops the "ham sandwich" model of reality, where layers of fake/spurious reality are interspersed with the real/divine world. Zebra is described as a "mimic" that disguises itself as material objects to insert divine influence into the system.
## key_claims
- Spurious vs. Real: Most of what we see is a "spurious" world, but the "real" world is hidden within it.
- Mimesis: Zebra uses mimicry to bypass the internal filters of the artifact, appearing as common objects (like the Fish sign).
- The Ham Sandwich: A metaphor for the layered, contradictory nature of perceived existence.
- Divine Transubstantiation: The process where the fake is replaced by the real without the "authorities" noticing.
