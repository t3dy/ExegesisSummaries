# The Teaching Machine: Ethics as the Key to Emancipation
## concise_summary
Dick identifies the world as a "Teaching Machine" created by a "sick/fevered" demiurge (James-James). He argues that ethical choices (balking) are the only way to "grade" out of this simulation and return to the "Sane God."
## key_claims
- Sick Demiurge: James-James is not evil but "fevered," creating out of sheer restless creativity without regard for his creations.
- The Universe as Anomie: This world lacks a "steersman" (justice); it is a disorderly void where everything collides by chance (Ananke).
- The Teaching Machine: The universe is a puzzle designed to test our ethical responses. We are "graded" and "emancipated" based on what we find objectionable.
- Ethical Balking: The first step to seeing the "ersatz" quality of the world is to refuse to cooperate with its unfairness (e.g., the Ramparts strike).
- The Advocate: Once we "balk" against the world, the Advocate (Holy Spirit) manifests to reveal the reality of the Gnostic "divine spark."
- Transubstantiation of the World: 3-74 was the "Sane God" (Zebra) penetrating the "Sick Cosmos" to provide a clue for return.
- Repudiation = Healing: The "volitional rupture" with the world heals the "primordial rupture" with God that caused the fall.
