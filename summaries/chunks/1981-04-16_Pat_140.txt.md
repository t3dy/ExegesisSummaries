## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_140
- **Section ID**: SECTION_017
- **Themes**: Ecotheology, The Christic Institute, Jesus Patibilis, The Plasmate as Information
- **Key Entities**: Pierre Teilhard de Chardin, Karen Silkwood

## Key Claims
- Dick discovers the "Christic Institute" (a real-world Catholic organization involved in the Karen Silkwood case), validating his synthesis of radical political action, eco-theology, and Christian eschatology.
- The 2-3-74 experience was a vision of Teilhard de Chardin's Point Omega: the Cosmic Christ (Jesus Patibilis) assimilating the entire ecosphere and transforming the universe into His own body.
- This "neo-pantheism" extends Christ's presence beyond organic life to include the electronic environment, printed media, and "trash." The universe is an evolving, thinking entity perfecting itself into high-order information (negative entropy).
- Malebranche provides the philosophical "how" (God seen in all things as representations), while Teilhard's ecotheology provides the "what" (Christ as the Plasmate actively assimilating reality).

## Entities
- **Pierre Teilhard de Chardin**: Jesuit priest and paleontologist whose concept of the Cosmic Christ evolving the universe toward "Point Omega" perfectly mirrors Dick's Tagore vision of the Plasmate.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Jesus Patibilis**: The Manichaean concept of the suffering Christ dispersed throughout all of nature, sharing the pain of creation.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Word as the Thing**: In God's intellect, the idea, the word, and the act of creation are identical. The universe is literally written into existence (as stated in the Sepher Yezirah), which explains how the Plasmate can consist of living information.
