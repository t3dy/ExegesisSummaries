# U_Tony_02
# SECTION_003
# [Undated, early 1975]
# Tony Hiss
## concise_summary
Conclusion of the Boucher memorial and follow-up notes. Dick recounts a mystical experience of light, a premonition of death, and a dream involving Gestapo agents.
## key_claims
- Linear time is "accumulative" and superimposes rings of experience.
- Dreams (the "analog or right hem computer") help register these superimpositions.
- Death is an unavoidable hunter from which there is no hiding.
## recurring_concepts
- Accumulative/Linear Time
- Orthogonal Time (implied by "superimposition")
- The "Right Hem Computer" (Neuroscience)
- The Angel of Death

## autobiographical_events
- Vision of a "uniform pale light" filling the room four nights before Pinky's death.
- Prayer in Latin while "the angel of death" seemed to be present.
- Dream of a woman dying from a shotgun blast.
- Recurring dream of riding a trolley with Gestapo agents.
