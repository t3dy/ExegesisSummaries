## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_132

- **Themes**: The Dialectic within God, Logos vs. Prerational Will, Hegel's Absolute Spirit
- **Key Entities**: Jakob Böhme, Hegel, Schelling, Angel Archer

## Key Claims
- The "Two Source Cosmogony" in *VALIS* is actually a representation of an internal dialectic *within* the mind of God (as first envisioned by Jakob Böhme), not a battle between God and Satan.
- The dark side of this dialectic is God's prerational/irrational Will; the bright side is God's reason (Logos/Hagia Sophia). The Logos side always wins the synthesis.
- Dick's 2-74 meta-abstraction was his personal experience of this synthesis. It was Hegel's "Absolute Spirit" resolving the dialectic within Dick's mind, producing sanity (Logos).
- In *BTA*, Angel Archer represents the Logos (Hagia Sophia) within Dick. Paradoxically, she denies the validity of the very event (Valis) that created her.

## Entities
- **Jakob Böhme**: The mystic who first perceived the dialectic within God (between Will and Reason) that Dick experienced in 1974.
- **Hegel**: His concept of reconciliation through higher synthesis perfectly describes Dick's 2-74 meta-abstraction.

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Two Gods**: A dramatic device in *VALIS* used to depict the internal dynamic process of God's mind, converting the prerational will into rational order (Logos).

## Theological Motifs
- **Process Theology**: God is not static but dynamic, characterized by a continuous dialectical process where reason constantly overcomes irrational will to achieve higher abstractions.
