## Key Claims
- In Dick's Hebrew-Kabbalist-Spinozist system, the representation (information) is exactly as real as the object it represents; there is no ontological degradation or "copy." The information *is* the object.
- This framework solves Descartes' dualism and Parmenides' problem of empiricism: we can trust our senses because the "information" we receive maps with absolute point-for-point parallelism to the internal mind of God.
- In 2-3-74, Dick apprehended the underlying "Infinity" attribute of reality, which transformed ordinary objects into a universal linguistic code.
- By perceiving this attribute of Infinity, all of reality functionally becomes a sacrament, possessing self-authenticating meaning that requires no external interpretation.

## Entities
- **Descartes**: Highlighted as the philosopher who first recognized the paralyzing problem of epistemology: how can we know our internal "representations" match the external world?

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Meta-Abstraction**: Restated here as the realization that a single "Idea" (Object) eternally instantiates in infinite places and times ("Not 2 mothers but 1 mother twice").

## Theological Motifs
- **Sacramental Reality**: The condition where ordinary physical reality, once apprehended along the axis of infinity, is revealed as a divine sign or liturgical act communicating directly with the observer.
