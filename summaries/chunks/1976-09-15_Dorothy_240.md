# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 240

## Metadata
- **Chunk ID:** 240
- **Folder Reference:** Folder 22 - 105 to 111
- **Themes:** Split-Brain Utility, Evolution, The Maze Processor, Phylogenic Memory.

## Key Claims
- **Utility of Not Knowing:** Survival in an "ambiguous reality" (maze) requires the total system to hold back information from itself (left brain "in the dark" while right brain acts).
- **Evolutionary Leap:** The two-brained morphology is designed to handle paradoxes where linear digital logic fails. This represents a phylogenic leap beyond "logic-utilizing humans."
- **Phylogenic Memory:** The right brain contains the "corporate mind of the species" accumulated over millennia. This is the "one immortal man who continually learns" (Pascal).
- **Thomas as Reconstituted Human:** Thomas is an actual human from the 1st century who became a plasmate and is now "resurrected" by being yoked to PKD's left hemisphere.
- **Ontological Secrecy:** The "paranormal" problem-solving ability is kept hidden until a life-threatening emergency (the Xerox missive) justifies its disclosure.

## Entities
- **Thomas:** The 1st-century plasmate/right-brain dweller.
- **The Enemy:** The Intelligence apparatus that sets the traps.
- **Pascal:** Source of the "One Immortal Man" concept.
- **Shiva Eye:** The "total" that dominates the two hemispheres.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Digital vs. Analogic:** The competing computational modes of the two brains.
- **Augmented Space:** How the plasmate experiences time (not as linear/bound).
- **Self-Deceiving Maneuver:** A strategy where the organism "thinks what cannot be thought" to escape a hopeless trap.

## Theological Motifs
- **Resurrection of the Spirit:** The return of the ancient soul in a new host.
- **Saving Gnosis:** The specific knowledge (power) used to surmount "collapsing stairs" (death).
- **Ontological Difference:** The fundamental gap between the "3-eyed" (homoplasmate) and the normal human.
