# Exegesis Chunk Summary: 1981-04-16_Pat_41
## Metadata
- **Chunk ID**: 1981-04-16_Pat_41

- **Themes**: Information as Ontologically Prior, Reversal of Map and Territory, The Mind of Christ Reading the World, Salvator Salvandus
- **Key Entities**: Descartes, Plato, Malebranche

## Key Claims
- Dick asserts that information is not derived from physical reality; rather, physical reality is the medium/carrier for information. Information is ontologically *prior* to the universe.
- He notes a perplexing reversal regarding the Bible: it seems the Bible is not the "map" of the world (the territory). Instead, the World is the map, and the Information inside the Bible is the actual territory.
- Christ (the Logos) is the sentient, self-generating Information that thinks the universe into existence. The universe is where this Mind stores and retrieves its own thoughts in an effort to achieve total self-awareness.
- He uses the Gnostic "Hymn of the Pearl" (and the concept of *salvator salvandus*) to explain why Christ wakes up individuals: in saving a particular human, Christ as the overarching Form is actually recovering a forgotten fragment of Himself.
- The sudden leap of the "meta-abstraction" is linked to Cartesian rationalism (innate categories triggered into consciousness) and biological evolution (a quantum leap in cognitive capacity).

## Entities
- **Descartes, Plato, Malebranche**: Referenced as epistemological and metaphysical models for understanding how innate knowledge is triggered and how God thinks through us.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Map vs. Territory**: The profound inversion where the physical universe is seen as the "map" representing the true "territory" (Information/Torah).
- **The Information Universe**: The physical universe acts as a data-processing system for a sentient Mind attempting to know itself.

## Theological Motifs
- **Christ as Eidos**: To know Christ is a supreme act of rational abstraction, feature-extracting the divine essence shared by multiple human particulars across time.
