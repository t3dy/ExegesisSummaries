# Claudia
## concise_summary
Dick identifies the "Miracle of Transubstantiation" as the mechanism by which God sanctifies and replaces ordinary reality ("trash of the alley"). He concludes that his experiences of the "Logos" were specifically the "Real Presence" of Christ, facilitated by the "Spirit of Elijah" (Holy Spirit) which refined him.
## key_claims
- The "Miracle of Transubstantiation" occurred in his environment: God consecrated or sanctified objects like the "trash of the alley" to be the "Body and Blood."
- Dick distinguishes between the *occurrence* of the miracle (ubiquitous) and his *perception* of it (the 3rd eye/mystical state).
- The 1974 experience involved "two different presents" superimposed (Double exposure), with the Roman one being more ontologically "real."
- Christ is the "Beebread": individuals are fed in their "cells" but must emerge into a "cooperative."
- The "Spirit of Elijah" acted as a "fierce refining spirit" that prepared him for the Logos/Christ.
- The "Parousia" (Second Advent) may have arrived quietly in 3-74 without the world noticing.
