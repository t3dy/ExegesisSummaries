## Key Claims
- Once justified, a person conceptually obliterates "Fate" (Heimarmene) and entirely replaces it with *Providentia* (the saving power of God). Man's transactions are no longer with the world, but directly with God.
- This direct, absolute reliance on God is a return to the essence of Judaism (the God of Abraham), bypassing the legalism of the Torah and restoring man to the immediate relationship with God that existed before the Fall (a return to Eden).
- The existential crisis and the call for aid (justification) can only occur *once* in a person's lifetime. It requires a lifetime of exhausting one's own capabilities until the soul is literally dying, forcing the ultimate cry for help.
- The 2-3-74 event was unequivocally a Christian experience of Justification. Dick emphasizes that this was Charis (grace) operating outside of church sacraments; it was a direct transaction between man and God.

## Entities
- **Heidegger**: Used by Dick to frame his Gnostic/Christian experiences in modern ontological terms (Dasein, Geworfenheit).

## Recurring Concepts
- **The Birth in the Spirit**: God completing His original act of creation by guaranteeing the soul's existence forever; synonomous with Justification.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Gospel of the Holy Spirit**: *Flow My Tears* is identified as essentially a new text of Luke/Acts, dictated by the Holy Spirit to express Protestant/Pauline existentialism.
