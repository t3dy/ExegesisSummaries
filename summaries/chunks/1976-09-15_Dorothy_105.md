# The Agony of the Urgrund
## concise_summary
Dick explores the theological concept of the "Urgrund" (the primordial ground of being) and its struggle within the material world. He suggests that the divine undergoes a form of "agony" or fragmentation when it encounters matter, and the goal of history is the Urgrund's self-healing.
## key_claims
- Urgrund in Matter: The divine essence is inextricably bound with, and suffering within, the material universe.
- Divine Agony: The process of existence is a painful "unfolding" or "transmutation" for the Urgrund.
- Healing through Transmutation: History is the process by which the divine recovers its unity and consciousness.
