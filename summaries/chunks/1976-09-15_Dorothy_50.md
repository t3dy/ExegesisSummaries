# St. Sophia and the Pluriform Trinity
## concise_summary
Dick identifies the "Voice" he heard (St. Sophia) as Holy Wisdom/Christ/Holy Spirit, unifying them into a "pluriform" Trinity. He argues that he was "adopted" by the deity to play an historic role, using his "what is real?" theme as a salvific tool to break the "curse" of the world's delusion.
## key_claims
- St. Sophia as Christ/Wisdom: The prophecy ("St. Sophia will be born again") confirms that she is the "Holy Wisdom" (Logos) who was "not acceptable before" (rejected as Christ).
- Pluriform Trinity: The 1 essence / 3 persons (Father, Wisdom/Christ, Holy Spirit) are multifaceted ("pluriform") and flow into each other.
- Adoption: Dick was "adopted" by the deity in 3-74 to serve as a prophet/messenger; his authority came from the "Parakletos" (Advocate/Holy Spirit).
- Breaking the Curse: By an act of "Pistis" (Faith) triggered by the "Gold Fish Sign," Dick broke the power of the "Dokos" (counterfeit world) and accessed Grace.
- Hypostatization: Our brains fail to see the Trinity because it is not "isomorphic" to human structures; we "perceptually reject" it unless Grace aids us.
- The 3 Stigmata mirror: His novel *3 Stigmata* is a "diabolic mirror opposite" of the divine transsubstantiation process.
- Salvific Writing: His 25 years of writing about "delusional reality" prepared him (and potentially others) to recognize the theophany.
- Zion/Babylon: He identifies 1974 with the "apocalyptic scene" of the siege of Babylon (Rome); he, Daniel, and John of Patmos all saw the same timeless event.
