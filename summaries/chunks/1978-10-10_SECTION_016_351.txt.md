- **Chunk ID**: 1978-10-10_SECTION_016_351

- **Themes**: Static Frames, Pretextual Cause, Illusion of Time and Space, Field Theory
- **Key Entities**: Parmenides, Malebranche, Leibnitz, Spinoza

## Key Claims
- Humans suffer from twin hallucinations: we perceive a continuous field of space as discontinuous (discrete objects), and we perceive a discontinuous series of events in time as a continuous flow (causation).
- Parmenides was fundamentally correct: motion is impossible. Reality is a progression of unrelated, discontinuous static frames (like a movie reel).
- "Pretextual cause" experienced in 3-74 was the withdrawal of Dick's mind projecting sequential causal links. He saw the static "frames" for what they were.
- The reality field acts as a "carrier" that sits in space; the perturbations or changes between frames are the "modulation" or information passing through the field.
- Reality's changes are self-generated instructions arising spontaneously ex nihilo within the field, revealing the field itself is sentient.

## Entities
- **Parmenides**: Ancient Greek philosopher whom Dick views as correctly realizing the impossibility of motion/change.
- **Malebranche / Leibnitz / Spinoza**: The Cartesians whose models of a unified field and occasionalism anticipate modern quantum mechanics.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Static Frames**: Snapshots of reality that replace one another without directly acting upon one another.
- **Pretextual Cause**: The realization that 'before' does not physically act on 'after'; events are numerically sequenced point-events, not causally linked forces.
- **The Binary System / Flip-Flop**: Reality's processes function on off-on (rest-motion) switching.

## Theological Motifs
- **Creation Ex Nihilo**: Micro-cosmogenesis occurs spontaneously as the universe seeds itself with information on an "as needed" basis.
- **Anokhi / "I am"**: The pure consciousness or total system that differentiates into VALIS (activity) over the void (space).
