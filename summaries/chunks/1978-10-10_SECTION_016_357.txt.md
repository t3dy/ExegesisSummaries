## Key Claims
- Dick categorizes reality into three hierarchical levels: Gestalt (*eidos*), "time atoms" (particulars), and discrete informational frames (subparticulars).
- The brain constructs a "particular" as an abstraction of possibility from the underlying subparticular frames. Therefore, perception and conception are inseparable.
- "Deconstruction" of reality into binary bits yields only a tautology ("information is information"). To derive meaning, the brain must synthesize or "meta-abstract" the bits into constants (*eide*).
- Platonist elements (epistemological insights into how the brain structures information into an illusory reality) are strictly the *means*. The Christian apocalyptic message (God is here, the Parousia is actively occurring) is the *end*.
- A "Platonist Christianity" (such as held by Augustine and Malebranche) correctly uses these philosophical mechanics to convey the Christian *kerygma*.

## Entities
- **Plato**: Advanced the necessity of abstracting constants (Forms) out of flux to attain knowledge.
- **Augustine / Malebranche**: Historical examples of theologians who successfully merged Platonist frameworks with Christian truth.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Subparticulars / Time Atoms**: The lowest level of reality's binary sequence before they are organized into discrete particulars.
- **Meta-Abstraction (Synthesis)**: The process of synthesizing the underlying informational frames into constants/Forms, yielding a dramatic narrative (the Christian apocalypse) rather than a mere data stream.

## Theological Motifs
- **Christian Apocalyptic History**: The grand narrative resulting from total synthesis, revealing a timeless cosmic battle in which the observer is an active participant and revolutionary.
- **The Parousia**: The ultimate "message" the brain discovers once it synthesizes reality correctly.
