# Chunk 217 Summary - 1976-09-15_Dorothy_217

## Description
PKD experiences a vision of the "Smuggled Savior" and the return of the "Head Apollo." He analyzes a laconic voice message—**"Philip the first"**—to conclude that he is the initial modern "viaduct" for a divine takeover of reality.

## Key Claims
- **Philip the First**: PKD interprets this phrase to mean he is the first "living reality" (human host) in this time period successfully taken over by the Brotherhood/Godhead as a viaduct.
- **The Smuggled Savior**: The second Advent is not a glorious public event but a "smuggling" of a divine child (Siddhartha/Apollo) into the world, unnoticed by "Scotland Yard" (the authorities).
- **The Raft Dream**: A dream of a little boy on a raft floating toward England (Albion) signifies the "invasion" of our world by a divine entity from an alternate universe or the future.
- **Siddhartha Awakens**: The transition from Part I (sleep/ignorance) to Part II (wakefulness/gnosis) of the "Great Book" of human history is occurring now.

## Entities
- **The Head Apollo**: Identified as the "rightful King" and the "Head of the Mystical Body" (Christ) who is about to return.
- **Philip the First**: PKD’s status as the initial "open outlet" for the "We" (the Brotherhood).
- **Scotland Yard**: A metaphor for the imperial authorities who try to intercept the "smuggled" divine influence.
- **Albion (England)**: The symbolic destination where the "divine child" or "rightful king" (Arthur/Apollo) reclaims the throne.

## Recurring Concepts
- **Cuckoo's Egg**: The tactic of the divine to place its agents into human host "nests" disguised as ordinary people.
- **Living Info Outlets**: The contrast between "dead" media (RF signals) and "living" reality (human host agents).
- **Transtemporal Refusion**: The process where parts of the "immortal personality" partitioned off by time are fused back together upon the abolition of linear time.
- **Testing the Dream**: PKD’s attempt to "verify" his dreams by checking for responsive detail; finding they are "received" rather than "generated" proves an external source.

## Theological Motifs
- **The Great Assize**: The arrival of the "Judge" (Pantocrator) and the weighing of all lives as "Time ends, literally."
- **King Arthur’s Return**: The secular/mythological version of the Second Coming—the return across the sea on the "silent, flat barge."
- **Azoth**: The alchemical "universal solvent" or sword used by the returning king.
- **Tremens Factus Sum**: The mixture of holy fear and faith as the "majesty of the divine" closes in.
