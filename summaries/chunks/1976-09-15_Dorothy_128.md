# The Three Stages of Revelation: From Dread to Bliss
## concise_summary
Dick outlines a three-stage progression of the human experience with the Divine: 1) Suffering/Dread (The world is real and hostile), 2) Amazement/Pantheism (The world is alive and animate), and 3) Bliss/Acosmic Panentheism (The world is irreal, and man is isomorphic with God). He clarifies that 3-74 was a "miracle" where God "atomized" the "slice of ham" (the world) separating them, allowing for a total fusion. He also refines his cosmogony, suggesting our world is an irreal delusion projected by a lesser entity but being "overruled" or invaded by a Good God (Ubik).
## key_claims
- Three Stages of Divine Encounter:
    1.  **Dread/Pain**: Man as the "crown of creation" viewing a unliving, hostile world.
    2.  **Amazement/Incomprehension**: The world is seen as animate/sentient (panpsychism), but man feels dwarfed.
    3.  **Bliss/Fusion**: The world "becomes" irreal (Dokos), and man realizes he is isomorphic with the vast sentient Mind.
- The Function of Miracle: A miracle's true purpose is to reveal God's nature, not just to fix a problem. In 3-74, God "reacquired" PKD.
- Overruling the Lesser Good: God doesn't just overrule "bad," He overrules "lesser" or "imperfect" good (Pauline cosmology of Christ over angels).
- Acosmic Panentheism: The world is real only in relation to man; in relation to God, it is an absolute void ( Dokos).
- Atomizing the Ham: God obliterated the barrier (the world) that separated the two isomorphic entities (Man and God).
